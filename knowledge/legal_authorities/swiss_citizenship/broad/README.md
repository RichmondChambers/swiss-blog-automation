# Broad Swiss Citizenship Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss citizenship and naturalisation blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring Swiss citizenship and naturalisation issues across more than one route, applicant category or procedural context.

## Current Broad Packs

legal_authority_facilitated_swiss_naturalisation_by_marriage.md

This pack should cover facilitated Swiss naturalisation by marriage, including the legal basis, marital relationship requirements, residence and cohabitation considerations, integration, public-order issues, procedure, evidence and drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss citizenship or naturalisation routes.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation, residence counting, C permit requirements, integration, language evidence, cantonal and communal procedure, refusals, postponements, appeals and drafting cautions.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core citizenship framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad Swiss citizenship packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Swiss Citizenship Act (SCA / BüG / LN);
the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance (SCO / BüV / OLN);
SEM guidance and official federal naturalisation materials;
federal naturalisation requirements;
cantonal and communal naturalisation requirements;
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;
ordinary naturalisation;
facilitated naturalisation;
federal, cantonal and communal competence;
residence requirements and residence counting;
integration and language requirements;
public-order, compliance and good-character considerations;
procedure, discretion and evidential assessment.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
key case law, where relevant;
cantonal and communal practice notes, where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
communal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as an automatic right to Swiss citizenship. Do not describe discretionary or evaluative naturalisation decisions as entitlements. Do not describe cantonal or communal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad Swiss citizenship authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

type of naturalisation route;
ordinary or facilitated naturalisation basis;
nationality and residence history;
length and continuity of residence;
residence counting rules;
C permit status, where required;
marriage, family relationship or descent from a Swiss citizen, where relevant;
integration record;
language evidence;
participation in economic life or education;
tax, debt enforcement or social assistance issues;
criminality, public-order or security concerns;
federal competence;
cantonal competence;
communal competence;
local procedure and evidential expectations;
refusal, postponement, appeal and reapplication strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss citizenship and naturalisation system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several citizenship routes, such as ordinary eligibility, facilitated naturalisation, C permit requirements, residence counting, integration, language evidence, procedure, refusals, postponements or appeals.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific citizenship or naturalisation route, such as ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, facilitated naturalisation for children of Swiss citizens, facilitated naturalisation for third-generation foreign nationals or re-naturalisation.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as interrupted residence, insufficient language evidence, integration concerns, public-order problems, social assistance, debt enforcement, cantonal objections, communal objections or procedural fairness issues.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad Swiss citizenship packs should not flatten route-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the naturalisation route, nationality, residence history, C permit status, marriage or family relationship, residence counting rules, absences, integration record, language evidence, public-order issues, debt enforcement, social assistance history, cantonal procedure and communal practice.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating eligibility as an entitlement to citizenship or implying that naturalisation follows automatically after a fixed number of years.
