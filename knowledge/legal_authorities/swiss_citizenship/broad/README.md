# Broad Swiss Citizenship by Naturalisation Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss citizenship by naturalisation blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring Swiss citizenship and naturalisation issues across more than one route, applicant category, canton, commune or factual scenario.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_ordinary_swiss_naturalisation_eligibility.md`

This pack should cover ordinary Swiss naturalisation eligibility, including residence duration, C permit requirements, integration, language, public order, financial compliance, cantonal and communal requirements, and recurring drafting cautions.

### `legal_authority_facilitated_swiss_naturalisation_by_marriage.md`

This pack should cover facilitated Swiss naturalisation by marriage, including marriage to a Swiss citizen, marital union requirements, residence or foreign-residence scenarios, integration, close ties to Switzerland, procedure and evidential risks.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_c_permit_requirement.md`

This pack should cover the C permit requirement for ordinary naturalisation, including settlement status, timing, exceptions if any, relationship with residence duration, permit stability and risks where the applicant has not yet obtained or maintained a C permit.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_residence_counting_and_continuity.md`

This pack should cover residence counting, lawful residence, qualifying years, continuity, residence interruptions, treatment of time spent under different permit types, cantonal residence requirements and drafting cautions around timing.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_after_absence_or_interrupted_residence.md`

This pack should cover naturalisation after absence from Switzerland or interrupted residence, including breaks in residence, loss of continuity, return to Switzerland, residence re-counting, evidential issues and timing strategy.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_integration_requirements.md`

This pack should cover integration requirements, including respect for public safety and order, constitutional values, participation in economic life or education, encouragement of family integration, knowledge of Switzerland and local integration expectations.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_language_requirements_and_evidence.md`

This pack should cover language requirements and evidence, including oral and written language levels, recognised certificates, exemptions or accommodations where relevant, cantonal or communal evidential practice and risks around weak or missing language proof.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_cantonal_communal_procedure.md`

This pack should cover cantonal and communal naturalisation procedure, including filing, authority roles, interviews, tests, local knowledge assessments, publication or consultation steps where relevant, timing, communications and practice-sensitive risks.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_refusal_postponement_and_appeal.md`

This pack should cover refusal, postponement and appeal issues, including refusal grounds, procedural fairness, reasons, reconsideration, appeal routes, reapplication strategy, new evidence and timing after an adverse decision.

### `legal_authority_swiss_naturalisation_drafting_cautions_and_proposition_bank.md`

This pack should cover recurring drafting cautions and proposition control for Swiss naturalisation content, including safe formulations, overstatement risks, route distinctions, canton-sensitive language and propositions that should be avoided or qualified.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss citizenship by naturalisation scenarios.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, C permit timing, residence counting, interrupted residence, integration, language evidence, cantonal and communal procedure, refusal, postponement, appeal and reapplication topics.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad Swiss citizenship by naturalisation packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Swiss Citizenship Act (SCA / BüG / LN); the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance (SCO / BüV / OLN); cantonal citizenship legislation; communal naturalisation rules and procedure; SEM guidance and official procedure materials; Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law; ordinary naturalisation rules; facilitated naturalisation rules; C permit requirements; residence duration and residence counting; residence continuity; absence and interrupted residence; integration requirements; language requirements; public order and security conditions; participation in economic life or education; social assistance and financial compliance issues; cantonal and communal discretion; refusal, postponement and appeal procedure.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal and communal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; communal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic acquisition of Swiss citizenship. Do not describe facilitated naturalisation as ordinary naturalisation. Do not describe cantonal or communal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad Swiss citizenship by naturalisation authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

naturalisation route; ordinary or facilitated naturalisation; applicant nationality; current permit status; C permit requirement; residence duration; residence counting; continuity of residence; residence interruptions; absences from Switzerland; marital status; Swiss spouse status; marital union; close ties to Switzerland; integration; language evidence; participation in economic life or education; social assistance history; tax compliance; debt enforcement; criminal-record issues; respect for public order and constitutional values; family composition; canton and commune of residence; local knowledge; interviews; tests; procedural timing; refusal grounds; postponement; appeal options; reapplication strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss citizenship by naturalisation system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several naturalisation scenarios, such as ordinary eligibility, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, C permit requirements, residence counting and continuity, absence or interrupted residence, integration, language evidence, cantonal and communal procedure, refusals, postponements, appeals and drafting cautions.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific naturalisation route or applicant category, such as ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, naturalisation of children, spouses of Swiss citizens, former Swiss citizens or applicants with special residence histories.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as weak integration, insufficient language evidence, social assistance history, criminal convictions, tax arrears, debt enforcement, long absences, interrupted residence, commune-level objections, failed interviews or post-refusal reapplication strategy.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

Swiss citizenship; Swiss naturalisation; ordinary naturalisation; facilitated naturalisation; facilitated naturalisation by marriage; citizenship by naturalisation; C permit; settlement permit; residence counting; residence continuity; interrupted residence; absence from Switzerland; integration requirements; language requirements; language evidence; participation in economic life; social assistance risk; public order; criminal record; cantonal naturalisation procedure; communal naturalisation procedure; naturalisation interview; naturalisation test; refusal; postponement; appeal; reapplication; SCA / BüG / LN for the Swiss Citizenship Act; SCO / BüV / OLN for the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance; SEM for the State Secretariat for Migration.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss citizenship practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad Swiss citizenship packs should not flatten route-specific, canton-specific or commune-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the naturalisation route, applicant nationality, current permit status, C permit status, residence duration, residence continuity, absences, marital status, Swiss spouse status, integration, language evidence, financial and criminal compliance, family composition, canton, commune, interviews, tests, local knowledge requirements, refusal history and timing.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating naturalisation as automatic once residence duration is met, confusing ordinary and facilitated naturalisation, or suggesting that all cantons and communes apply the same procedure and evidential test.
