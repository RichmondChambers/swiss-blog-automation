# Core Swiss Citizenship by Naturalisation Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss citizenship by naturalisation blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss citizenship by naturalisation framework before moving into broad, route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

### `legal_authority_core_swiss_citizenship_by_naturalisation_framework.md`

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss citizenship by naturalisation, including ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation where relevant, C permit requirements, residence counting and continuity, integration, language, cantonal and communal procedure, refusal, postponement, appeal and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss citizenship by naturalisation framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple Swiss citizenship scenarios, including:

ordinary Swiss naturalisation; facilitated naturalisation by marriage; naturalisation for C permit holders; residence counting and continuity; naturalisation after absence or interrupted residence; integration requirements; language requirements and evidence; cantonal and communal procedure; refusals, postponements and appeals; drafting cautions and proposition control for naturalisation content.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core Swiss citizenship by naturalisation pack should explain the relationship between:

the Swiss Citizenship Act (SCA / BüG / LN); the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance (SCO / BüV / OLN); cantonal citizenship legislation; communal naturalisation rules and procedure; SEM guidance and official procedure materials; Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law; ordinary naturalisation requirements; facilitated naturalisation routes; C permit requirements; residence duration and residence counting; integration and language requirements; public order and security conditions; financial participation and social assistance issues; cantonal and communal discretion; refusal, postponement, reconsideration and appeal procedure.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of Swiss citizenship by naturalisation routes; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal and communal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; communal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic acquisition of Swiss citizenship. Do not describe facilitated naturalisation as ordinary naturalisation. Do not describe cantonal or communal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core Swiss citizenship by naturalisation authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

naturalisation route; ordinary or facilitated naturalisation; applicant nationality; current permit status; C permit requirement; residence duration; residence counting; continuity of residence; residence interruptions; absences from Switzerland; marital status; Swiss spouse status where relevant; integration; language ability; participation in economic life or education; social assistance history; tax compliance; debt enforcement; criminal-record issues; respect for public order and constitutional values; family composition; canton and commune of residence; cantonal and communal evidential expectations; interviews, tests or hearings; refusal grounds; postponement; appeal rights; timing and reapplication strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several Swiss citizenship by naturalisation scenarios, such as ordinary eligibility, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, C permit requirements, residence counting and continuity, absence or interrupted residence, integration, language evidence, cantonal and communal procedure, refusals, postponements, appeals and drafting cautions.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific naturalisation route or applicant category, such as ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, naturalisation of children, naturalisation of spouses of Swiss citizens, former Swiss citizens or applicants with special residence histories.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

Swiss citizenship; Swiss naturalisation; ordinary naturalisation; facilitated naturalisation; facilitated naturalisation by marriage; citizenship by naturalisation; C permit; settlement permit; residence counting; residence continuity; interrupted residence; absence from Switzerland; integration requirements; language requirements; language evidence; participation in economic life; social assistance risk; public order; criminal record; cantonal naturalisation procedure; communal naturalisation procedure; naturalisation interview; naturalisation test; refusal; postponement; appeal; reapplication; SCA / BüG / LN for the Swiss Citizenship Act; SCO / BüV / OLN for the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance; SEM for the State Secretariat for Migration.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss citizenship practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Swiss citizenship by naturalisation should not be treated as automatic once residence duration is met.

The analysis usually depends on the naturalisation route, current permit status, residence history, residence continuity, absences, C permit status, integration, language ability, financial and criminal compliance, family circumstances, canton, commune, evidence, procedure and timing.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating eligibility as automatic acquisition, flattening ordinary and facilitated naturalisation into one route, or implying that cantonal and communal practice is uniform across Switzerland.
