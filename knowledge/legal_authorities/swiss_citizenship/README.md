# Swiss Citizenship Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss citizenship by naturalisation blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss citizenship by naturalisation, including ordinary naturalisation, facilitated naturalisation by marriage, C permit requirements, residence counting, residence continuity, interrupted residence, integration, language evidence, cantonal and communal procedure, refusals, postponements, appeals and reapplication strategy.

## Folder Structure

```text
swiss_citizenship/
  broad/
  core/
  issue_specific/
  route_specific/
  README.md
```

## Folder Purposes

### `core/`

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss citizenship by naturalisation framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Swiss Citizenship Act (SCA / BüG / LN);  
the Swiss Citizenship Ordinance (SCO / BüV / OLN);  
cantonal citizenship legislation;  
communal naturalisation rules and procedure;  
SEM guidance and official procedure materials;  
ordinary naturalisation;  
facilitated naturalisation;  
C permit requirements;  
residence counting and continuity;  
integration and language requirements;  
refusal, postponement and appeal procedure.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general Swiss citizenship and naturalisation topics across multiple applicant profiles, routes, cantons and communes.

### `broad/`

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss citizenship by naturalisation scenarios.

Examples include:

ordinary Swiss naturalisation eligibility;  
facilitated Swiss naturalisation by marriage;  
C permit requirement;  
residence counting and continuity;  
naturalisation after absence or interrupted residence;  
integration requirements;  
language requirements and evidence;  
cantonal and communal procedure;  
refusal, postponement and appeal;  
drafting cautions and proposition bank.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one Swiss citizenship or naturalisation scenario.

### `route_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on a specific Swiss citizenship or naturalisation route or applicant category.

Examples include:

ordinary naturalisation;  
facilitated naturalisation by marriage;  
facilitated naturalisation for children of Swiss citizens;  
naturalisation of children and young people;  
spouses of Swiss citizens;  
former Swiss citizens;  
applicants born in Switzerland;  
applicants with foreign residence and close ties to Switzerland;  
applicants relying on special statutory routes.

Route-specific packs should identify the legal basis, applicant category, entitlement or discretion, eligibility conditions, procedure, evidence, risks and drafting cautions for the specific route.

### `issue_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories within Swiss citizenship and naturalisation practice.

Examples include:

weak integration evidence;  
insufficient language evidence;  
social assistance history;  
debt enforcement or tax arrears;  
criminal convictions;  
public-order concerns;  
long absences from Switzerland;  
interrupted residence;  
uncertain residence counting;  
C permit timing problems;  
commune-level objections;  
failed interviews or tests;  
postponement after changed circumstances;  
refusal and reapplication strategy.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the core, broad or route-specific packs provide.

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation and ordinances;  
cantonal citizenship legislation;  
communal naturalisation rules and official materials;  
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal guidance;  
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;  
official cantonal naturalisation authority guidance;  
official communal naturalisation guidance, where available;  
cantonal and communal administrative practice, where evidenced;  
internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;  
existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;  
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;  
a table of legal propositions;  
primary legal texts;  
official guidance and administrative materials;  
key case law, where relevant;  
cantonal and communal practice notes, where relevant;  
practical legal analysis;  
drafting cautions;  
source list;  
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;  
eligibility only;  
discretion;  
facilitation;  
guidance;  
procedure;  
evidential practice;  
cantonal practice;  
communal practice;  
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic acquisition of Swiss citizenship. Do not describe facilitated naturalisation as ordinary naturalisation. Do not describe cantonal or communal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Swiss citizenship by naturalisation should not be treated as automatic, uniform or based only on residence duration.

The analysis usually depends on:

naturalisation route;  
ordinary or facilitated naturalisation;  
applicant nationality;  
current permit status;  
C permit status;  
residence duration;  
residence counting;  
continuity of residence;  
residence interruptions;  
absences from Switzerland;  
marital status;  
Swiss spouse status;  
marital union;  
close ties to Switzerland;  
integration;  
language ability;  
language evidence;  
participation in economic life or education;  
social assistance history;  
tax compliance;  
debt enforcement;  
criminal-record issues;  
respect for public order and constitutional values;  
family composition;  
canton of residence;  
commune of residence;  
local knowledge;  
interviews or tests;  
refusal history;  
postponement;  
appeal options;  
reapplication strategy.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, treat naturalisation as automatic, confuse ordinary and facilitated naturalisation, or flatten canton-specific and commune-specific requirements into uniform Swiss law.
