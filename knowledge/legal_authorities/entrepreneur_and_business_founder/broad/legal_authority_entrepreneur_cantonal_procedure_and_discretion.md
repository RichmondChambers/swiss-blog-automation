# Swiss Founder And Entrepreneur WorkAuthorisation Authority Pack

## Executive legal summary

Swiss founder and entrepreneur work-authorisation cases are unusually canton-sensitive because the decisive federal rules are framed at a relatively high level of generality, while the first-instance procedural pathway, the composition of the cantonal decision-makers, the evidence bundle, and many practical sequencing questions are handled locally. For third-country nationals, the core federal route is admission to self-employment under Article 19 AIG, read together with the general requirements in Articles 20 to 25 AIG and the cantonal labour-market preliminary-decision machinery in Article 40 AIG and Article 83 VZAE. Current official federal guidance also states that self-employed third-country cases with residence in Switzerland are permit-required from day one and are subject to both cantonal and federal procedure. 1

The federal criteria are demanding but not mechanically reducible to a single checklist.The file must show, at minimum, that the project serves Switzerland's overall economic interest, that the necessary financial and operational conditions are met, that there is an autonomous and sufficient basis of subsistence, and that the personal-qualification rules for third-country nationals are satisfied. The SEM's current directives make clear that the "overall economic interest" test in founder cases turns on sustainable labour-market benefit, such as regional or sectoral diversification, job creation or retention, significant investment, and new mandates for the Swiss economy; for start-ups, innovation capacity and the commercialisation of academic research may also be relevant. 2

The canton-sensitive element arises because those federal criteria are operationalised differently across cantons. Geneva expressly routes the file through OCPM and OCIRT, states that OCIRT gives the labour-market preliminary decision and may seek the opinion of the tripartite economic commission, and recommends prior contact with the cantonal economic and innovation office to assess the fit of the project with the Geneva economic context. Zurich places third-country labour-market admissions within the Office for Economy and publishes its own explanatory note requiring a specific application justification, business plan, accounts or projected balance sheet, and often rental or credit documents. Solothurn publishes an especially concrete list including market and cost analysis, Swiss cooperation contracts, and evidence of acquired customers, and even publishes a service duration of three to six weeks. Uri issues a detailed factsheet requiring, among other things, a three-year business plan, criminal-record extract and translations into German, and states that the applicant must generally await the procedure abroad. These are not differences in federal substantive law; they are differences in how cantons structure proof and process around the same federal legal test. 3

Economic-development involvement must therefore be described carefully. It exists in some cantons, but not always as a formal decisional step. Geneva is theclearest official example: prior contact with OCEI is recommended before a third-country independent-permit filing, specifically to assess the project's fit with the local economy. Zurich's business and economic development division and labour-permit division sit within the same cantonal Office for Economy, which shows institutional proximity, but Zurich's published permit materials do not make economic-development endorsement a formal legal requirement. Vaud and Zug both have official economic-promotion structures supporting business creation and settlement, but the migration and labour-market materials reviewed here do not state that such support replaces or controls the immigration decision. Any future public-facing content should therefore avoid implying that "economnic-development backing" itself grants eligibility. 4

Commercial-register sequencing is another area where over-generalisation is risky. SEM's current directives say that the deed of incorporation and/or trade-register entry should in principle be filed, but allow a post-decision three-month grace period for supported start-ups demonstrably within a cantonal or federal promotion programme. Vaud's current permit form expressly accommodates an "entreprise en formation", which shows that at least one canton structures the filing form on the basis that the enterprise may still be in formation when the permit file is lodged. Solothurn, by contrast, expressly asks for a trade-register extract. The correct drafting lesson is not that trade registration is or is not required nationally; it is that the permissible order of incorporation, business-plan finalisation and immigration filing must be checked against the relevant canton's own materials. 5

Finally, self-employment cases must be distinguished from freer-movement cases. The most discretionary and evidence-heavy founder route is the third-country route. Cantonal EU/EFTA pages in Bern, Solothurn and Basel-Stadt show a much lighter structure centred on registration and proof of a genuine, effective self-employed activity. For internal content, that mneans "Swiss entrepreneur permit" should never be treated as a single homogeneous route. If the case is a third-country founder case, the content system should map the canton before generating procedural guidance. 6

## Structural overview

## Federal framework

The federal framework is built around Article 19 AIG for self-employment, Article 23 AIG for personal qualifications, Article 40 AIG for cantonal permit authority and labour-market preliminary decisions, and Article 99 AIG for SEM approval procedure. Article 83 VZAE assigns the first labour-market assessment to the canton before first issuance of a work-related L or B permit, including self-employment under Articles 18 to 25 AIG. Official SECO guidance confirms that third-country self-employment with Swiss residence is permit-required from the first day and subject to a combined cantonal and federal procedure. 7

## Canton migration authority role

The migration authority is the residence-permit authority, but its exact procedural role varies by canton. In Geneva, OCPM receives the file and issues the permit only if the labour-market arm has first accepted it. In Bern, the Migration Service issues the residence permit after approval and indicates that renewals generally also require approval from the cantonal economy office and, depending on the case, SEM. In Vaud, the communal foreigner's officeacts as the reception and transmission desk for SPOP, and the permit form itself routes filings differently depending on whether the applicant is already resident in Vaud, resident in another canton, or abroad. 8

## Labour-market authority role

The labour-market authority is often the real gatekeeper in founder cases. Federal law requires a cantonal labour-market preliminary decision where there is no right to work. Geneva identifies OCIRT's foreign-labour directorate as the body competent to render the preliminary decision; Vaud's form contains a field for thhe prior decision of DISMAT; Bern requires filing with AWI; Zurich's Office for Economy handles third-country labour-permit applications; Zug's Office for Economy and Labour

handles third-country self-employment; and Uri combines labour and migration functions in asingle office. 9

## SEM and federal approval

SEM approval remains central in founder cases and must not be omitted in route-specific content. The current SEM directives state that cantonal preliminary decisions admitting third-country nationals to self-employment under Article 19 AIG are approval-subject irrespective of sector or qualification, by reference to Article 1 letter a item 1 ZV-EJPD. Geneva's official page similarly states that the cantonal decision is then transmitted to SEM for approval. Under Article 85 VZAE, cantonal labour-market and migration authorities may also refer cases to SEM where federal-law compliance needs to be reviewed. 10

## Economic-development involvement

Economic-development involvement is not uniform. Geneva expressly recommends prior contact with OCEI for third-country independent cases in order to evaluate the fit of the project with the Geneva economic context. Zurich's official organisational chart shows that Standortförderung and the labour-permit division sit within the same Office for Economy, and Zurich's settlement pages confirm that cantonal business promotion accompanies firms from location evaluation to launch. Zug's Office for Economy and Labour formally contains both labour-force permits and economic-promotion functions, and its business-contact point describes itself as a one-stop shop assisting with company formation and required authorisations. Vaud's SPEI officially supports implantation, creation and SME development, but the Vaud permit materials reviewed here do not state that SPEI gives a binding immigration assessment. 11

## Document expectations

SEM's founder guidance requires a business plan covering activities, financing, market opportunity, staffing development and recruitment, planned investments, turnover and revenue, and asks for organisational links, external investor commitments, and the deed of incorporation and/or trade-register entry in principle. Zurich requires a specific justification, business plan, accounts or projected balance sheet and often rental or credit documents. Solothurn asks for a business plan with market and cost analysis, trade-register extract, proof of financial security, Swiss cooperation contracts and evidence of acquired customers. Uri requires a three-year business plan, links to Swiss businesses,a criminal-record extract and German translations. Geneva asks for a full project dossier with three-year financial projections, proof of autonomous income, CV, diplomas, labour certificates and a motivation letter. Basel-Stadt's official self-employed page links to a distinct self-employed application and a business-plan checklist; the searchable snippets from those official documents show that Basel expects a detailed project sketch and, in its business-plan checklist, AHV compensation-office confirmation of the self-employed activity. 12

### Timing and sequencing

Published timing is sparse and must be qualified. Geneva states that the file should be lodged six to eight weeks before the intended start date, and the same lead-time appears on Geneva's frontier self-employment page. Solothurn publishes a three- to six-week service duration. Bern's third-country employment page asks for filings at least eight weeks before the intended start date, which is not a founder-specific statement but does show that some cantons publish advance-filing expectations. SEM's general FAQ on gainful employment says that the entry-residence-employment process can take six to eight weeks, but that is not founder-specific and should be treated as a general federal orientation

rather than a promise for self-employed start-up cases. Sequencing also varies: SEM allows in-principle later filing of incorporation evidence in supported start-up cases, while cantonal forms such as Vaud's "entreprise en formation" show that some cantons accommodate filing before full incorporation. 13

### Local variation

The practical route differs materially by canton. Geneva has the clearest published two-office model and the strongest formal statement about economic-office pre-contact. Zurich publishes canton-specific explanatory notes and runs the filing through its labour-permit platform. Solothurn and Uri publish particularly detailed documentary expectations. Vaud splits reception and transmission across communes, SPOP and the cantonal labour-market authority. Basel-Stadt distinguishes sharply between a self-employed permit application and a business-plan checklist. Zug publishes a lighter substantive page but embeds labour-force and economic-promotion functions in the same office. Ticino's official materials clearly identify a "third countries and labour market" service and a digital permit portal, but publicly available founder-specific guidance appears comparatively thin. 14

### Draftinng implications

Future content must separate federal criteria from cantonal handling. It should say which points are fixed by AIG/VZAE and SEM directives, and which points come from a particular canton's filing route, checklist or institutional setup. It should also avoid stating that economic-promotion input is a universal or determinative element, because the official record shows that Geneva describes it expressly, while other cantons more often describe it as business support rather than as a formal permit stage. 15

### Primary legal texts

#### AIG article 19

**Provision** **identified:** Article 19, Bundesgesetz über die Ausländerinnen und Ausländer und über die Integration (AIG; SR 142.20). The accessible official text states that foreign nationals may be admitted to self-employment where this corresponds to the overall economic interest, the necessary financial and businessconditions are met, and an adequate autonomous basis of subsistence exists. Current federal and cantonal guidance also applies the wider Articles 20 to 25 AIG framework to such cases, especially quotas and personal qualifications. This matters because Article 19 is the anchor provision for third-country founder admission. 28

<!-- 7 -->

#### AIG article 23

**Provision** **identified:** Article 23 AIG. The accessible official text states that work-related short-stay and residence permits may generally be granted only to executives, specialists and other qualified workers. Thnis matters because federal law does not create a blanket entrepreneur exemption: even founder cases are usually assessed through the third-country qualification filter unless a different legal basis applies. 18

#### AIG article 38

**Provision** **identified:** Article 38 AIG, especially paragraph 3 on switching into self-employment.SEM's current directives explain that a person already holding a B permit without a work entitlement may apply to the competent canton to switch from employed to self-employed activity, and that for unrestricted B-permit holders the "overall economic interest" standard may be assessed more flexibly than for newly arriving founders. This matters because founder pathways differ materially between new entry and in-country status change. 29

#### AIG article 40

**Provision** **identified:** Article 40 AIG. The accessible official text states that the cantons issue the relevant permits, but where there is no work entitlement a labour-market preliminary decision by the competent cantonal authority is required for admission to work, job changes, and switching into self-employment. This matters because founder cases often involve at least two cantonal actors: the residence-permit authority and the labour-market authority. 30

#### AIG article 99

**Provision** **identified:** Article 99 AIG. The accessible official text states that the Federal Council determines which permits and cantonal labour-market preliminary decisions must be submitted to SEM for approval, and that SEM may refuse approval or restrict the cantonal decision. This matters because positive cantonal handling does not end the matter in approval-subject founder cases. 31

#### VZAE article 83

**Provision** **identified:** Article 83, Verordnung über Zulassung, Aufenthalt und Erwerbstätigkeit (VZAE; SR 142.201). The accessible official text states that before the first issuance of a short-stay or residence permit for gainful activity, the canton decides whether the requirements are met for self-employed or employed activity under Articles 18 to 25 AIG. This matters because it is the regulatory basis for the cantonal labour-market gatekeeping step. 20

#### VZAE articles 84 and 85

**Provision** **identified:** Articles 84 and 85 VZAE. Article 84 states that cantonal labour-market preliminary decisions are valid for six months and can be extended for important reasons. Article 85 states that SEM is competent for approvalin the approval-required cases and that cantonal labour-market and migration authorities may refer a cantonal decision to SEM to check compliance with federal law. This matters because founder files may need re-filing or approval routing if implementation is delayed, and because referral power exxists even in doubtful cases. 20

<!-- 8 -->

#### VZAE article 88 and ZV-EJPD article 1

**Provision** **identified:** Article 88 VZAE on cantonal enforcement authorities, and Article 1 of the Verordnung des EJPD über die dem Zustimmungsverfahren unterliegenden ausländerrechtlichen Bewilligungen und Vorentscheide (ZV-EJPD), as described in the current SEM directives. Article 88 leaves it to the cantons to designate the competent authorities. Thne SEM directives state that self-employment admissions under Article 19 AIG are approval-subject under Article 1 letter a item 1 ZV-EJPD, regardless of sector or qualification. This matters because institutional layouts differ by canton, but the federal approval overlay remains nationally important. 32

## Official federal guidance

The most important federal guidance instrument is the SEM's current Weisungen und Erläuterungen im Ausländerbereich, chapter on stay with gainful activity, current to 1 January 2026. For founder matters,it does five things of practical importance. First, it clarifies the immigration-law definition of self-employment and notes that titles such as president or CEO, or even trade-register entry alone, do not by themselves settle the self-employed versus employed distinction. Secondly, it gives substantive content to the "overall economic interest" test in founder/start-up files. Thirdly, it states that positive cantonal preliminary decisions under Article 19 AIG are approval-subject. Fourthly, it directs that first founder permits should usually be limnited to a maximum of two years in the start-up phase,with extension tied to actual delivery against the plan. Fifthly, it specifies the expected business-plan evidence and the limited possibility of filing incorporation or trade-register evidence later for supported start-ups. 33

The SEM directives also matter on status changes. Their current treatment of Article 38 paragraph 3 AIG says that a switch from employed to self-employed activity by a B-permit holder may be assessed more generously where the permit was not subject to restrictive conditions and the person is already integrated into Swiss society and the labour market. That is a route-specific nuance which should be preserved in internal content, because it is not the same as the new-entry entrepreneur route. 23

SEM's public FAQ on gainful employment gives general procedural orientation. It states that for third-country employment the application is lodged in the canton of work, and that if the canton approves, the authorisation is transmitted to the Swiss representation abroad; it also gives a general lead-time of around six to eight weeks. That guidance is not founder-specific and should not be treated as a published service standard for self-employed start-up files, but it is still useful as federal-level context on the canton-plus-federation sequencing model. 34

SECO's page on self-employed third-country nationals provides a compact, current federalI statement of scope. It says that self-employment by third-country nationals who take up residence in Switzerland is, in principle, permit-required from the first day and subject to the permit procedure of canton and Confederation. This is particularly useful for keeping internal content tightly focused on the third-country founder route and distinguishing it from EU/EFTA free-movement cases. 35

## Cantonal practice notes

### Geneva

**Authority** **and** **source.** Geneva's official materials are unusually explicit. The core sources are the OCPM page for independent permits, the third-country entrepreneur subpage, the general portal for foreign labour, and the Guichet Entreprises and OCEI pages. **Filing** **route** **or** **procedure.** The file is lodged with OCPM. For third-country founders, OCPM grants the permit only if OCIRT's foreign-labour directorate first accepts the application; depending on the type of case, OCIRT presents the request to the canton's tripartite economic commission. Geneva states that the canton's decision is then sent to SEM for approval. **Document** **expectations.** Geneva asks for a full project dossier/business plan, detailed three-year financial forecasts, proof of sufficient autonomous income, CV, diplomas, work certificates, a motivation letter, passport copy and photo. **Economic-development involvement.** Geneva expressly recommends prior contact with OCEI to assess the project's fit with the Geneva economic context; OCEI's own pages present it as the cantonal office for entrepreneurship, implantation and business development. **Timing** **information.** Geneva states that the application should be filed six to eight weeks before the intended start date and that the activity cannot begin before authorisation is granted. **Classification.** These points are mostly official procedure and guidance rather than primary legal rules; the OCIRT/OCPM/SEM sequence is, however, directly tied to the federal statutory structure. 36

#### Zurich

**Authority** **and** **source.** Zurich's official materials sit mainly on the Office for Economy pages and related migration pages. **Filing** **route** **or** **procedure.** Zurich publishes a canton-specific process note for self-employed persons and routes labour-market filings through its work-permit system; its migration page also shows that where the person lives in another canton, the Migration Office acts after receiving the labour-market preliminary decision. **Document** **expectations.** Zurich's entrepreneur guidance for third-country nationals requires a specific application justification, business plan, balance sheet or projected balance sheet, and may use rental or credit contracts as supporting evidence; the broader third-country note says applications are examined individually and the authority may request additional papers. Documents should be in German or English. **Economic-development** **involvement.** Zurich's organisational materials show that business-and-economic development and the labour-permit division sit in the same Office for Economy, and the canton's settlement pages say business promotion accompanies firms from evaluation to start. But the entrepreneur permit materials do not make business-promotion endorsement a mandatory immigration step. **Timing** **information.** No founder-specific service time was identified in the reviewed Zurich entrepreneur materials. **Classification.** Federal-law criteria appear on the Zurich pages, but the document package and institutional layout are canton procedure/guidance. 37

### Solothurn

**Authority** **and** **source.** Solothurn publishes both a topic page and a service page through the Migrationsamt. **Filing** **route** **or** **procedure.** The application is made in the canton where the company seat is located; if the company is based in Solothurn, the applicant must submit the form and listed documents to the cantonal authority. **Document** **expectations.** Solothurn's service page is one of the most document-rich official cantonal sources reviewed, requiring passport copy, business plan including market and cost analysis, trade-register extract, rental contract or offer, CV, diplomas/qualifications, job description, proof of financial security, cooperation contracts with Swiss companies, evidence of acquired customers, and a reasoned cover letter. **Economic-development involvement.** No formal economic-development role is described on the reviewed Solothurn entrepreneur pages.Timing **information.** The service page publishes a duration of three to six weeks. **Classification.** This is official cantonal procedure/guidance, not federal law. 38

### Basel-Stadt

**Authority** **and** **source.** Basel-Stadt's official page for self-employed persons with third-country nationality is concise but practically useful. **Filing** **route** **or** **procedure.** It directs applicants to the Amt für Wirtschaft und Arbeit and links to a separate self-employed work-permit application and a separate business-plan checklist. **Document** **expectations.** The page itself confirms that Basel uses a dedicated self-employed application and business-plan checklist. Search-accessible official snippets from those linked documents indicate that the application asks for details of the project and the planned percentage of self-employment, while the business-plan checklist asks, among other things, for AHV compensation-office confirmation of the assumption of self-employed activity. **Economic-development** **involvement.** Basel-Stadt separately publishes official company-settlement and start-up support pages, but the entrepreneur permit page does not assign them a formnal immigration-assessment role. **Timing** **information.** No founder-specific processing time was identified in the reviewed Basel-Stadt sources. **Classification.** The permit page and linked forms/checklists are official cantonal procedure/guidance. 39

#### Zug

**Authority** **and** **source.** Zug's official self-employment and labour-force pages sit within the Officefor Economy and Labour. Filing **route** **or** **procedure.** The self-employment page routes third-country founders to the "B2" application for residence over four months and gives the Office for Economy and Labour as the contact point. **Document expectations.** Zug's entrepreneur page states that a business plan or projected balance sheet is mandatory and that rental or credit agreements are helpful supporting evidence; it also repeats the need for enough start capital and qualifying personal characteristics. **Economic-development involvement.** Zug's official organisational pages are notable because they place work and residence permits and economic-promotion services in the same office, and the economic-promotion contact point describes itself as a one-stop shop that helps with company formation, required authorisations and settlement. #### Vaud

**Authority** **and** **source.** Vaud's relevant official materials are more form-driven than narrative.The reviewed sources include the labour-market permit form, the frontier form listing self-employed annexes, and the communal foreigner's office guidance. **Filing** **route** **or** **procedure.** Vaud's labour-market permit form routes filings according to the applicant's location: already residing in Vaud, residing in another canton, or abroad. The communal foreigner's office is described as the reception and transmission desk for permit applications to SPOP; for applicants abroad the form routes the file to the cantonal employment authority. The same form contains a field for the labour-market preliminary

<!-- 11 -->

decisionby DISMAT and identifies "independent activity" as a route on the form itself. **Document** **expectations.** Vaud's frontier self-employed annex list requires business plan, proof of financial resources, a lease for the company premises, and detailed explanations of regulatory steps taken or planned, including labour-law, trade-police, professional-authorisation, social-security affiliation and health-police aspects. The main permit formalso explicitly contemplates an "enterprise in formation". **Economic-development** **involvement.** Vaud's SPEI officially supports implantation, company creation and SME development, but no reviewed Vaud migration source made SPEI a formal permit stage. **Timing** **information.** No founder-specific official processing time was identified in the reviewed Vaud entrepreneur materials. **Classification.** Mostly official forms and administrative guidance. 41

### Bern and Ticino

Bern and Ticino are useful chiefly as cautionary contrasts. Bern's reviewed official materials clearly show the two-body structure for third-country labour cases: filing with AWI at least eight weeks in advance on the labour side, and permit issuance by the Migration Service after approval on the migration side. But the reviewed Bern founder-specific public material is thinner than Geneva's or Solothurn's. Ticino's official pages clearly identify a "third countries and labour market" service and a central permit portal, yet public founder-specific narrative guidance in the reviewed sources is comparatively sparse; this itself is a useful drafting point, because not every canton publishes the same level of entrepreneur-specific operational detail. 42

### Practical legal analysis and drafting cautions

Entrepreneur cases require local procedural mapping because the federal test is only half the story. "Overall economic interest", "necessary financial and business conditions", and "qualified worker" are national concepts, but the authority that first evaluates them, the form in which they are proved, and the degree to which the canton expects local market evidence, Swiss counterparties,regulatory-licensing roadmaps, or advance contact all vary by canton. Geneva's file architecture is not Zurich's; Solothurn's published checklist is much more granular than many other cantons; Uri adds an await-abroad rule and translation requirement; Vaud's forms show a different allocation between commune, migration office and labour-market authority. 43

That local variation affects both evidence and timing. A file that is legally viable in principle may still be weak in practice if the selected canton expects a more developed market case, fuller local regulatory mapping, or stronger evidence that the business is already operational or credibly funded. It also affects chronology. SEM's directives and several cantonal forms show that company formation and immigration filing do not always follow a single mandatory national sequence: some files may be lodged while the company is still being formed, while other cantonal materials demand a trade-register extract or strongly prefer incorporation evidence up front.Any automated route guidance that collapses these differences into one national sequence would be misleading. 44

Economic-development involvement should be stated conservatively. Officially described involvement ranges from Geneva's explicit recommendationof OCEI pre-contact, through Zurich's and Zug's institutional proximity between economic-promotion and labour-permit functions, to cantons where the reviewed migration materials do not formally place economic-promotion bodies in the permit workflow at all. Support from a business-promotion body may strengthen the commercial credibility of a dossier, but the reviewed official sources do not justify a general statement that such support guarantees, strongly predicts, or formally substitutes for labour-market and migration approval. 45

<!-- 12 -->

A route may therefore be legally open yet practically difficult in a particular canton. The SEM directives themselves show that first permits are time-limited and that extension depends on actual results against the business plan. Cantons then addd evidential and procedural texture. In internal drafting terms, "possible under Article 19 AIG" and "practically strong in Canton X on the currently published evidence model" are not the same proposition and should be kept separate. 46

The common weak points in founder files can be inferred from the official sources with reasonable confidence. They include an under-developed business plan; failure to show why the project serves Swiss rather than merely private economic interests; inadequate proof of start capital, operating premises or autonomous subsistence; uncertainty about whether the applicant is genuinely self-employed rather than functionally employed; missing or badly sequenced company-formation evidence; missing local regulatory permissions; and failure to appreciate that SEM approval still sits above the canton in approval-subject cases. Those weaknesses are not all listed in one federal text, but they emerge consistently from the SEM directives and the cantonal checklists and forms reviewed here. 47

### Drafting cautions

·Do not say that entrepreneur or founder procedure is the same in every canton. 48

·Do not treat cantonal forms, checklists or service pages as federal law. Use them as canton-specific procedure or guidance only. 49

·Do not omit SEM approval in new third-country self-employment cases. 50

·Do not imply that economic-development support guarantees a permit outcome. 45

·Do not nationalise one canton's filing sequence, especially on whether trade-register evidence must exist before filing. 5

·Do not promise processing times unless the timing is officially published for the relevant canton and expressly qualified. 51

·Do not collapse new-entry founder cases into the different Article 38 paragraph 3 AIG switch-to-self-employment route for existing B-permit holders. 23

·Do not assume "self-employed" merely because the person is a shareholder, director or CEO; the federal guidance requires real analysis of organisational control and entrepreneurial risk. 52

·Do not ignore local regulatory authorisations outside immigration where a canton expressly asks for them in the entrepreneur file. 53

### Open questions and limitations

Official material is uneven across cantons. Geneva, Zurich, Solothurn and Uri publish comparatively usable entrepreneur-specific guidance; Vaud is more form-based; Ticino's entrepreneur-specific public guidance appears thinner in the reviewed sources. Some official downloadable forms on Basel-Stadt and Zug were indexed in search but could not be fully opened through the search interface, so this pack relies on their searchable official snippets for the specific points attributed to them. In addition, the Fedlex article textsavailable through the search index were not always the latest searchable consolidation pages; accordingly, the pack uses those texts mainly for stable structural propositions that are also reflected in current 2026 SEM or current cantonal guidance. 54
