# Swiss Work Permits Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss work permits and employer sponsorship blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss work permits, including EU / EFTA hiring, non-EU work permits, employer sponsorship, labour-market testing, salary and local conditions, quotas, intra-company transfers, posted workers, short-term assignments, frontier workers, trainees, graduates, remote work, entrepreneurs, renewals, refusals and appeals.

## Folder Structure

```text
work_permits/
  broad/
  core/
  issue_specific/
    entrepreneur_and_business_founder/
  route_specific/
  README.md
```

## Folder Purposes

### `core/`

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss work permit and employer sponsorship framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);  
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA);  
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;  
cantonal labour-market and migration authority practice;  
federal approval practice;  
EU / EFTA mobility rules;  
non-EU admission rules;  
employer sponsorship;  
salary and local employment conditions;  
quotas;  
refusal, refiling and appeal procedure.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general Swiss work permit topics across multiple applicant profiles, employers, routes and factual scenarios.

### `broad/`

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss work permit scenarios.

Examples include:

legal framework and employer sponsorship;  
EU / EFTA hiring into Switzerland;  
non-EU work permit admission criteria and employer burden;  
salary, local conditions and labour-market compliance;  
quotas, timing and filing strategy;  
short-term assignments, notifications and cross-border service;  
ICT, trainee and special employment categories;  
employment changes, renewals and permit continuity;  
cantonal practice and procedure;  
refusals, refilings and appeals.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one Swiss work permit scenario.

### `route_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on a specific Swiss work permit route, applicant category, employment model or assignment structure.

Examples include:

non-EU local hire work permits;  
senior and specialist hires;  
intra-company transfers;  
short-term assignments and project-based work;  
posted workers and notification routes;  
frontier worker G permits;  
trainees and stagiaires;  
graduate switch from study to work;  
remote work and foreign-employer fact patterns;  
founder and entrepreneur work authorisation.

Route-specific packs should identify the legal basis, applicant category, employer or host structure, entitlement or discretion, eligibility conditions, procedure, evidence, risks and drafting cautions for the specific route.

### `issue_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories within Swiss work permit practice.

Examples include:

entrepreneur and business founder cases;  
weak labour-market evidence;  
borderline salary or local conditions;  
quota exhaustion;  
remote foreign-employer work;  
change of employer risks;  
continued work after employment changes;  
renewal after business underperformance;  
refiling after refusal;  
family implications of work permit instability.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the core, broad or route-specific packs provide.

### `issue_specific/entrepreneur_and_business_founder/`

Use this folder for deeper founder-specific legal authority packs.

Examples include:

entrepreneur and business founder framework;  
business plan and evidence;  
cantonal procedure and discretion;  
company formation versus work authorisation;  
economic interest and business substance;  
family residence and settlement planning;  
founder role, salary and employment structure;  
investment, funding and financial viability;  
job creation and labour-market benefit;  
refusals and refiling strategy;  
renewals, business performance and continuity.

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation, ordinances and treaties;  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and relevant EU / EFTA materials where applicable;  
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal guidance;  
official cantonal labour-market authority guidance;  
official cantonal migration authority guidance;  
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;  
cantonal administrative practice, where evidenced;  
industry or collective-agreement materials where relevant to salary and local conditions;  
internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;  
existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;  
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;  
a table of legal propositions;  
primary legal texts;  
official guidance and administrative materials;  
key case law, where relevant;  
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;  
practical legal analysis;  
drafting cautions;  
source list;  
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;  
eligibility only;  
discretion;  
facilitation;  
guidance;  
procedure;  
evidential practice;  
labour-market practice;  
cantonal practice;  
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe employer sponsorship as automatic approval. Do not describe company formation as work authorisation. Do not describe EU / EFTA facilitation as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Swiss work permits should not be treated as a single route.

The analysis usually depends on:

applicant nationality;  
EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status;  
current residence status;  
employer structure;  
Swiss employer;  
foreign employer;  
host entity;  
proposed role;  
seniority;  
specialist skills;  
qualifications;  
salary;  
local employment conditions;  
labour-market evidence;  
quota availability;  
assignment duration;  
employment contract;  
secondment structure;  
service agreement;  
place of work;  
remote-work elements;  
canton of employment;  
federal approval;  
permit type;  
family implications;  
renewal history;  
change of employer;  
refusal grounds;  
refiling or appeal strategy.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, treat employer sponsorship as automatic approval, imply that company formation creates work authorisation, apply EU / EFTA rules to non-EU / EFTA applicants, or flatten route-specific and canton-specific practice into uniform Swiss law.
