# Broad Swiss Work Permits Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss work permits and employer sponsorship blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring work permit issues across more than one route, employer category, applicant nationality or factual scenario.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_work_permits_legal_framework_and_employer_sponsorship.md`

This pack should cover the overall legal framework and employer sponsorship architecture for Swiss work permits, including the relationship between immigration permission, labour-market approval, employer burden, cantonal procedure and federal approval.

### `legal_authority_eu_efta_hiring_into_switzerland.md`

This pack should cover hiring EU / EFTA nationals into Switzerland, including free movement principles, registration or permit procedure, employment residence, frontier work where relevant and limits on applying EU / EFTA rules to third-country nationals.

### `legal_authority_non_eu_work_permits_admission_criteria_and_employer_burden.md`

This pack should cover non-EU work permit admission criteria, including employer sponsorship, priority rules, labour-market testing, seniority or specialist requirements, qualifications, economic interest, quotas and evidential burden.

### `legal_authority_salary_local_conditions_and_labour_market_compliance.md`

This pack should cover salary and local employment conditions, including market salary, collective agreements where relevant, working conditions, employment contracts, allowances, secondment terms and labour-market compliance evidence.

### `legal_authority_work_permit_quotas_timing_and_filing_strategy.md`

This pack should cover quota availability, annual and quarterly quota issues where relevant, timing of filings, start-date planning, sequencing risks, canton and federal coordination and drafting cautions around uncertain quota outcomes.

### `legal_authority_short_term_assignments_notifications_and_cross_border_service.md`

This pack should cover short-term assignments, notification procedure, cross-border service provision, posted workers, temporary work in Switzerland, assignment duration, service-contract evidence and risks where notification is insufficient.

### `legal_authority_ict_trainee_and_special_employment_categories.md`

This pack should cover intra-company transfers, trainee categories, stagiaires and other special employment categories, including eligibility conditions, assignment structure, training purpose, documentation and limits on use.

### `legal_authority_employment_changes_renewals_and_permit_continuity.md`

This pack should cover employment changes, change of employer, change of canton, role changes, permit renewal, continuity of employment, loss of employment, unemployment issues and risks to permit continuity.

### `legal_authority_work_permit_cantonal_practice_and_procedure.md`

This pack should cover cantonal filing practice, authority roles, evidential expectations, processing steps, labour-market office and migration office coordination, federal approval and practice-sensitive risks.

### `legal_authority_work_permit_refusals_refilings_and_appeals.md`

This pack should cover refusal grounds, procedural fairness, appeal routes, reconsideration, refiling strategy, new evidence, timing and drafting cautions after an adverse work permit decision.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss work permit routes.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in EU / EFTA hiring, non-EU work permit applications, employer sponsorship, labour-market testing, salary and local conditions, quota planning, short-term assignments, special employment categories, renewals, refusals and cantonal procedure.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad Swiss work permit packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; cantonal labour-market authority practice; cantonal migration authority practice; federal approval practice; employer sponsorship; labour-market testing; priority of domestic and EU / EFTA labour; salary and local employment conditions; quotas; intra-company transfer rules; notification procedure; posted-worker rules; short-term assignment procedure; frontier worker rules; change of employer; renewal; refusal, refiling and appeal procedure.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; labour-market practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe employer sponsorship as automatic approval. Do not describe EU / EFTA facilitation as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad Swiss work permit authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

applicant nationality; EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status; current residence status; employer structure; Swiss employer; foreign employer; host entity; role title and duties; seniority; specialist skills; qualifications; salary; local employment conditions; collective agreements where relevant; labour-market evidence; recruitment evidence; quota availability; assignment duration; employment contract; service agreement; secondment letter; place of work; remote-work elements; canton of employment; competent authorities; federal approval; permit type; start date; renewal; change of employer; change of canton; family implications; refusal grounds; refiling or appeal strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss work permit and employer sponsorship system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several work permit scenarios, such as EU / EFTA hiring, non-EU admission criteria, employer burden, labour-market testing, salary and local conditions, quotas, short-term assignments, special employment categories, cantonal procedure, renewals, refusals and appeals.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific work permit route or applicant category, such as non-EU local hires, intra-company transfers, posted workers, frontier workers, trainees, graduates, senior specialists, remote work or founder work authorisation.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as entrepreneur and business founder cases, weak labour-market evidence, borderline salary, quota exhaustion, remote foreign-employer work, change of employer risks, renewal after underperformance or refiling after refusal.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

Swiss work permit; work authorisation; employer sponsorship; EU / EFTA hiring; non-EU work permit; third-country national; labour-market test; priority of domestic and EU / EFTA workers; salary and local employment conditions; quota; senior specialist; intra-company transfer; ICT; posted worker; notification procedure; short-term assignment; cross-border service provision; frontier worker G permit; trainee; stagiaire; graduate switch from study to work; change of employer; permit renewal; refusal; refiling; appeal; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad work permit packs should not flatten nationality-specific, route-specific, employer-specific or canton-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on nationality, employer structure, role, salary, qualifications, labour-market evidence, quota availability, canton, duration, place of work, assignment model, permit type, renewal history and procedural posture.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating employer sponsorship as automatic, applying EU / EFTA rules to non-EU / EFTA applicants, or suggesting that all Swiss work permit applications are assessed under the same legal test.
