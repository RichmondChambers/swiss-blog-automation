# Core Swiss Work Permits and Employer Sponsorship Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss work permits and employer sponsorship blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss work permit framework before moving into broad, route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

### `legal_authority_core_swiss_work_permits_and_employer_sponsorship_framework.md`

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss work permits and employer sponsorship, including EU / EFTA and non-EU / EFTA distinctions, employer sponsorship, labour-market admission criteria, salary and local employment conditions, quotas, cantonal and federal procedure, renewals, refusals and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss work permit and employer sponsorship framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple Swiss employment immigration scenarios, including:

EU / EFTA hiring into Switzerland; non-EU work permit applications; senior and specialist hires; intra-company transfers; short-term assignments; posted workers; trainees and stagiaires; graduate transition from study to work; frontier worker permits; remote work and foreign-employer fact patterns; entrepreneur and founder work authorisations; employment changes; renewals; permit continuity; refusals, refiling and appeals.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core Swiss work permit pack should explain the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; cantonal labour-market and migration authority practice; federal approval practice; EU / EFTA mobility rules; non-EU admission rules; employer sponsorship; labour-market testing; salary and local employment conditions; quotas; priority of domestic and EU / EFTA labour; professional qualifications; economic interest; permit type and duration; employment changes; renewal; refusal, refiling and appeal procedure.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the Swiss work permit framework; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; labour-market practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe employer sponsorship as automatic approval. Do not describe EU / EFTA free movement rules as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core Swiss work permit authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

applicant nationality; EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status; employer location; Swiss employing entity; foreign employer; proposed role; seniority; specialist skills; qualifications; salary; local employment conditions; labour-market test; quota availability; economic interest; assignment duration; employment contract; secondment structure; intra-company transfer structure; posted-worker notification; short-term work; remote-work pattern; canton of employment; cantonal authority expectations; federal approval; permit type; renewal; change of employer; change of canton; family residence; refusal, refiling and appeal strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several work permit scenarios, such as employer sponsorship, EU / EFTA hiring, non-EU admission criteria, salary and local conditions, quotas, short-term assignments, cantonal procedure, renewals and refusals.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific work permit route or factual scenario, such as non-EU local hires, intra-company transfers, posted workers, frontier workers, trainees, graduates, senior specialists, remote work or founder work authorisation.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as entrepreneur and business founder cases, weak labour-market evidence, borderline salary, quota exhaustion, changes of employment, remote foreign-employer work, renewal after underperformance or refiling after refusal.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Swiss work permits should not be treated as a single route.

The analysis usually depends on nationality, employer structure, place of work, role, salary, qualifications, labour-market evidence, quota availability, canton, duration, assignment structure, permit type, change of employment, renewal history and procedural posture.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating employer sponsorship as automatic approval, applying EU / EFTA facilitation to non-EU / EFTA applicants, or flattening canton-specific labour-market practice into uniform Swiss law.
