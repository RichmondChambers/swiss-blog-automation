# Issue-Specific Swiss Work Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains issue-specific legal authority packs for the Swiss work permits and employer sponsorship blog automation workflow.

Issue-specific packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require deeper treatment of a narrower risk issue, factual pattern or complex sub-category within Swiss work permit practice.

## Current Issue-Specific Areas

### `entrepreneur_and_business_founder/`

This folder contains authority packs for Swiss entrepreneur and business founder work authorisation, including company formation, founder role, business plan evidence, economic interest, funding, job creation, cantonal discretion, renewals and refusals.

## Purpose of the Issue-Specific Folder

Use this folder for topics that need more granular treatment than the core, broad or route-specific work permit packs provide.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a risk point, evidence issue or complex factual pattern is likely to recur across work permit advice or blog content but is too narrow for a broad thematic pack.

Examples include:

entrepreneur and business founder cases; weak labour-market evidence; quota exhaustion; borderline salary or local conditions; remote work for a foreign employer; change of employer risks; continued work after employment change; renewal after business underperformance; refusals and refiling after weak evidence; family implications of work permit instability.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Issue-specific work permit packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant; SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; cantonal labour-market authority practice; cantonal migration authority practice; employer sponsorship; salary and local employment conditions; labour-market testing; quotas; permit type and duration; renewal; change of employer; refusal, refiling and appeal procedure.

## Recommended Structure for Issue-Specific Packs

Each issue-specific pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; labour-market practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe employer sponsorship as automatic approval. Do not describe company formation as work authorisation. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss work permit and employer sponsorship system.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several work permit scenarios, such as EU / EFTA hiring, non-EU admission criteria, salary and local conditions, quotas, procedure, renewals and refusals.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific work permit route or applicant category.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories requiring deeper treatment.

## Naming Convention

Use clear, descriptive filenames beginning with:

`legal_authority_`

Where a sub-folder has its own topic prefix, follow that prefix consistently.

For entrepreneur and founder packs, use:

`legal_authority_entrepreneur_`

Use lowercase filenames, underscores rather than spaces, and the `.md` extension for Markdown authority packs.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Issue-specific packs should not detach the risk issue from the underlying work permit route.

The analysis may still depend on nationality, employer structure, role, salary, labour-market evidence, quota availability, canton, duration, permit type, renewal history and procedural posture.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating factual workarounds as lawful routes, or flattening practice-sensitive risks into general rules.
