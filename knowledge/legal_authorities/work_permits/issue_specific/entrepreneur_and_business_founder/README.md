# Entrepreneur and Business Founder Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains issue-specific legal authority packs for Swiss entrepreneur and business founder work authorisation within the Swiss work permits blog automation workflow.

These packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning founders, entrepreneurs, owner-managers, start-up applicants, company formation, business plans, investment, job creation, economic interest, renewals, refusals and family or settlement planning.

## Current Issue-Specific Packs

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_and_business_founder_framework.md`

This pack should cover the overall legal framework for entrepreneur and business founder work authorisation, including the relationship between company formation, self-employment or employment structure, immigration permission, labour-market approval, economic interest and cantonal discretion.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_business_plan_and_evidence.md`

This pack should cover business plans and evidence, including commercial viability, market analysis, financial forecasts, client pipeline, operational readiness, contracts, funding, premises, staffing and evidential weaknesses.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_cantonal_procedure_and_discretion.md`

This pack should cover cantonal procedure and discretion, including filing strategy, local economic-development considerations, authority roles, evidence expectations, processing steps and canton-sensitive risks.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_company_formation_vs_work_authorisation.md`

This pack should cover the distinction between forming a Swiss company and being authorised to work for or manage that company, including common misconceptions around incorporation, shareholding, directorship and operational activity.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_economic_interest_and_business_substance.md`

This pack should cover Swiss economic interest and business substance, including innovation, regional benefit, market need, commercial substance, premises, employees, customers, suppliers and genuine operational activity.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_family_residence_and_settlement_planning.md`

This pack should cover family residence and long-term planning for entrepreneurs, including derivative residence, family reunification, permit renewal, C permit implications, naturalisation planning and risks where business performance affects status.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_founder_role_salary_and_employment_structure.md`

This pack should cover founder role, salary and employment structure, including employed founder models, self-employment, director duties, salary, local employment conditions, social security, corporate control and labour-market compliance.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_investment_funding_and_financial_viability.md`

This pack should cover investment, funding and financial viability, including capitalisation, source of funds, investor commitments, liquidity, forecasts, runway, revenue evidence and the limits of passive investment.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_job_creation_and_labour_market_benefit.md`

This pack should cover job creation and labour-market benefit, including planned Swiss hires, recruitment, local employment impact, training, specialist roles, timelines and evidential cautions around speculative job creation.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_refusals_and_refiling_strategy.md`

This pack should cover refusals and refiling strategy, including common refusal grounds, weak evidence, lack of economic interest, inadequate funding, unclear role, insufficient business substance, appeal options and revised filings.

### `legal_authority_entrepreneur_renewals_business_performance_and_continuity.md`

This pack should cover renewals, business performance and continuity, including trading performance, revenue, job creation progress, compliance, tax and social security issues, continued economic interest and risks to renewal.

## Purpose of This Folder

Use this folder for issue-specific packs that provide deeper treatment of entrepreneur and business founder work authorisation than the general work permit or route-specific founder packs.

These packs should help the generator deal with recurring founder-specific issues, including:

company formation versus work authorisation; founder role and employment structure; business plan evidence; Swiss economic interest; investment and funding; financial viability; job creation; business substance; cantonal discretion; renewals; refusals; family residence and settlement planning.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Entrepreneur and business founder packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; cantonal labour-market and migration authority practice; economic interest criteria; self-employment and employed-founder structures; salary and local employment conditions; company formation rules; commercial register evidence; business planning; funding and investment evidence; job creation; tax, social security and corporate compliance; family residence rules; renewal and loss-of-status risks.

## Recommended Structure for Entrepreneur Packs

Each entrepreneur pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the relevant founder issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; commercial practice; labour-market practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe company formation as work authorisation. Do not describe investment as a residence-by-investment route. Do not describe founder ownership as automatic permission to work. Do not describe cantonal economic-interest practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

entrepreneur work permit; business founder work authorisation; Swiss founder permit; self-employment; employed founder; company formation; incorporation; commercial register; founder role; business plan; economic interest; business substance; investment; funding; financial viability; job creation; Swiss labour-market benefit; salary and local employment conditions; family residence; permit renewal; refusal; refiling; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives.

Avoid terminology suggesting that Switzerland operates a general residence-by-investment programme for founders.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Entrepreneur and business founder cases should not be treated as ordinary company formation matters.

The analysis usually depends on nationality, company structure, founder role, employment model, economic interest, business substance, funding, viability, job creation, canton, evidence, renewals and procedural posture.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from implying that incorporation creates a right to work, that investment alone secures residence, or that a founder can freely manage a Swiss business without appropriate work authorisation.
