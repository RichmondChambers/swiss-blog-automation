# Retirement Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss retirement residence blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss retirement residence, including EU / EFTA retirees, non-EU / EFTA retirees, residence without gainful activity, financial self-sufficiency, personal ties to Switzerland, housing, health insurance, family relocation, permit renewal, loss of status and long-term planning issues.

## Folder Structure

```text
retirement_permit/
  broad/
  core/
  specific/
  README.md
```

## Folder Purposes

### `core/`

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss retirement residence framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);  
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA);  
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;  
EU / EFTA residence rules for persons not pursuing gainful activity;  
non-EU / EFTA retiree residence rules;  
financial self-sufficiency;  
no-gainful-activity requirements;  
personal ties to Switzerland;  
cantonal discretion;  
entry clearance, D visa, cantonal authorisation and residence permit issue.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general retirement residence topics across multiple applicant profiles, nationalities and residence scenarios.

### `broad/`

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several retirement residence scenarios.

Examples include:

legal framework and route architecture;  
EU / EFTA retirees;  
non-EU / EFTA retirees;  
financial self-sufficiency and public funds risk;  
personal ties to Switzerland;  
main home, physical presence and centre of life;  
no gainful activity and retirement from economic life;  
housing, insurance and practical conditions;  
family members and derivative residence;  
cantonal discretion and local practice;  
procedure, sequencing and evidence;  
permit type, duration, renewal and long-term planning;  
C permit and naturalisation planning;  
comparison with lump sum taxation and other HNWI routes.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one retirement residence scenario.

### `specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues, applicant profiles, nationality groups, canton-sensitive topics or complex sub-categories within retirement residence.

Examples include:

UK retirees;  
US retirees;  
EU / EFTA financially self-sufficient applicants;  
non-EU / EFTA retirees with Swiss family;  
former Swiss residents;  
retirees relocating with dependent family members;  
weak personal ties to Switzerland;  
borderline financial self-sufficiency;  
health insurance problems;  
social assistance risk;  
insufficient physical presence;  
continued business activity;  
foreign board roles;  
renewal after changed finances;  
naturalisation after retirement residence.

Specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the core or broad thematic packs provide.

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation, ordinances and treaties;  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and related EU / EFTA materials where relevant;  
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal guidance;  
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;  
official cantonal migration authority guidance;  
cantonal administrative practice, where evidenced;  
internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;  
existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;  
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;  
a table of legal propositions;  
primary legal texts;  
official guidance and administrative materials;  
key case law, where relevant;  
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;  
practical legal analysis;  
drafting cautions;  
source list;  
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;  
eligibility only;  
discretion;  
facilitation;  
guidance;  
procedure;  
evidential practice;  
cantonal practice;  
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe EU / EFTA facilitation as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Retirement residence should not be treated as a single route.

The analysis usually depends on:

applicant nationality;  
EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status;  
age;  
retirement status;  
whether the applicant has ceased gainful activity;  
financial resources;  
pension income;  
capital resources;  
health insurance;  
housing;  
main home in Switzerland;  
physical presence;  
centre of life;  
personal ties to Switzerland;  
family composition;  
applicable legal basis;  
whether the route creates an entitlement or is discretionary;  
cantonal discretion;  
evidence;  
entry procedure;  
permit type and duration;  
renewal history;  
public funds risk;  
C permit and naturalisation objectives.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, treat discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, apply EU / EFTA rules to non-EU / EFTA applicants, imply that retirement residence is automatic for wealthy applicants, or flatten canton-specific practice into uniform Swiss law.
