# Core Retirement Residence Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss retirement residence blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss retirement residence framework before moving into broad, route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

### `legal_authority_core_swiss_retirement_residence_framework.md`

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss retirement residence, including the main statutory routes, EU / EFTA and non-EU / EFTA distinctions, financial self-sufficiency, no-gainful-activity requirements, personal ties to Switzerland, housing, health insurance, residence permit basis, cantonal discretion, renewal risks and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss retirement residence framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple retirement residence scenarios, including:

retirement residence for EU / EFTA nationals; retirement residence for non-EU / EFTA nationals; financially self-sufficient retirees; applicants with personal ties to Switzerland; applicants seeking residence without gainful activity; family members and derivative residence; main home and physical presence requirements; housing and insurance conditions; public funds risk; cantonal discretion and local practice; permit type, duration and renewal; C permit and naturalisation planning; comparison with lump sum taxation and other HNWI residence routes.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core retirement residence pack should explain the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; cantonal migration authority practice and procedure; EU / EFTA residence rules for persons not pursuing gainful activity; non-EU / EFTA residence rules for retirees; financial self-sufficiency requirements; public funds and social assistance risk; no-gainful-activity restrictions; personal ties to Switzerland; main home and physical presence; housing and health insurance requirements; entry clearance, D visa, cantonal authorisation and residence permit issue.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the Swiss retirement residence routes; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe EU / EFTA facilitation as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core retirement residence authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

applicant nationality; EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status; age and retirement status; whether the applicant has ceased gainful activity; whether the route falls under AFMP / FZA or LEI / AIG; whether the route creates an entitlement or is discretionary; financial self-sufficiency; pension income; capital resources; health insurance; housing; main home in Switzerland; physical presence; centre of life; personal ties to Switzerland; family composition; derivative residence for family members; public funds and social assistance risk; cantonal competence; evidential expectations; entry clearance and D visa procedure; permit type and duration; renewal conditions; loss of status; C permit and naturalisation implications.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several retirement residence scenarios, such as EU / EFTA retirees, non-EU / EFTA retirees, financial self-sufficiency, personal ties, physical presence, family residence, cantonal discretion, evidence, renewal and long-term planning.

Use `route_specific/` or `specific/` for packs focused on a specific applicant category, nationality group, factual profile or residence scenario, such as UK retirees, US retirees, EU / EFTA financially self-sufficient applicants, non-EU / EFTA retirees with Swiss family, former Swiss residents or applicants relocating with dependent family members.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as weak personal ties, borderline financial self-sufficiency, health insurance problems, social assistance risk, insufficient physical presence, continued business activity, renewal after changed finances or naturalisation after retirement residence.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

retirement residence; Swiss retirement residence; retiree residence permit; residence without gainful activity; financially self-sufficient residence; EU / EFTA retiree; non-EU / EFTA retiree; personal ties to Switzerland; main home in Switzerland; physical presence; centre of life; financial self-sufficiency; pension income; public funds risk; social assistance risk; health insurance; suitable accommodation; residence permit; B permit; C permit; naturalisation; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives; D visa, where relevant to entry and sequencing.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Retirement residence should not be treated as a single route.

The analysis usually depends on the applicant’s nationality, EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status, age, retirement status, financial resources, health insurance, housing, personal ties to Switzerland, physical presence, centre of life, family composition, applicable legal basis, cantonal discretion, evidence, entry procedure and long-term objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, applying EU / EFTA rules to non-EU / EFTA applicants, or implying that all retirees are assessed under the same legal test.
