# Broad Retirement Residence Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss retirement residence blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring retirement residence issues across more than one applicant category, nationality group, permit route or factual scenario.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_legal_framework_and_route_architecture.md`

This pack should cover the overall legal framework and route architecture for Swiss retirement residence, including the relationship between EU / EFTA and non-EU / EFTA rules, residence without gainful activity, financial self-sufficiency, personal ties, cantonal discretion, permit basis and long-term planning.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_eu_efta_retirees.md`

This pack should cover retirement residence for EU / EFTA nationals, including residence rights under the AFMP / FZA, financial self-sufficiency, comprehensive health insurance, absence of social assistance dependency, family members and procedural requirements.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_non_eu_efta_retirees.md`

This pack should cover retirement residence for non-EU / EFTA nationals, including age and retirement conditions, personal ties to Switzerland, no-gainful-activity requirements, financial resources, health insurance, cantonal discretion and federal approval issues where relevant.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_financial_self_sufficiency_and_public_funds_risk.md`

This pack should cover financial self-sufficiency, pension income, capital resources, sustainable maintenance, public funds risk, social assistance concerns, supplementary benefits issues and drafting cautions where financial resources affect eligibility or renewal.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_personal_ties_to_switzerland.md`

This pack should cover personal ties to Switzerland, including previous residence, family connections, property ownership, regular stays, cultural or social connections, Swiss schooling or historical links, and evidential weaknesses in discretionary retiree cases.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_main_home_physical_presence_and_centre_of_life.md`

This pack should cover main home requirements, physical presence, centre of life, residence substance, travel pattern, household formation, tax residence interaction and risks where the applicant’s Swiss residence appears too thin.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_no_gainful_activity_and_retirement_from_economic_life.md`

This pack should cover retirement from economic life, no-gainful-activity restrictions, passive investment, foreign business ownership, board roles, occasional work, remote work risk and drafting cautions where continued activity may undermine retirement residence.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_housing_insurance_and_practical_conditions.md`

This pack should cover suitable accommodation, lease or property evidence, household formation, health insurance, accident cover where relevant, practical relocation arrangements and evidential expectations for showing viable residence in Switzerland.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_family_members_and_derivative_residence.md`

This pack should cover spouses, partners, children and dependent family members in retirement residence cases, including derivative residence, household formation, financial support, health insurance, schooling where relevant and risks where family members have independent activity or residence issues.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_cantonal_discretion_and_local_practice.md`

This pack should cover cantonal discretion, local filing practice, evidential expectations, authority roles, procedural sequencing, canton-sensitive risks and the danger of treating cantonal retirement residence practice as uniform Swiss law.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_procedure_sequencing_and_evidence.md`

This pack should cover procedural sequencing, including applications from abroad, D visa steps, cantonal filing, federal approval where relevant, evidence preparation, timing, document submission and risks arising from premature entry or incorrect filing.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_permit_type_duration_renewal_and_long_term_planning.md`

This pack should cover permit type, duration, renewal, continuing eligibility, changes in finances, health insurance or residence pattern, loss of status risk and longer-term planning for settlement or continued residence.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_c_permit_and_naturalisation_planning.md`

This pack should cover long-term residence planning, including C permit considerations, ordinary residence periods, integration, language requirements, naturalisation risks, continuity of residence and the limits of retirement residence as a long-term planning route.

### `legal_authority_retirement_residence_comparison_with_lump_sum_and_other_hnwi_routes.md`

This pack should compare retirement residence with lump sum taxation and other HNWI residence routes, including tax positioning, immigration discretion, financial thresholds, no-gainful-activity restrictions, residence substance, family relocation and long-term planning.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several retirement residence scenarios.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in EU / EFTA retiree residence, non-EU / EFTA retiree residence, financial self-sufficiency, personal ties, no-gainful-activity restrictions, family relocation, permit renewal, loss of status and long-term planning topics.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad retirement residence packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; EU / EFTA rules for persons not pursuing gainful activity; non-EU / EFTA retiree admission rules; cantonal migration authority guidance and practice; federal approval practice where relevant; financial self-sufficiency requirements; no-gainful-activity restrictions; health insurance rules; housing and household formation; personal ties to Switzerland; main home, physical presence and centre of life; public funds and social assistance risks; family residence rules; permit renewal practice; C permit and naturalisation rules.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe EU / EFTA facilitation as applicable to non-EU / EFTA applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad retirement residence authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

applicant nationality; EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status; age; retirement status; whether the applicant has ceased gainful activity; applicable legal basis; whether the route creates an entitlement or is discretionary; financial self-sufficiency; pension income; capital resources; public funds and social assistance risk; health insurance; suitable accommodation; main home in Switzerland; physical presence; centre of life; personal ties to Switzerland; family composition; derivative residence; no-gainful-activity restrictions; foreign business activity; passive investment; board roles; entry clearance and D visa procedure; cantonal competence and evidential expectations; permit type and duration; renewal conditions; loss of status; C permit and naturalisation planning; comparison with lump sum taxation or other HNWI residence routes.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss retirement residence system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several retirement residence scenarios, such as EU / EFTA retirees, non-EU / EFTA retirees, financial self-sufficiency, personal ties, physical presence, no gainful activity, family residence, evidence, procedure, renewal and long-term planning.

Use `specific/` for packs focused on a specific applicant category, nationality group, factual profile or residence scenario, such as UK retirees, US retirees, EU / EFTA financially self-sufficient applicants, non-EU / EFTA retirees with Swiss family, former Swiss residents or applicants relocating with dependent family members.

Use `issue_specific/` if added for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as weak personal ties, borderline financial self-sufficiency, health insurance problems, social assistance risk, insufficient physical presence, continued business activity, renewal after changed finances or naturalisation after retirement residence.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

retirement residence; Swiss retirement residence; retiree residence permit; residence without gainful activity; financially self-sufficient residence; EU / EFTA retiree; non-EU / EFTA retiree; personal ties to Switzerland; main home in Switzerland; physical presence; centre of life; financial self-sufficiency; pension income; public funds risk; social assistance risk; health insurance; suitable accommodation; residence permit; B permit; C permit; naturalisation; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives; D visa, where relevant to entry and sequencing.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad retirement residence packs should not flatten nationality-specific, status-specific or canton-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the applicant’s nationality, EU / EFTA or non-EU / EFTA status, age, retirement status, financial resources, pension income, capital, health insurance, housing, personal ties to Switzerland, physical presence, centre of life, family composition, applicable legal basis, cantonal discretion, evidence, entry procedure, renewal history and long-term objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, applying EU / EFTA rules to non-EU / EFTA applicants, implying that retirement residence is automatic for wealthy applicants, or suggesting that all retirees are assessed under the same legal test.
