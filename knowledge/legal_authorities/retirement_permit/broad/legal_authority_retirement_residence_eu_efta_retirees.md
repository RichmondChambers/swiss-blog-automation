# Swiss EU EFTA Retirement Residence Authority Pack

## Executive legal summary

For EU/EFTA nationals, Swiss "retirement residence" is not a separate visa category in the sense often used in informal guides. The legal architecture is the AFMP route for **persons** **not** **pursuing** **an** **economic** **activity,** implemented in Switzerland through the Ordinance on the Free Movement of Persons, SR 142.203, and administered in practice through SEM guidance and cantonal registration procedures. Retirees are one use-case within that broader treaty category, alongside students and other economically inactive persons. The route is therefore materially different fromn the discretionary non-EU/EFTA retirement route under Article 28 FNIA: for EU/EFTA nationals it is fundamentally a **treaty-based** **residence** **right,** provided the conditions are met. 1

The core AFMP conditions are comparatively narrow. The applicant must show sufficient financial means for themselves and their family members so that they will not need Swiss social assistance, and they must have all-risks health insurance cover; SEM guidance expressly states that this includes accident cover. Swiss implementing guidance treats financial sufficiency as situational rather than fixed by one national amount. As a rule, means are sufficient if a comparable Swiss person could not claim social assistance; for pension-entitled persons, the relevant benchmark is the threshold for Swiss supplementary benefits. SEM also states that there are **no** **quotas** for residence without gainful activity under the AFMP. 2

The standard permit is the **B** **EU/EFTA** **residence** **permit,** orddinarily issued for five years and valid throughout Switzerland. Official SEM guidance says it is automatically renewed so long as the conditions remain satisfied. The authorities may, however, set an earlier review point: the treaty allows revalidation after two years if considered necessary, and Swiss guidance confirms that in cases of doubtful means the initial permit may be limited to two years. If financial means or insurance later cease to exist, revocation or non-renewal becomes possible; Swiss guidance also treats later reliance on social assistance or a claim to supplemnentary benefits as a serious renewal risk. 3

For EU/EFTA retirees, **there** **is** no **separate AFMP** **age** **threshold** and no federal requirement equivalent to the non-EU/EFTA notion of having "definitively retired from economic life". The treaty category is simply "not pursuing an economic activity". That said, the route does turn on whether the person is genuinely being admitted as non-active. If the person takes up work in Switzerland, the legal basis shifts to the worker, self-employed or services route. An important official practice point is SEM's 2022home-office circular: it says an EU/EFTA national working from Switzerland for a foreign employer, with no Swiss labour-market connection and no Swiss client-facing activity, is treated for foreign-nationals law purposes as a **non-active** **person** **under Annex** **I** **Article** **24,** and the foreign income may count toward sufficient resources. But where there is a Swiss labour-market connection, Swiss employment or cross-border service rules comne into play instead. 4

Family members are treated comparatively favourably. The AFMP guarantees residence rights for family members **irrespective** **of nationality** and also guarantees their access to gainful activity irrespective of nationality. Core family members are the spouse, dependent descendants under 21 or otherwise

dependent, and dependent ascendants; for students the category is narrower, but that limitation is not normally relevant to retiree files. Swiss federal guidance also recognises the AFMP's facilitation clause for "other family members"; SEM's 2026 directives expressly mention cohabiting partners as an example, but correctly state that this is not a strict entitlement under Article 3(2). The family member's status is derivative: it exists only while the sponsor's original AFMP residence right exists. 5

On accommodation, the sources draw an important distinction. In the material reviewed, the principal economically inactive EU/EFTA applicant's black-letter federal conditions are financial sufficiency and insurance, not a separately stated housing test. In practice, however, cantonal authorities require a Swiss residential address and usually ask for a lease or host attestation as part of the application file.By contrast, for **family** **reunification** the SEM's 2026 directives treat appropriate housing for the whole family as a condition under Annex I Article 3(1), and cantonal checklists reflect that approach.Put differently: housing is clearly material in family cases and procedurally material in all cases, but the safer drafting position is not to present "adequate accommodation" as an unqualified, standalone AFMP eligibility rule for the principal retiree unless the proposition is expressly limited to family reunion or cantonal practice. 6

Procedure is decentralised. For stays above three months, the applicant must register after arrival and apply through the commune or cantonal migration authority; official SEM FAQ states that, for residence without gainful activity, registration should be made within 14 days of arrival. Zurich explains that the commune forwards the application to the cantonal migration office; Geneva provides a detailed filing route and evidential list. In ordinary AFMP economically inactive cases, the pack did not identify any SEM approval layer equivalent to the federal approval sometimes seen in third-country cases. 7

## Structural overview of the EU EFTA framework

**Legal basis.** AFMP Article 6 guarantees residence for persons not pursuing an economic activity in accordance with Annex I, and AFMP Article 7 adds derivative residence rights for family members irrespective of nationality and labour-market access for family members irrespective of nationality. The current SEM1 legal-basis page identifies SR 142.203 as the Ordinance on the Free Movement of Persons. 8

**Route** **architecture.** The operative route for retirees is Annex I, Chapter V, Article 24: residence for non-active persons. Retirees are expressly treated by SEM as one example within the broader "economically inactive" class, rather than as a self-contained permit category. That is why descriptions such as "retirement visa" are, at best, shorthand. 9

**Permit** type **and** **duration.** The ordinary document is the B EU/EFTA permit without gainful activity. SEM states that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals are entitled to that permit if they show sufficient means and adequate insurance; the permit is ordinarily five years, valid throughout Switzerland, and may be renewed while conditions continue to apply. Where means are doubtful, review after two years is possible. 10

**Financial self-sufficiency.** The treaty uses a no-social-assistance test. Swiss implementation translates that into welfare and, for pension-entitled persons, supplementary-benefit benchmarks. The upshot is that there is no single universal figure: the test is personal-situation based. Official and judicial materials accept pensions, capital and other reliable resources; later federal case law also accepts third-party guarantees in principle, provided the evidence is robust enough. Premium subsidies under Swiss health-insurance law are not, without more, treated as social assistance for this purpose. 11

<!-- 2 -->

**Health** **and** **accident insurance.** Treaty wording requires all-risks sickness insurance cover; SEM guidance states in plain terms that economicallyinactive persons must be fully covered for health risks, including accident insurance. 12

**No** **gainful activity.** Treaty law frames the route as residence for persons not pursuing an economic activity. The federal materials reviewed do not impose a separate retirement-age threshold or a non-EU-style requirement of complete withdrawal from economic life. In practice, the legal question is whether the person remains properly classifiable as economically inactive under AFMP law. SEM's home-office circular is particularly important for modern files because it treats foreign-employer remote work with no Swiss market link as stilI falling within Article 24 practice, while Swiss-market-linked work does not. 13

**Accommodation** **and** **main** **residence.** The principal AFMP conditions in the reviewed federal sources are means and insurance. I did not find a federal black-letter AFMP text making "adequate accommodation" a standalone eligibility requirement for the principal economically inactive applicant. Cantonal practice nevertheless requires proof of a Swiss address as part of the file. For family reunification, SEM guidance expressly treats appropriate housing for the whole family as a condition. 14

**Family** **members.** Core family members are protected regardless of nationality. Other relatives and cohabiting partners may benefit from facilitated admission under the AFMP's "other family members" clause, but not from an automatic right. Official SEM annex material also notes that prior durable residence by a third-country family member in an EU/EFTA state is not required. 15

**Procedure** **and** **cantonal practice.** Registration and file-handling are canton-facing. SEM's FAQ says non-active EU/EFTA nationals should register within 14 days of arrival and apply for the permit; Zurich and Geneva both show that the file is assembled locally and that lease/address evidence, identity documents and resource evidence are routinely required. 7

## Primary legal texts

**AFMP** **Article** 6. The Agreement guarantees residence in the territory of a contracting party to persons not pursuing an economic activity in accordance with Annex I. This is the treaty entry-point for EU/EFTA retiree residence. 29

**AFMP** **Article** 7(d) **and** (e). The Agreement requires provision for residence rights of family members irrespective of nationality, and for family members' right to pursue an economic activity irrespective of nationality. These provisions explain why AFMP family reunification is nationality-neutral and why derivative family rights are structurally broader than under ordinary foreign-nationals law. 30

**Annex** I, **Article** **24(1) and** (2). The treaty text for non-active persons provides that a national of a contracting party not pursuing an economic activity and having no other right of residence shall receive a residence permit valid for at least five years if they prove sufficient financial means not to require social assistance and all-risks sickness insurance cover. It further states that means are sufficient if they exceed the level below which nationals in the same personal situation could claim social-security/ welfare benefits, and allows the host state to require revalidation at the end of the first two years if necessary. This is the core black-letter rule for EU/EFTA retiree residence. 31

<!-- 6 -->

**Annex** I, **Article** 3. Article 3 entitles a person with a right of residence to be joined by family members; it lists the spouse, qualifying descendants and dependent ascendants as family members regardless of nationality, and it contains a facilitation clause for other family members not falling within the core categories. The same article also governs proof documents. This provision structures family-member analysis for EU/EFTA retirees. 32

**Ordinance on the Free Movement of Persons, SR 142.203.** The current SEM legal-basis page identifies SR 142.203 as the applicable ordinance. Official SEM-hosted ordinance text and the 2026 SEM directives show that Article 16 implements the financial-means test by reference to Swiss welfare levels and, for pension-entitled persons, supplementary-benefit thresholds; Article 17 allows earlier revalidation in non-active cases where authorities consider it necessary. Because the current Fedlex text was not directly parsable in the web session, the wording here is given conservatively as a close paraphrase rather than a verbatim current quotation. 33

**SEM** **B** **EU/EFTA** **permit guidance.** Although not itself primary law, the SEM's permit page is useful for translating the treaty/ordinance framework into permit architecture: it states that non-working EU/EFTA nationals are entitled to a B EU/EFTA permit if they show sufficient means and adequate health and accident insurance, and that the ordinary permit validity is five years. 34

## Official guidance and cantonal practice

At federal level, the most useful administrative materials are the SEM factsheet **"Residing** **in** **Switzerland** **without** **gainful** activity", the SEM's B EU/EFTA permit page, the SEM FAQ on free movement, the January 2026 **VFP** **Directives,** and the April 2022 SEM circular on **home** **office** **under** **the** **AFMP** **and** **third-country** **law.** Taken together, these materials confirm the five-year B permit structure, the welfare/EL benchmark approach to finances, the importance of all-risks insurance, the lack of quotas, the within-14-days registration expectation, the derivative nature of family rights, and the special administrative treatment of foreign-employer remote work with no Swiss labour-market link. 35

The cantonal material is best used as **procedure** **and** **evidence** **guidance,** not as a statement of federal black-letter law. Geneva's March 2026 page on EU/EFTA residence without gainful activity says the applicant has a right of residence if they have sufficient financial resources and comprehensive health and accident insurance; it then gives a useful evidential list, stating that resources may come from a pension, personal wealth, telework for a foreign employer with no impact on the Swiss labour market, the duly authorised gainful activity of a spouse, or a Swiss-based guarantor. Geneva also requires a valid identity document, a copy of the lease or host attestation, and documents proving sufficient means. 36

Zurich's official page likewise shows the practical sequence: after entry, EU/EFTA nationals file the residence application with the local commune if the stay will exceed three months or 90 working days, and thecommune forwards the file to the cantonal migration office. Zurich's EU/EFTA family reunification checklist also requires proof of relationship, proof of support for dependent relatives, proof of sufficient means for non-active sponsors, and a copy of the apartment lease for an appropriate family dwelling. This is useful confirmation that address and housing evidence are part of ordinary file preparation even where thne federal treaty text focuses principally on means and insurance. 37

A further official practice point comes from the SEM annex to its directives on family reunification: it expressly notes that, for third-country family members of EU/EFTA sponsors, prior durable residence in an EU/EFTA state is irrelevant, citing BGE 136 II 5. That is a heIpful anti-misstatement source for later drafting. 38

## Practical legal analysis

The decisive legal distinction is that the EU/EFTA retiree stands on **AFMP** **treaty** **ground** rather than on discretionary Swiss admission law. If the applicant is genuinely a national of an EU/EFTA state, seeks residence as a non-active person, and can prove means and insurance, the legal structure is rights-based and not quota-driven. That is why the AFMP route is usually much stronger than the non-EU/EFTA retirement route: there is no Article 28-style age threshold, no "particular personal ties" test, and no general discretionary balancing exercise equivalent to the one applied in third-country retirement files. 39

Financial self-sufficiency is the main practical battleground, but it is not properly reduced to a single number. Swiss implementation uses a contextual public-funds analysis: welfare thresholds for non-pension cases, supplementary-benefit thresholds for pension-entitled cases, and continuing scrutiny over time. That is why evidence quality matters more than slogan-level claims about "wealth". Pensions, capital and other stable resources plainly matter; third-party support may work, but the court has insisted on a realistic, enforceable structure; and later resort to social assistance or supplementary benefits is a concrete renewal risk. By contrast, ordinary health-insurance premium subsidies do not automatically collapse the case because the Federal Supreme Court has distinguished them from social assistance and from supplementary benefits. 40

The non-active route also requires careful handling of work-related facts. In classic retiree filesthe issue is easy: the person is simply living on pension or assets. In modern cases it is often not. SEM's home-office circular shows that Swiss practice has adapted: foreign-employer remote work with no Swiss labour-market or Swiss-client connection can still be treated, for immigration-law purposes,as compatible with Article 24 non-active residence, and the resulting income can count toward means. But that is an **administrative** **qualification** **rule,** not a broad licence to treat any paid activity as irrelevant. If there is a Swiss labour-market connection, Swiss employer, Swiss client acquisition, or cross-border services reality, the file may move out of Article 24 and into a different AFMP status analysis. Later content should therefore avoid simplistic statements such as "no work at all is allowed" or, conversely, "remote work is always permitted". 25

Family-member cases need equal care. The AFMP is generous in making family rights nationality-neutral and in allowing family members to work, but those rights remain derivative from the sponsor's own residence right. The sponsor must therefore establish and keep their original AFMP basis. That is why recent case law matters: in 2C152/2025 the Federal Supreme Court rejected an attempt to use the third-country spouse's Swiss employment as the engine that would generate the resources needed for the EU citizen sponsor's own economically inactive residence. Equally, unmarried partners and other non-core relatives should not be treated as enjoying a full AFMP entitlement: the better official formulation is that their admission is to be facilitated, and SEM's own directives say no direct claim arises from that clause alone. 41

On accommodation and "main residence", the safe analytical position is nuanced. For the principal non-active applicant, the federal sources reviewed did not yield a clearly stated standalone AFMP housing threshold analogous to ordinary domestic immigration law. But in real file-building terms, a Swwiss residential address is obviously central because the person is seeking residence in Switzerland, not a purely formal permit, and cantonal interfaces require lease or host evidence. For family reunification, the housing point is stronger still because SEM's directives treat appropriate housing for the family as a condition. Later drafting should therefore distinguish **federal eligibility** **rules** **from** **procedural and** **evidential expectations** rather than merging them. 14

## Drafting cautions and source list

The following cautions flow directly from the authorities above and should govern later content generation. 42

·Do not describe the EU/EFTA route as a standalone Swiss "retirement visa" without clarifying that the legal basis is AFMP residence for **persons not pursuing an economic activity.**

·Do not import the non-EU/EFTA retirement age requirement into EU/EFTA analysis.

·Do not suggest that one fixed national amount guarantees success; the financial test is benchmarked against Swiss welfare or supplementary-benefit thresholds and depends on personal circumstance.

·Do not omit all-risks health insurance, including accident cover.

·Do not state that any receipt of public support is automatically fatal; premium subsidies are not the same as social assistance or supplementary benefits.

·Do not state that any third-party guarantee is automatically enough; guarantees are acceptable in principle but are scrutinised for reliability and practical enforceability.

·Do not say that EU/EFTA retirees must always cease all paid activity everywhere; treaty law speaks in terms of non-active status, and SEM practice makes a specific allowance for foreign-employer remote work with no Swiss labour-market connection.

·Do not say that remote work is always permitted either; Swiss-market-linked work changes the legal classification.

·Do not state that unmarried partners have the same AFMP entitlement as spouses; the safer formula is facilitation, not automatic right.

·Do not present "adequate accommodation" as an unqualified federal eligibility rule for the principal applicant unless the sentence is expressly limited to family reunification or to cantonal procedural evidence.

·Do not imply that family rights are independent of the sponsor; they are derivative from the sponsor's original AFMP residence right.

**Open** **questions** **and** **limitations.** The current Fedlex text of SR 142.203 was not directly parsable in this session, so the discussion of Articles 16 and 17 rests on the official SEM-hosted ordinance text available in the results and on the 2026 SEM directives applying those articles. I also did not identify, in the reviewed federal AFMP texts, a standalone black-letter housing condition for the principal economically inactive applicant; that point should therefore be drafted as a qualified proposition, not a hard federal rule. 43
