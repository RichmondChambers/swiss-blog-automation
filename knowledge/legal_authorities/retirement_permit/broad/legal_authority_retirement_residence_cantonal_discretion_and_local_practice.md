# Swiss Retirement Residence Cantonal Discretion and Local Practice

## Executive legal summary

### A. Executive legal summary

Swiss "retirement residence" is not a single national visa category. In practice, the expression covers two very different legal routes. For EU/EFTA nationals, residence without gainful activity is governed primarily by the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and its implementing rules; if the applicant proves sufficient means and comprehensive sickness insurance, the position is rights-based rather than discretionary. For non-EU/EFTA nationals, by contrast, the relevant route is Article 28 AIG together with Article 25 VZAE, and that route is expressly discretionary: even where the statutory conditions appear to be met, there is no legal entitlement to the permit. 1

Cantonal and communal authorities are central to how both routes are administered, but their role differs by route. In EU/EFTA cases, cantons and communes mainly verify compliance with treaty conditions, receive registrations, and issue the documentary permit that reflects an underlying treaty right. Zurich's official guidance, for example, states that persons covered by theAFMP generally have a legal claim if the relevant conditions are met, and that EU/EFTA applications in Zurich are filed on arrival with thne competent residents' registration office. Geneva's official pages likewise require an application for stays over three months and list the evidence the canton expects to see. 2

In non-EU/EFTA retirement cases, cantonal discretion is much broader. SEM's 2026 foreigner-sector directives state that Article 28 AIG and Article 25 VZAE conditions must be met cumulatively, summarising them as minimum age 55, special personal ties to Switzerland, necessary financial means, and no gainful activity in Switzerland or abroad other than management of one's own assets. The same directives also say that grant of the residence permit is to be submitted to SEM for approval. Public cantonal materials from Zurich and Lucerne show that cantons do not merely tick off those federal conditions mechanically: they demand canton-specific evidence, assess the credibility and depth of local ties, and apply their own evidential expectations to the financial and personal-ties analysis. 3

The clearest area of approval variation is evidence. Zurich's public retiree guidance requires the applicant to state why residence is sought in Zurich, identify the intended municipality, provide a detailed account of ties to the canton, and confirm in writing that no gainful activity is pursued in Switzerland or abroad. Zurich's longer internal/public instruction on residence without gainful activity for third-country nationals goes further still: it says family ties aloone do not establish the "special personal ties" required by Article 28 AIG; it treats the route as restrictive; it assesses finances over the applicant's expected remaining lifetime; and it applies narrow limits to support from relatives. Lucerne's public note is shorter, but still requires proof of special personal ties, prior Swiss connections, and a D-visa application from abroad with criminal-record formalities. 4

For EU/EFTA retirees, cantonal practice also varies, but more at the level of documentary handling than legal discretion. Geneva publicly accepts a range of resource sources, including pensions, personal assets, an authorised spouse's work, a Swiss-based guarantor, and even telework for a foreign employer with no impact on the Swiss labour market. Zurich's AFMP gguidance emphasises the claim-based nature of the route, the standard five-year duration of the initial permit, and the possibility of a shorter two-year duration where necessary. Lucerne and Ticino publish relatively concise notices focused on forms, insurance, deadlines, renewal, notification duties, and the effects of prolonged absence abroad. 5

The most important drafting point for internal use is therefore to distinguish sharply between **law** and **cantonal practice.** Federal law and treaty rules set the legal architecture; SEM guidance explains how the federal administration expects those rules to be applied; cantons and communes implement them, gather evidence, and make the first-line case assessment; and public canton materials reveal meaningful differences in how retirement cases are prepared and scrutinised. Those differences are especially marked for non-EU/EFTA retirees, where the law is discretionary and the published practice of Zurich, Lucerne, Geneva and Ticino shows materially different emphases on ties, finances, procedure, and renewal compliance. 6

## Structural overview of cantonal practice and discretion

### B. Structural overview of the leggal framework

**Federal law** **and** **treaty** **layer.** The AFMP route for EU/EFTA nationals is governed by treaty law, chiefly Annex I Article 24 AFMP, together with the VFP/OLCP implementing rules and SEM's free-movement directives. The third-country retiree route is governed by domestic immigration law, chiefly Article 28AIG and Article 25 VZAE. The content of those conditions is federal; cantons do not create their own legal retirement categories. 7

**AFMP** **rights.** For EU/EFTA nationals who are economically inactive, the decisive issue is whether the treaty conditions are met. OfficiaI sources state that non-working EU/EFTA nationals have a right of residence if they have sufficient financial means and comprehensive sickness insurance. SEM's directives add that there are no quotas for stays without gainful activity and that the restrictive admission conditions in Articles 23-25 VZAE do not apply to AFMP residence. 8

**Cantonal administration.** Cantonal migration offices are the operational gatekeepers. Geneva's OCPM states that its foreigners' service is the competent cantonal authority for examining, granting and renewing residence authorisations. Zurich's and Lucerne's migration pages perform the same practical function through public application pages, forms, and cantonal guidance notes. Cantons therefore shape the outcome through evidential demands, not by rewvriting federal law. 9

**Communes** **and** **local registration.** Communes remain important because Swiss residence law is tied to factual local residence and registration. The V/ZAE requires registration within 14 days after entry for a stay without gainful activity of more than three months where entry authorisation was issued,and ch.ch explains that arrival at the new commune generally has to be declared within 14 days.Zurich's AFMP guidance says the EU/EFTA application is filed on arrival with the competent residents' registration office; Ticino's notices likewise reqquire notification of various changes within 14 days. 10

**Where** **discretion** **sits.** Discretion is narrowest under the AFMP and widest under Article 28 AIG. Zurich's AFMP guidance says persons falling within the agreement's material scope generally have a legal claim if the conditions are met. By contrast, SEM's foreigner-sector directives, Zurich's third-country guidance, and Federal Supreme Court case law all say that Article 28 AIG is a discretionary basis only and confers no entitlement. 1

**Where** **practice** **varies** **most.** The strongest published variation appears in financial methodology, proof of personal ties, procedural sequencing, and documentary granularity. Geneva publicly lists acceptable sources of means for EU/EFTA applicants, including some resource types not mentioned on other cantonal pages. Zurich publishes a much more developed analytical framework for third-country retirees, including actuarial-style lifetime sufficiency analysis and limits on third-party support. Lucerne publishes simpler but still restrictive public notes, while Ticino's public notices focus heavily on ongoing compliance, notification duties, renewal timing, and lapsing after long absences. 12

## Official guidance and cantonal practice notes

### E. Official guidance / administrative materials

SEM's 2026 directives on the foreigner sector are the most important federal administrative source for third-country retiree cases. In the retirement section they state that Article 28 AIG and Article 25 VZAE conditions must be fulfilled cumulatively, list the four core conditions, note that the permit grant is to be submitted to SEM for approval, and explain that close relatives in Switzerland do not on their own satisfy the required ties because the relationship must be to Switzerland itself. 33

SEM's 2026 free-movement directives and its official fact sheet on residence in Switzerland without gainful activity are the core federal administrative sources for EU/EFTA retirees. They confirm that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals have an AFMP-based right if sufficient means and comprehensive sickness insurance are shown, state that the relevant social-assistance benchmark is based on SKOS for general non-workers, and explain that newly arriving pensioners must exceed the EL threshold. They also confirm that a B EU/EFTA permit is normally issued for five years, but may be limited to two years if the means are not secure. 34

Official case law adds important clarification. In 2C891/2022, the Federal Supreme Court restated the text of Article 24 Annex I AFMP, confirmed the sufficient-mneans and sickness-insurance requirements, explained that support from a third party can in principle count if genuinely available, and rejected unduly rigid proof requirements that would hollow out the AFMP right. In 2C484/2022,the court

confirmed that, for Article 24 Annex I AFMP, the receipt of supplementary benefits is treated as equivalent to social assistance and can justify non-renewal. 30

For Article 28 AIG, official Supreme Court decisions reinforce the discretionary character of the route. The Court has stated in multiplejudgments that Article 28 AIG is merely a basis for discretionary permits and not a claim-right. In 2C124/2023 it also upheld refusal where the factual ties to Switzerland were too weak on the facts found below. 35

### F. Cantonal practice notes

Geneva.Genev/a's OCPM publicly describes itself as the competent cantonal authority for examining, granting and renewing residence and work permits. Its EU/EFTA "without activity" page is unusually detailed: it lists acceptable resource sources, including a pension, personal assets, telework for a foreign employer with no impact on the Swiss labour market, authorised work by a spouse, and a Swiss-based guarantor; it also lists the local documentary set, including ID, lease/host attestation, proof of resources, and family-status documents. Geneva's general overview page further states that third-country applicants seeking a stay of more than 90 days without gainful activity must in principle apply before entry, from abroad, and-if visa-reqquired-also through the competent Swiss representation. These points are procedural and evidentialI rather than black-letter federal law. 36

**Zurich.** Zurich publishes the most developed public material located in this research. For EU/EFTA applicants its guidance says persons covered by the AFMP generally have a legal claim, and that the application is filed after entry together with registration at the local residents' office. For third-country retirees, Zurich's specific application page requires a pass copy, reasons for residence in Zurich, the intended municipality, a detailed account of ties to Zurich, and a written confirmation that no gainful activity is pursued in Switzerland or abroad. Zurich's broader instructionon third-country residence without gainful activity describes the route as restrictive, says family ties alone do not suffice,assesses fundIs over expected lifetime, includes region-specific insurance averages and care-cost projections, and limits third-party support to one quarter of living costs for two years. These are clearly practice and methodology points, not freestanding federal rules. 37

**Lucerne.** Lucerne's public "Retirees" page and its two one-page notes are shorter but still useful.For non-EU/EFTA retirees Lucerne restates the cumulative federal conditions, says close family ties alone are not enough, and requires the D-visa application route from abroad, a home-country criminal-record extract in the original with German translation and apostille, and proof of earlier stays. For EU/EFTA non-workers it uses the SKOS benchmark, confirms the EL threshold for pensioners, and requires comprehensive sickness insurance. The same EU/EFTA note also says that if retirees later claim supplementary benefits or other non-contributory special benefits such as premium reductions,the permit can be revoked or not renewed; that statement is stricter than the SEM free-movement guidance on premium subsidies and should therefore be treated as a local-practice signal rather than a clean statement of federal law. 38

**Ticino.** The official Ticino notices found are older, but still show practical local administration. The EU/ EFTA notice states that the permit is valid throughout Switzerland, that changes of address, civil status and family composition must be notified within 14 days, that departure abroad must be notified in advance,that renewal must be sought at least 14 days before expiry, and that the permit lapses after more than six months' uninterrupted absence abroad. The third-country notice similarly stresses purpose-bound validity, 14-day notification duties, renewal timing, six-month lapse through absence abroad, and housing/means requirements for family reunification. These are best treated as practical compliance notes, and their age should be kept in mind. 39

## Practical legal analysis and drafting cautions

### G. Practical legal analysis

The central legal divide is between **treaty implementation and administrative discretion.** EU/EFTA retirement residence does not depend on a canton deciding whether it wishes to admit the person as a retiree; rather, the canton verifies whether AFMP conditions are met. That is why official federal and cantonal sources describe a legal claim, standard five-year B EU/EFTA permits, the inapplicability of the restrictive Article 25 VZAE retiree criteria, and no quotas for economically inactive AFMP residence. Cantonal variation therefore occurs mostly through documentary handling, timing, and the practical assessment of whether the evidence truly proves sufficient means and insurance. 40

Third-country retirement residence differs fundamentally. Article 28 AIG is cast in permissive language, and the Federal Supreme Court expressly treats it as a discretionary basis only. That matters because the legal question is not simply whether the federal minima are ticked, but whether the canton-subject to federal supervision and, according to SEM's directives, SEM approval-exercises its discretion favourably on the facts. Zurich's public instruction demonstrates how that discretion is operationalised: a restrictive approach, a detailed inquiry into ties to the canton, and a stricter financial methodology than the bare words of Article 28 AIG alone would suggest. 41

The evidential centre of gravity also differs sharply. Under the AFMP, the core question is whether the person is objectively self-sufficient and insured. Geneva's EU/EFTA page shows a relatively pragmatic evidential stance, expressly listing a range of accepted resource sources, including foreign-employer telework where there is no Swiss labour-market impact. The Supreme Court's 2C891/2022 judgment pulls in the same direction by accepting third-party support in principle if it is genuinely available and proved in a proportionate way. 42

Under Article 28 AIG, however, cantons may ask more searchingly why this canton and why Switzerland now. Zurich's insistence on reasons for choosing Zurich and the intended municipality, together with a detailed presentation of canton-specific ties, shows how local discretion can turn a formally federal "special personal ties" requirement into a distinctly local evidential exercise. Lucerne's note is less elaborate, but still requires proof of earlier Swiss stays and special personal ties, plus a D-visa route and criminal-record formalities from abroad. 43

Financial variation isone of the most important approval variables. Federal law supplies the baseline benchmark-EL for Article 28 retirees, SKOS/EL rules for AFMP non-workers depending on category-but it does not itself publish a single national retirement amount. Zurich's public third-country guidance illustrates how cantonal practice builds out that benchmark by adding rent annd related living costs, canton/regional average health-insurance calculations, and-after age 70-expected care costs, assessed against statistical life expectancy. That methodology is not found in such detail in the federal statute itself, but it is immensely important in real preparation because it changes how much capital and what evidence a canton may want to see. 44

Published local guidance also reveals some inconsistency. SEM's free-movement directives say premium reductions for health insurance are not treated as social assistance for residence purposes, citing Federal Supreme Court case law. Lucerne's public EU/EFTA note, however, says that even other non-contributory special benefits such as premium reductions can lead to revocation or non-renewal. Internal drafting should therefore avoid turning any one cantonal leaflet into a statement of uniform federal law, while also recognising that such local positions can affect real-world file handling and risk appetite. 45

Finally, commune-level residence substance still matters because registration and permit administration are tied to real local residence. Federal rules require registration within 14 days in relevant cases, and official cantonal notices require prompt notification of address changes and other local-status changes. Ticino's public notices are particularly explicit about 14-day notifications, renewal timing, and loss after lengthy absence abroad. These points are not unique to retirement cases, but they become especially important at renewal stage, when cantonal authorities test whether the factual route being claimed is the one actually being lived. 46

### H.Drafting cautions

·Do not describe Swiss retirement residence as one unified "retirement visa" framework; distinguish AFMP residence rights from Article 28 AIG discretionary admission. 47

·Do not imply that a non-EU/EFTA retiree who satisfies the age, ties and finnance criteria has a legal entitlement to a permit. 16

·Do not present cantonal evidential preferences as though they were federal black-letter law. Zurich's life-expectancy model and cap on third-party support are local practice points. 24

·Do not collapse "special personal ties to Switzerland" into family ties alone. Federal and cantonal official mmaterials treat that as insufficient in ordinary cases. 17

·Do not state a single nationwide retirement-funds amount for third-country retirees unless the point is explicitly tied to a canton's published method or to the EL benchmark concept. 48

·Do not assume all cantons take the same view on third-party guarantees, premium reductions, or the evidence needed to prove local ties. 49

·Do not omit the role of communes and local registration offices, especially for EU/EFTA filings and post-arrival compliance. 50

·Do not omit SEM's supervisory role inArticle 28 retiree cases where the federal directives indicate submission for approval. 21

·Do not treat older cantonal leaflets as conclusive evidence of current nationwide practice; use them as canton-specific practice indicators and date them where possible. 51

·Do not assume that a permit's formal grant resolves all future risk; official sources repeatedly stress revocation or non-renewal if the underlying conditioons cease to be met. 52
