# Broad Swiss C Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss settlement and C permit blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring C permit and settlement issues across more than one applicant category, nationality group, permit route or factual scenario.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_ordinary_c_permit_eligibility_pack.md`

This pack should cover ordinary C permit eligibility, including residence duration, continuity of residence, current permit status, nationality-based differences, integration conditions, financial independence, compliance and recurring drafting cautions.

### `legal_authority_fast_track_c_permit_routes_pack.md`

This pack should cover accelerated or fast-track C permit routes, including early settlement after successful integration, nationality-based settlement agreements where relevant, spouses and family-related routes, evidential thresholds and limits on availability.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_integration_language_and_compliance_pack.md`

This pack should cover integration, language ability, respect for public order, financial participation, education or employment, social assistance risk, debt enforcement, criminal-record issues, tax compliance and evidential standards.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_cantonal_practice_and_procedure_pack.md`

This pack should cover cantonal procedure, filing practice, forms, evidence, renewal or conversion process, authority roles, communications with migration offices, local discretion and practice-sensitive risks.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_absence_lapse_and_preservation_pack.md`

This pack should cover absence from Switzerland, lapse of C permit status, preservation or suspension requests, time limits, relocation abroad, return planning and risks arising from long or repeated absences.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_rights_stability_and_limits_pack.md`

This pack should cover the rights, stability and limits of C permit status, including labour-market access, change of canton, family implications, renewal formalities, downgrade risk, revocation risk and circumstances where status may still be lost.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_family_life_planning_pack.md`

This pack should cover family life planning for C permit holders and applicants, including spouses, children, family reunification implications, derivative residence, children approaching adulthood, household formation and long-term family residence planning.

### `legal_authority_c_permit_naturalisation_planning_pack.md`

This pack should cover naturalisation planning for C permit holders, including residence periods, continuity of residence, integration, language, criminal and financial compliance, municipal and cantonal requirements, and the relationship between C permit status and Swiss citizenship applications.

### `legal_authority_l_b_c_permit_long_term_planning_comparison_pack.md`

This pack should compare L, B and C permits for long-term planning, including residence stability, renewal, labour-market access, family implications, settlement eligibility, naturalisation planning and strategic risks.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several settlement and C permit scenarios.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in ordinary C permit applications, fast-track settlement, integration and language evidence, family life planning, absences, preservation, canton-sensitive procedure, permit stability, naturalisation planning and long-term comparison with L or B permits.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad C permit packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant; SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; nationality-based settlement agreements, where relevant; cantonal migration authority guidance and practice; ordinary C permit eligibility; accelerated settlement routes; integration and language requirements; public-order and compliance conditions; financial independence and social assistance risk; absence, lapse and preservation rules; family residence rules; change of canton rules; downgrade, revocation and loss of status; naturalisation law and practice.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; nationality-specific practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic grant. Do not describe accelerated settlement routes as available to all applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad C permit authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

applicant nationality; current permit type; length of lawful residence; ordinary or accelerated eligibility; continuity of residence; residence interruptions; applicable settlement agreement; integration; language evidence; economic participation; education or employment; financial independence; social assistance history; debt enforcement; criminal-record issues; tax compliance; public-order concerns; family composition; spouse or child status; change of canton; absences from Switzerland; preservation requests; renewal or control procedure; cantonal competence; evidential expectations; downgrade risk; revocation risk; naturalisation planning; comparison with L and B permit status.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss C permit and settlement system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several C permit scenarios, such as ordinary eligibility, fast-track routes, integration and language, cantonal practice, absence and preservation, rights and limits, family life planning, naturalisation planning and comparison with L or B permits.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific C permit route or applicant category, such as EU / EFTA nationals, third-country nationals, spouses of Swiss citizens, spouses of C permit holders, children, refugees, stateless persons or nationals benefiting from settlement agreements.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as social assistance history, debt enforcement, criminal convictions, long absences, weak language evidence, failed integration, downgrade risk, revocation risk, cross-cantonal moves or preservation after relocation abroad.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

C permit; Swiss C permit; settlement permit; permanent residence; settled residence; ordinary C permit; fast-track C permit; accelerated C permit; early C permit; integration requirements; language requirement; financial independence; social assistance risk; public order; good conduct; absence from Switzerland; C permit lapse; preservation of C permit; family life planning; naturalisation planning; L permit; B permit; residence permit; change of canton; downgrade; revocation; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad C permit packs should not flatten nationality-specific, route-specific or canton-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the applicant’s nationality, current permit type, residence duration, ordinary or accelerated route, applicable settlement agreement, integration evidence, language ability, financial independence, compliance history, family circumstances, absences, canton, evidential expectations, renewal procedure and naturalisation objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating settlement as automatic, implying that all nationalities follow the same route or timeline, or suggesting that a C permit cannot lapse, be downgraded or be revoked.
