# Settlement and C Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss settlement and C permit blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss C permits and settlement residence, including ordinary eligibility, fast-track routes, integration and language requirements, cantonal procedure, absence and preservation, family life planning, naturalisation planning, rights, stability, loss of status and comparison with L and B permits.

## Folder Structure

```text
settlement_and_c_permit/
  broad/
  core/
  issue_specific/
  route_specific/
  README.md
```

## Folder Purposes

### `core/`

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss C permit and settlement framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);  
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant;  
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;  
nationality-based settlement agreements, where relevant;  
ordinary C permit eligibility;  
accelerated or fast-track settlement routes;  
integration and language requirements;  
absence, lapse and preservation rules;  
cantonal procedure;  
downgrade, revocation and loss of status.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general C permit and settlement topics across multiple applicant profiles, nationalities and residence scenarios.

### `broad/`

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several settlement and C permit scenarios.

Examples include:

ordinary C permit eligibility;  
fast-track C permit routes;  
integration, language and compliance;  
cantonal practice and procedure;  
absence, lapse and preservation;  
rights, stability and limits;  
family life planning;  
naturalisation planning;  
L, B and C permit long-term planning comparison.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one C permit or settlement scenario.

### `route_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on a specific C permit route or applicant category.

Examples include:

EU / EFTA nationals;  
third-country nationals;  
spouses of Swiss citizens;  
spouses of C permit holders;  
children and young people;  
refugees;  
stateless persons;  
nationals benefiting from settlement agreements;  
applicants moving from B permit to C permit;  
applicants relying on accelerated integration.

Route-specific packs should identify the legal basis, applicant category, entitlement or discretion, eligibility conditions, procedure, evidence, risks and drafting cautions for the specific route.

### `issue_specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories within C permit and settlement practice.

Examples include:

social assistance history;  
debt enforcement or certificates of loss;  
criminal convictions;  
tax arrears;  
long absences from Switzerland;  
C permit preservation after relocation abroad;  
weak language evidence;  
failed or borderline integration;  
downgrade risk;  
revocation risk;  
cross-cantonal moves;  
family separation or divorce;  
children becoming adults before settlement;  
naturalisation risks after C permit grant.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the core, broad or route-specific packs provide.

## Naming Convention

Use clear, descriptive filenames beginning with:

`legal_authority_c_permit_`

For core framework packs, use the prefix:

`legal_authority_core_swiss_c_permit_`

Use the `legal_authority_fast_track_c_permit_`, `legal_authority_ordinary_c_permit_` or `legal_authority_l_b_c_permit_` prefixes where those better describe the pack.

Recommended examples:

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation, ordinances and treaties;  
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and relevant EU / EFTA materials where applicable;  
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal guidance;  
nationality-based settlement agreements or reciprocal arrangements, where relevant;  
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;  
official cantonal migration authority guidance;  
cantonal administrative practice, where evidenced;  
internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;  
existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;  
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;  
a table of legal propositions;  
primary legal texts;  
official guidance and administrative materials;  
key case law, where relevant;  
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;  
practical legal analysis;  
drafting cautions;  
source list;  
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;  
eligibility only;  
discretion;  
facilitation;  
guidance;  
procedure;  
evidential practice;  
cantonal practice;  
nationality-specific practice;  
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic grant. Do not describe accelerated settlement routes as available to all applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Important Drafting Cautions

A C permit should not be treated as automatic, unconditional or identical for all nationalities.

The analysis usually depends on:

applicant nationality;  
current permit type;  
length of lawful residence;  
continuity of residence;  
ordinary or accelerated eligibility;  
applicable settlement agreement;  
integration evidence;  
language ability;  
economic participation;  
education or employment;  
financial independence;  
social assistance history;  
debt enforcement;  
criminal-record issues;  
tax compliance;  
public-order concerns;  
family composition;  
spouse or child status;  
canton of residence;  
cantonal procedure;  
absences from Switzerland;  
preservation requests;  
renewal or control procedure;  
downgrade risk;  
revocation risk;  
naturalisation objectives.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, treat C permit grant as automatic, imply that all nationalities follow the same route or timeline, or suggest that C permit status cannot lapse, be downgraded or be revoked.
