# Settlement and C Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss settlement and C permit blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss settlement, permanent residence and C permit applications, including ordinary C permit eligibility, fast-track routes, long-term planning, absences, lapse and preservation, integration, language, family-life considerations, cantonal practice and procedure.

## Folder Structure

settlement_and_c_permit/
  broad/
  core/
  issue_specific/
  route_specific/
  README.md

## Folder Purposes

### core/

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss settlement and C permit framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant;
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;
ordinary and early C permit eligibility;
residence continuity and interruption;
integration, language and compliance requirements;
cantonal competence and practice-sensitive decision-making.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general settlement and C permit topics across multiple applicant categories and routes.

### broad/

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several settlement and C permit routes.

Examples include:

ordinary C permit eligibility;
fast-track C permit routes;
C permit absences, lapse and preservation;
cantonal practice and procedure;
integration, language and compliance;
rights, stability and limits of C permit status;
family-life planning and settlement timing;
naturalisation planning after or alongside C permit acquisition;
long-term planning comparisons between L, B and C permits.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one route or applicant category.

### route_specific/

Use this folder for packs focused on a specific settlement route, applicant category or immigration pathway.

Examples include:

ordinary C permit applications;
early or fast-track C permit applications;
C permit eligibility following long-term B permit residence;
settlement planning for EU / EFTA nationals;
settlement planning for non-EU / EFTA nationals;
settlement planning for spouses, children or family members;
transitions from L permit or B permit status toward C permit eligibility.

Route-specific packs should identify the legal basis, residence route, eligibility conditions, integration requirements, procedure, evidence, risks and drafting cautions for the specific applicant category.

### issue_specific/

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories within settlement and C permit work.

Examples include:

long absences from Switzerland;
risk of C permit lapse;
preservation or retention of C permit status during absence;
insufficient language evidence;
integration concerns;
social assistance or debt enforcement issues;
criminality, compliance or suitability concerns;
interrupted residence;
cantonal discretion or inconsistent cantonal practice;
family-life consequences of settlement refusal or delay.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the general route or broad thematic pack provides.

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation, ordinances and treaties;
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal guidance;
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;
Official cantonal migration authority guidance;
Internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;
Existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
cantonal practice notes where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law. Do not describe C permit eligibility as automatic where the legal framework leaves room for conditions, assessment or cantonal procedure.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

settlement permit / C permit;
permanent residence, where useful for public-facing explanation;
LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act;
OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment;
AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons, where relevant;
SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives;
ordinary C permit;
early C permit or fast-track C permit, where legally appropriate;
lapse, preservation or retention of C permit status, where relevant.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Settlement and C permit applications should not be treated as a single uniform route.

The analysis usually depends on:

nationality;
permit history;
length and continuity of residence;
applicable legal basis;
whether the person is applying under ordinary or early settlement provisions;
whether any treaty or reciprocal arrangement is relevant;
absences from Switzerland;
integration record;
language evidence;
social assistance, debt enforcement or compliance issues;
criminality or public-order concerns;
family circumstances;
cantonal procedure and evidential expectations;
whether C permit status has already been granted and may lapse, be retained or be downgraded.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, flatten route-specific conditions or imply that settlement follows automatically after a fixed number of years.
