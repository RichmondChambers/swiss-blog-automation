# Core Swiss C Permit Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss settlement and C permit blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss C permit framework before moving into broad, route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

### `legal_authority_core_swiss_c_permit_framework_pack.md`

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss C permits and settlement residence, including ordinary eligibility, accelerated or fast-track routes, integration requirements, nationality-based distinctions, permit stability, absence and lapse rules, family life planning, naturalisation planning, cantonal procedure and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss C permit and settlement framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple C permit scenarios, including:

ordinary C permit eligibility; fast-track C permit routes; C permits for EU / EFTA nationals; C permits for third-country nationals; C permits for spouses and family members; integration and language requirements; good conduct and compliance; financial independence and social assistance risk; absence from Switzerland; preservation of C permit status; downgrade or revocation risk; cantonal procedure; family life planning; comparison with L and B permits; naturalisation planning.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core Swiss C permit pack should explain the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant; SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; nationality-based settlement agreements, where relevant; cantonal migration authority practice and procedure; ordinary settlement eligibility; accelerated or fast-track settlement eligibility; integration criteria; language requirements; compliance and public-order conditions; financial independence and social assistance risk; absence, lapse and preservation rules; family residence considerations; C permit rights, stability and limits; downgrade, revocation and loss of status; naturalisation planning.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the Swiss C permit framework; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; cantonal practice; nationality-specific practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe eligibility as automatic grant. Do not describe accelerated settlement routes as available to all applicants. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core Swiss C permit authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

applicant nationality; current permit type; length of lawful residence; continuity of residence; ordinary or accelerated eligibility; applicable bilateral or settlement agreement; integration; language ability; economic participation or education; financial independence; social assistance history; debt enforcement or criminal-record issues; tax compliance; family composition; spouse or child status; residence interruptions; absences from Switzerland; preservation requests; canton of residence; cantonal evidential expectations; renewal and control procedure; downgrade risk; revocation risk; naturalisation objectives.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several settlement and C permit scenarios, such as ordinary eligibility, fast-track routes, integration and language, cantonal practice, absence and preservation, rights and limits, family life planning, naturalisation planning and comparison with L or B permits.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific C permit route or applicant category, such as EU / EFTA nationals, third-country nationals, spouses of Swiss citizens, spouses of C permit holders, children, refugees, stateless persons or nationals benefiting from settlement agreements.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as social assistance history, debt enforcement, criminal convictions, long absences, weak language evidence, failed integration, downgrade risk, revocation risk, cross-cantonal moves or preservation after relocation abroad.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

C permit; Swiss C permit; settlement permit; permanent residence; settled residence; ordinary C permit; fast-track C permit; accelerated C permit; early C permit; integration requirements; language requirement; financial independence; social assistance risk; public order; good conduct; absence from Switzerland; C permit lapse; preservation of C permit; family life planning; naturalisation planning; L permit; B permit; residence permit; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; AFMP / FZA for the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

A C permit should not be described as unconditional or incapable of being lost.

The analysis usually depends on the applicant’s nationality, current permit type, residence history, applicable legal basis, ordinary or accelerated route, integration evidence, language ability, financial independence, compliance history, canton, absences from Switzerland, family circumstances, renewal procedure and long-term objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating C permit grant as automatic, implying that all nationalities follow the same timeline, or flattening canton-specific evidence and procedure into uniform Swiss law.
