# Swiss legal authority pack on refusals and weak points in senior and specialist hire work permit cases

Internal authority pack for knowledge/legalauthorities/workpermits/routespecific/seniorspecialisthires/

## Executive legal summary

Swiss law does not create a standalone "senior hire" or "specialist hire" route for ordinary third-country work permit cases. The operative legal structure is cumulative: salaried admission requires that the employment serve the interests of the economy as a whole, that an employer file the application, and that the conditions in Arts. 20-25 FNIA/AIG be met. "Managers, specialists and other qualified workers" is the personal-qualification gate in Art. 23, not a self-sufficient approval category. SEM's non-EU/EFTA guidance presents the same cumulative framework. 1

For internal content work, most weak Swiss senior/specialist files should be analysed as either a **substantive** **failure** to satisfy one of those cumulative conditions, or an **evidential failure** to prove it. The recurring weak points in official materials are: inadequate proof of labour-market priority; insufficiently demonstrated qualifications; a mismatch between the claimed role and the candidate's background; salary that does not fit local, occupational and sector norms; roledescriptions that exclude Swiss/EU/EFTA candidates through irrelevant criteria; and weak employer material on business need, projects, staffing and reasons for recruitment. Cantonal practice confirms that authorities expect a signed contract, CV, diplomas, work certificates, recruitment evidence and an employer explanation, often with additional staffing and business information. 2

Two narrow relaxation mechanisms matter, but neither eliminates the need for proof. First, where a role falls within a demonstrable structural labour shortage, SEM allows eased proof of priority and, in some fields, eased proof of qualifications; even then, cantons may still ask for special evidence, and sector-specific rules remain applicable. Secondly, intra-group transfer under Art. 46 VZAE is a narrow exception aimed at higher management, highly qualified experts and indispensable specialists; SEM exporessly warns that project staff may not be used for general local tasks and must be justified by specific project know-how. 3

Unsupported "commercial urgency" is usually not a free-standing refusal ground in the legislation. In practice it fails because, without ddocuments, it does not prove overall economic interest, does not justify an exception from ordinary admission requirements, and does not cure weak recruitment or qualification evidence. Official materials are also relatively thin on "re-filing after refusal": federal appeal and reconsideration rules are clear, but there is no single officiaI nationwide doctrine treating a re-file as a special remedy. A materially improved neww filing is therefore best treated as a fresh ordinary application, not as a substitute for appeal. 4

## Structural overview of refusals and weak points

A Swiss authority is unlikely to be persuaded by seniority stated only in titles. SEM states that the relevant qualification may consist of a university degree, a university of applied sciences diploma, special specialist training plus several years' experience, additional vocational training, or extraordinary indispensable specialist knowledge. SEM also says that the existence of the required qualification can sometimes be inferred from the function itself, such as founders or company leaders of labour-market-significant businesses; but that is still an evidential exercise, not a presumption. In intra-company transfer cases, SEM describes the protected class as executive staff with broad operational responsibility and corresponding decision-making powers, not merely anyone carrying a management-sounding title. 5

A "specialist" claim fails where the specialism is generic, transferable to the local market, or described only at a high level. SEM's guidance on project workers and service providers is particularly important: the inflowv of foreign labour is to be limited to specialists whose **specific** **project** **know-how** means they cannot be replaced by local or European workers, and authorities must prevent project staff from carrying out general IT tasks that compete wvith local labour. That guidance goes directly to weakly evidenced specialism, over-broad role descriptions and role over-tailoring around one candidate. 6

Candidate-role mismatch is an orthodox weakness, not an exotic one. Zurich'sofficial guidance states that professional qualifications must correspond to the new activity to be performed, and lists the expected proof: education records, work certificates, CV and identity material. Geneva likewise requires CV, diplomas and work certificates, together with an employer letter explaining the recruitment. A file that claims seniority or technical indispensability but presents a background only loosely connected to the actual Swiss post is weak on Art. 23 grounds even if the candidate is accomplished in the abstract. 7

Recruitment evidence remains central. SEM's 2026 handbook says the priority principle applies in principle in every case and every labour-market situation; where shortage is not objectively shown, the employer must prove search efforts that are timely, genuinely directed at filling the post with priority workers, and not merely formal. SEM adds that contact with third-country nationals should occur only after those efforts have failed, and that priority candidates must not be screened out by professionally irrelevant criteria such as non-essential language skills, foreign stays or niche knowledge with only a slight connection to the post. Geneva's official practice is stricter still: the vacancy should be reported to the cantonal employment office as early as possible, searches should be made in Switzerland and the EU/EFTA, and the employer must show that a locally available worker cannot reasonably be trained in time. 8

Salary inconsistency can damage a case in two ways. First, it can amount to a direct Art. 22 problem if the offered pay and conditions are not local, occupational and sector-standard. Secondly,it can undercut the claim that the role is genuinely senior or specialist if the remuneration looks inconsistent with the asserted level of responsibility or specialism. SEMI's handbook requires cantons to compare the offer to legal rules, actual pay for comparable work in the same business and sector, and statistical salary data, and it treats current wage data as a mminimum in urban settings. Only salary elements with a contractual entitlement may generally be counted; expenses are not salary, and stock or participation elements count only on restrictive conditions. Authorities also require a written, binding contract with the essential employment terms. 9

Weak employer justification is a recurring evidential problem. SEM's public-interest guidance says the system is intended to align admission with the long-term overall economic interest of Switzerland, not with structural preservation through low-poaid labour or with narrow private interests. Geneva operationalises this by demanding an employer letter describing the company's situation, projects and reasons for the hire, coupled with staff-composition information; it may also condition renewal or extension on the achievement of objectives announced inthe application. St Gallen expressly asks for an employer justification of the need for the foreign worker. Unsupported statements that a hire is "commercially critical" or "urgent" are therefore weak unless tied to evidence. 10

Incomplete or inconsistent documents are often better understood as a proof failure than as a free-standing legal rule. SEM and cantonal materials repeatedly require a signed contract, identity documents, CV, diplomas, work certificates and recruitment evidence, and cantonal practice may add translations, employer motivation letters, staffing data, a criminal-record extract or language requirements for submissions. If titles, dates, salaries, job descriptions and recruitment narratives do not align across forms, contracts and supporting evidence, the authority has little basis to accept the claimed seniority, specialism or business necessity. 11

### Primary legal texts and official guidance

**Primary** **legal texts.** The main statutory anchors are Arts. 18, 21, 22, 23 and 30 FNIA/AIG. Art. 18provides the cumulative architecture for salaried employment; Art. 21 sets the priority rule; Art. 22governs local salary and employment conditions and distinguishes salary from reimbursed expenses; Art. 23 restricts admission to managers, specialists and other qualified workers; and Art. 30 lists only specific derogations from the ordinary admission rules. On the ordinance side, Art. 46 VZAE is the narrow rule for intra-company transfer, and Art. 22a VZAE governs the duration of expense reimbursement in long-term postings. 30

<!-- 5 -->

**Official guidance** **and** **administrative** **materials.** The most important operational authority is SEM's handbook chapter on gainful employment, updated to 1 January 2026. It gives the working framework on overall economic interest, priority, shortage-occupation easements, salary assessment and personal qualifications. The public SEM page on labour-market admission for non-EU/EFTA nationals provides the same logic in a shorter format. SEM's annex on local salary and working conditions is particularly useful on what may count as salary in posting situations, and its annex on project staff and service providers is the strongest official text against generic "specialist" claims in project-based and IT-heavy files. SECO's 2023 indicator system is relevant because SEM expressly uses SECO indicators when identifying structural shortage occupations. 31

### Cantonal practice notes and case law

**Cantonal practice** **notes.** Zurich is useful on the alignment point: it states that thecandidate's professional qualifications must match the new activity, requires the core qualification documents, and says that in shortage occupations the applicant must still prove at least a vocational qualification or usually five years' experience; it also notes that ddocuments should be filed in German or English. Geneva is the most explicit canton in the reviewed material on weak-file architecture: it requires early vacancy notification to the employment office, real searches in Switzerland and the EU/EFTA, proof that a local worker cannot reasonably be trained in time, CV/diplomas/work certificates, an employer motivation letter describing the situation and projects, detailed recruitment outcomes, staff-composition data and a signed contract. Geneva also warns that the canton may issue a different permit from the one requested and may condition renewal on the objectives announced by the employer. Luzern frames the conditionsas cumulative and emphasises both local salary compliance and the signed contract. St Gallen is notable for asking not only for recruitment proof and employer justification, but also for a criminal-record extract and translations where documents are not in German. Ticino's official labour-market page restates the core examination points as quotas, priority, salary/ working conditions and qualifications. 32

**Case** **law** **notes.** Case law is most useful here as a confirmation of themes already embedded in SEM guidance, rather than as a separate doctrinal body. The published decision BVGE 2011/1, concerning C-2638/2010, is the key reported authority identified in the research: its headnote states that third-country admission is intended to be restrictive, subordinated to a longer-term overall economic interest, and tied to requirements on search efforts under Arts. 21 and 23 AuG/AIG. SEM's 2026handbook also expressly relies on a line of Federal Administrative Court cases for three points:the economic-interest test is not meant to support particular private interests or low-wage structure preservation; the priority principle applies in principle in every labour-market situation; and authorities may expect genuine recruitment and training efforts rather than formal compliance. Because the handbook itself is the clearest reliable digest, that is the safest way to use these cases in internal content. 33

### Practical legal analysis

The most important analytical division is between **legal error** **and** **evidence** **weakness.** A legal error exists where the authority applies the wrong rule, misunderstands the scope of an exception, or misstates a procedural remedy. An evidence weakness exists where the correct rule is applied, but the file does not prove overall economic interest, priority, salary compliance, role-candidate fit or genuine seniority/specialism. The official Swiss materials reviewed are heavily proof-focused, which is why most "senior/specialist refusal" problems are better framed as evidential failures than as doctrinal disputes. 34

<!-- 6 -->

For file rebuilding, the safest method is to map every document to a statutory or guidance issue.For **overall economic interest,** the employer should provide a dated justification tying the role to conncrete Swiss business activity, projects, customers, delivery obligations, expected economic contribution and why the post is needed in Switzerland rather than elsewhere; Geneva's requirement for a motivation letter describing the company's situation and projects is a useful template for the type of material authorities expect. For **priority,** the rebuilt file should contain a dated recruitment chronology, vacancy notices, RAV/OCE evidence, Swiss and EU/EFTA advertising, agency searches, candidate lists, rejection reasons linked to job-relevant criteria only, and, where relevant, an explanation of why local upskilling was not realistic in time. For **salary,** the file should include the signed contract, salary benchmark outputs and a clean separation between salary, allowances and reimbursed expenses. For **qualifications,** it should include CV, diplomas, work certificates, translations and a role-to-candidate matrix showing exactly how each required competence is proven. These individual pieces are not all listed in one federal master checklist, but each is directly responsive to an issue identified in SEM or cantonal guidance. 35

If the case is framed around **seniority,** the rebuilt evidence should go beyond titles and include reporting lines, scope of authority, budget responsibility, headcount responsibility,board or management reporting, annd, if relevant, proof that the role is part of a wider group-management structure. That recommendation is an inference from the legal materials rather than a statutory checklist, but it matches SEM's emphasis on executive function, broad operational responsibility and decision-making powers in intra-group contexts, and on the possibility of inferring qualification from the actual function of founders or business leaders. Without that kind of operational evidence, a "head of", "director" or "VP" label is often thin. 36

If the case is framed around **specialism,** the rebuilt file should distinguish the job from general market work. The strongest materials are those showing project-specific knowledge, proprietary systems knowledge, unusual certifications, specialised technical history, regulated-profession permissions where relevant, and a short narrative explaining why those competences are not meaningfully available in the Swiss/EU/EFTA labour pool. This is especially important in project, posting and IT-related cases because SEM explicitly says that general IT tasks should not be filled through foreign project staff and that only specialists with specific project know-how should be admitted on that logic. 6

As to **appeal,** **reconsideration** **and** **re-filing,** the black-letter federal position is relatively clear only for federal administrative remedies. Any ruling is in principle appealable; the ordinary appeal period is 30days; and the lower authority may reconsider the contested ruling before filing its formal response. Revision is separately regulated and is a limited tool, not a general opportunity to improve a weak merits file. Official sources reviewed do not provide a similarlyclear nationwide doctrine on re-filing after a refusal in this work-permit context. Accordingly, for internal drafting purposes, are-file should be characterised conservatively as an ordinary fresh application with improved evidence and current facts, not as a defined appellate remedy. 27

One final analytical point matters for blog and system drafting: **unsupported** **urgency** **is** **usually** **an** **evidence problem, not an independent legal category.** Swiss law recognises overall economic interest and certain narrow derogations; it does not recognise a free-floating rule that "commercial urgency" by itself justifies admission of an otherwise weak senior/specialist file.So urgency should only be stated where the underlying documents prove it, and where it is linked to the real statutory

### Drafting cautions and source list

**Drafting** **cautions.** Internal content on refusals should avoid treating "senior", "specialist", "commercially critical" or "shortage occupation" as approval shortcuts. It should not suggest that a high title proves seniority, that a shortage occupation removes the need for recruitment evidence entirely, that salary alone proves managerial or specialist status, or that intra-company transfer waives all ordinary admission requirements. It should also avoid describing "commercial urgency" as a free-standing exception, and should distinguish carefully between a statute-based failure, a narrow exception, and a file that simply lacks proof. Where discussing appeals and re-filing, the content should state expressly that federal remedies are defined, but that reviewed official materials are much thinner on a general Swiss re-filing doctrine for these cases. 38
