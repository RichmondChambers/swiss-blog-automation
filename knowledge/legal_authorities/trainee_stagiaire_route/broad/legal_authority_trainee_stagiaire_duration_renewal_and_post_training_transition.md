# Swiss trainee and stagiaire duration, renewal and post-training transition authority pack

# Executive legal summary

In Switzerland, the trainee or stagiaire route is not a free-standing long-term work category. It is an exception route within the Federal Act on Foreign Nationals and Integration and the Ordinance on Admission,Residence and Employment, used to facilitate international exchange and continuing professional training under bilateral trainee agreements. The legal core is FNIA Article 30 paragraph 1letter gJ and OASA Article 42; the current SEM operational materials describe the route as work experience for people who have completed training and want to improve professional and language skills in Switzerland. 1

The route-specific duration ceiling on the Swiss side is 18 **months overall.** OASA Article 42 allows SEM to issue trainee authorisations for stays of up to 18 months and to extend them only within that maximum. SEM's current instructions say the work permit is granted for a maximum of 18 months,that several stays are possible only if their combined duration does not exceed 18 months, and that any extension is subject to approval on justified grounds only. 2

Swiss law and guidance treat the route as **temporary, training-led, and** **purpose-bound.** The trainee must work in the profession in which they were trained; the employment contract must include an integrated training programme; part-time work and self-employment are not permitted; staffleasing is, as a matter of principle, not allowed; and SEM guidance says trainees should not exceed 5% of the employer's workforce if more than one is engaged. These features are all strong indicators that the route is not meant to solve ordinary staffing needs. 3

Renewal and repeat use are tightly constrained. OASA Article 56 says a foreign national mnay receive a short-stay authorisation for a stagiaire stay only once, with exceptions only in justified individual cases. OASA Article 57 bars immediate chaining of different short-stay authorisation categories, including stagiaire authorisations, and requires at least two months abroad between listed short-stay permits. SEM's directives add that the purpose of a continuing-training stay should be achieved within the authorised period, that renewal for trainees is only justified in rare exceptions, and that repeating the same stage or an analogous function does not justify a further trainee authorisation. 4

A move from trainee status into ordinary Swiss employment is therefore **not** an "extension" of the trainee route. If the purpose of stay changes, OASA Article 54 requires a **new** **permit.** Any move to local hire must then satisfy the ordinary admission rules for third-country workers, including overall economic interest, quota compliance where applicable, priority for workers in Switzerland and the EU/ EFTA labour market, customary salary and working conditions, and personal qualifications. A further general FNIA rule also matters: a person lawfully admitted for a temporary stay who then applies for a longer stay must usually wait for the decision abroad, unless the admission requirements are clearly fulfilled and the canton allows the person to remain during the procedure. 5

For internal drafting, the safest position is this**: the trainee / stagiaire route is a temporary training** **mechanism capped at 18 months, not a stepping-stone that can safely be presented as a routine** **route** **to** **local** **hire.** Any post-training employment outcome must be framed as a separate, fully discretionary ordinary-permit question. That conclusion is not stated in one sentence in the legislation, but it follows directly from the route's purpose limitation, the 18-month cap, the repeat-stay restrictions, and the anti-circumvention rules on successive short stays. 6

## Structural overview of duration, renewal, and post-training transition

The federal structure has four layers. First, FNIA Article 30 paragraph 1 letterg provides the statutory basis for derogations from the ordinary admission requirements in order to facilitate international exchange and continuing professional education and training. Secondly, OASA Article 42 gives the specific trainee rule and ties procedure and permit issuance to trainee agreements and intergovernmental administrative arrangements. Thirdly, bilateral agreements set country coverage, age limits and, in some cases, quotas or agreement-specific programme features. Fourthly,SEM guidance and forms operationalise thhe route in practice, including required evidence, extension handling and job-change control.7

There is an important source distinction on country coverage. The current SEM "Young professionals" instructions for entry to Switzerland listthe presently operational contracting countries as Argentina, Australia, Canada, Chile, Indonesia, Japan, Monaco, New Zealand, Philippines, Russia, San Marino, South Africa, Tunisia, Ukraine and the USA, and they note that EU/EFTA nationals no longer need a permit to work in Switzerland because of free movement. SEM's wider 2026 FNIA directives still list a broader historical field of 35 trainee agreements, but explain that theagreements with EU/EFTA states are practically no longer used after the end of transitional restrictions. 8

The duration analysis must keep **route-specific limits** distinct from **general permit-validity rules.** OASA Article 42 caps trainee stays at 18 months and allows extension only within that cap. Separately, FNIA Article 32 says a short-stay permit is for limited stays of up to one year and mnay be extended, while FNIA Article 33 governs residence permits for stays of more than one year. OASA Article 58 says an initial residence permit is valid for one year and can in general be prolonged by two years. None of those general validity mechanics overrides the trainee route's own 18-month ceiling. 9

Evidence of duration must be training-led, not vacancy-led. SEM's instructions require a written employment contract with an integrated training programme. Thestandard SEM contract includes a dedicated "programme of training during the stay" for linguistic and professional improvement, and the application form requires the duration of employment, a training programme, diplomas, CV and work certificates. The role description, schedule, courses and other training content therefore carry real legal weight in explaining why the proposed duration is necessary. 10

The Swiss-side extension logic is narrow. SEM states that an extension request must be made about two months before expiry, must explain the reason for extension, and must include a new employment contract with a training programme. The directives state that extensions are possible within the 18-month maximum and are not charged to the agreement contingents, but they also emphasise that the training purpose should normally be completed within the original authorised period and that repeated or analogous stages do not justify a further trainee authorisation. 11

Any post-training move into ordinary Swiss employment is structurally different. OASA Article 54 says a new permit is required if the purpose of stay changes. At that point the ordinary third-country labour-admission rules re-enter the case: overall economic interest, quotas, priority for workers with access to the Swiss and EU/EFTA labouur market, customary salary and conditions, and qualification requirements. In some cases, the general FNIA rule on applications for a longer stay after lawful temporary stay may also require the applicant to wait abroad for the decision unless the canton allows them to remain. 5

#### Primary legal texts

The key statutory base is the FNIA. Article 30 paragraph 1 letter g allows derogations from the ordinary admission requirements in order to facilitate international economic, scientific and cultural exchange and continuing professional education and training. Article 32 deals with short-stay permits as purpose-specific permits for limited stays of up to one year, extendable for good cause; Article 33 deals with residence permits for stays of more than one year and confirms that they too are purpose-specific. Article 17 is relevant to post-training transitions because it says a person lawfully admitted for a temporary stay who then applies for a longer stay must, in principle, wait abroad for the decision unless the canton allows them to remain because the requirements are clearly fulfilled.Articles 18 and 20-23then set the ordinary salaried-employment rules for any later move into normal employment. 25

The route-specific ordinance is OASA Article 42. It says trainee procedure and permit issuance follow trainee agreements and intergovernmental administrative arrangements; SEM may grant trainee permits for stays of up to 18 months; and trainee permits may be extended only within that 18-month maximum. Since 1 January 2025, Article 42 paragraph 2bis also creates a quota-fallback mechanism for agreements without their own quota figures, linked to Annex 1 number 9. OASA Article 54 says a new permit is needed where the purpose of stay changes. OASA Articles 56 and 57 then impose the important anti-recycling rules on repeat short stays and on chaining categories of short-stay permit. OASA Article 58, by contrast, is a general validity rule for residence permits and should not be confused with the trainee route's own maximum duration. 26

### Official guidance and administrative materials

SEM's current "Young professionals" page and the November 2025 instructions are the most important operational authorities. They identify the current active contracting countries for entry into Swwitzerland, confirm that the route is for people who have completed training, say remuneration must meet local and sectoral standards at roughly entry level, and state that work permits are granted for a maximum of 18 months. The same instructions also say that several stays are possible only if the overall total does not exceed 18 months, that extensions need justified grounds, and that job changes are exceptional and subject to approval. 28

The SEM standard employment agreement and application form are especially useful where the authority pack needs to explain evidential expectations. The contract requires a detailed programme of training during the stay aimed at improving linguistic and professional skills, and states a maximum of 18 months. The application form asks for the duration of employment, education history, professional experience and language skills, and requires enclosures including the employment contract with training programme, diploma copies, CV, work certificates and passport copy.Those forms support a conservative drafting position that the proposed duration must be justified by a concrete training plan, not by a generic labour need. 29

SEMM's 2026 FNIA directives are the strongest source for deeper administrative practice. They state that trainees are persons aged 18 to 30 or 35 who, after completing vocational training, come for a limited period of up to 18 months to improve professional and linguistic knowledge; the stage must be in the learned profession; salary must match local and sectoral norms; trainee numbers may not exceed 5% of staff; extension requests go directly to SEM; job changes require written justification; a profession change is impossible; and repeated same-stage or analogous-function renewals are not justified. They also distinguish the broader historical universe of trainee treaties from the current free-movement position for EU/EFTA nationals. 30

The federal SME portal adds a concise but useful confirmation: the "internship permit" is for a maximum of 18 months and is only for stays of young professionals completing in-house training under, among other things, the trainee agreement framework. That short statement is not as detailed as SEM's own materials, but it reinforces the character of the route as a temporary training authorisation rather than an ordinary work permit. 31

### Cantonal practice notes

The clearest canton-level practice note located was Zurich's instruction on employment-based stay for third-country nationals. Zurich restates thatthe stagiaire procedure and permit issuance follow the intergovernmental arrangements, that SEM may issue trainee permits for stays of up to 18 months, and that any extension application must be filed with SEM. This is useful because it shows canton-level practice deferring the substantive extension decision to SEM rather than treating extension as a routine local renewal. 32

Zurich's instruction is also helpful on anti-chaining practice. It repeats the Article 56 and 57 rules, confirms that one may normally obtain only one stagiaire short-stay permit and must spend at least two months abroad between listed short-stay permits, and explains a limited concession under which a four-month permit may in some cases be followed immediately by an Article 42 trainee permit. Crucially, however, the earlier four months are counted against the later permit's total duration, so the second permit consumes the first rather than adding to it. 33

### Practical legal analysis

The first practical question is always whether the proposed period is genuinely the period needed to complete the training objective. Swiss materials do not just ask for a job contract; they ask for an employment contract with an integrated training programme, including the type of training, job description, schedule and courses. Where the host cannot explain why the learning objective needs the proposed length,or where the contract reads like ordinary productive employment with only token training language, the case is mnaterially weaker. 10

The second practical question is whether an "extension" is really an extension of training, or instead evidence of ordinary labour demand. SEM's own directives are the key authority here: the purpose of the stay should be achieved within the authorised period; renewal for trainee or continuing-training stays should be rare; and repeating the same stage or an analogous function does not justify a further authorisation. As a result, anapplication that says, in substance, "the employer still needs this person doing the same work" is much weaker than one that demonstrates a specific unfinished training module, documented rotation, or a clearly higher-level new training phase still within the same 18-month cap. 35

The same logic explains why changes in host, role or employer are sensitive. SEM instructions treat job change as exceptional and require a new training contract; the directives add that a profession change is impossible because the stage must be undertaken in the learned profession. If the training plan changes substantially, the authority can fairly ask whether the original trainee purpose still exists or whether the application has drifted into a different labour-market need. 16

Post-training employment with the same Swiss employer should therefore be analysed as a **fresh** **ordinary-permit** **problem,** not as a continuation of the trainee case. OASA Article 54 requires a new permit if the purpose changes, and the ordinary salaried-employment rules then apply in full. For most third-country nationals, that means the employer must be able to satisfy overall economic interest, quota availability where relevant, labour-market priority, wage compliance and qualification requirements. A further obstacle may arise from the FNIA's general rule that a person admitted for a temporary stay who later applies for a longer stay should normally wait abroad for the decision unless the canton lets them remmain during the process because the conditions are clearly fulfilled. 5

A different post-training route is possible only if the facts independently fit that route. For example, FNIA Article 30 paragraph 1 letter h covers intra-group transfers of senior management and essential specialists, and SEM's directives explain that OASA Article 46 creates a simplified recruitment procedure for qualifying international-company transfers. But that is a separate route with separate legal tests; it is not a doctrinal "conversion" of trainee status. 36

The safest internal analytical rule is therefore this: if **the** **extension** **or** **post-training** **request** **looks like** **the host wants to keep filling the same business function, assumetrainee-route risk is high.** That is an inference, but it is a conservative one and it is firmly supported by the official anti-circumvention rules, the one-time short-stay logic, the two-month abroad requirement for chained short stays, the centrality of the training programme, and the SEM directive against repeating the same or analogous stage. 37

### Drafting cautions and source list

Internal drafting should stay deliberately conservative. The safest formulations are: **"maximum** **18months overall", "extension only within the 18-month cap and only on justified grounds",** **"temporary training in the learned profession", and "post-training employment requires a fresh** **ordinary-permnit** **assessment".** Avoid wording that suggests the route is a long-term employment pathway, that renewal is expected, or that a trainee can simply "convert" tolocal hire without a new permit and a new legal basis. Also avoid conflating the route's own duration ceiling with the general validity rules for L or B permits. Where country-specific agreement rules or quota arrangements matter, say so expressly and attach them to the specific agreement rather than generalising. 38
