# Core Lump Sum Taxation Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss lump sum taxation blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss lump sum taxation residence framework before moving into broad, route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

### `legal_authority_core_swiss_lump_sum_taxation_residence_framework.md`

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss lump sum taxation residence, including the main tax and immigration rules, expenditure-based taxation conditions, residence permit basis, canton selection, no-gainful-activity restrictions, physical presence, family residence, renewal risks and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss lump sum taxation residence framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple lump sum taxation residence scenarios, including:

HNWI residence under lump sum taxation; taxation according to expenditure; third-country national applicants; EU / EFTA applicants where relevant; retired or financially independent applicants; family relocation; spouses and dependent children; canton selection; tax ruling or tax agreement procedure; D-visa and entry sequencing where relevant; no gainful activity in Switzerland; foreign business ownership; board roles; physical presence and tax domicile; residence permit renewal; loss of status; C permit and naturalisation planning.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core lump sum taxation pack should explain the relationship between:

the Federal Act on Direct Federal Taxation (DBG / LIFD); the Federal Act on Tax Harmonisation (StHG / LHID); cantonal tax legislation and practice; tax ruling or tax agreement procedure; the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; federal and cantonal case law where relevant; cantonal migration authority practice and procedure; immigration discretion; tax domicile and residence substance; expenditure-based taxation; no-gainful-activity restrictions; entry clearance, D visa, cantonal authorisation and residence permit issue.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the Swiss lump sum taxation residence route; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; tax ruling practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe tax eligibility as a right to residence. Do not describe a cantonal tax agreement as an immigration approval. Do not describe discretionary residence routes as entitlements. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core lump sum taxation authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

applicant nationality; prior Swiss residence history; current tax residence; whether the applicant is taking up Swiss residence for the first time or after a qualifying absence; tax eligibility; immigration admissibility; canton availability; cantonal tax practice; cantonal migration practice; federal approval where relevant; expenditure base; control calculation; no gainful activity in Switzerland; foreign business ownership; board roles; passive investment; physical presence; tax domicile; centre of life; family composition; housing; financial resources; health insurance; entry clearance and visa procedure; permit renewal; loss of lump sum taxation status; loss or non-renewal of residence status; C permit and naturalisation implications.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several lump sum taxation residence scenarios, such as tax eligibility, expenditure minimums, immigration discretion, canton selection, evidence, physical presence, family residence, compliance, renewal or long-term planning.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific applicant category or residence scenario, such as third-country HNWI applicants, EU / EFTA applicants, retired applicants, family relocation cases or applicants moving from a specific country.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as foreign board roles, partial Swiss activity, weak residence substance, complex family structures, prior Swiss residence, canton abolition risk, dual tax residence, renewal after business changes or naturalisation while taxed on expenditure.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

lump sum taxation; taxation according to expenditure; expenditure-based taxation; Swiss lump sum taxation; HNWI residence; residence permit; B permit; C permit; naturalisation; no gainful activity in Switzerland; foreign business activity; board roles; tax domicile; centre of life; physical presence; canton selection; cantonal tax ruling; tax agreement; expenditure minimum; control calculation; DBG / LIFD for the Federal Act on Direct Federal Taxation; StHG / LHID for the Federal Act on Tax Harmonisation; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives; D visa, where relevant to entry and sequencing.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss tax and immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Lump sum taxation should not be treated as a residence-by-investment programme.

The analysis usually depends on the applicant’s nationality, prior Swiss residence history, canton, tax ruling practice, immigration discretion, business activities, family composition, physical presence, housing, financial resources, evidence, entry procedure, renewal history and long-term objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, implying that a tax agreement alone guarantees a Swiss residence permit, or flattening canton-specific conditions into uniform Swiss law.
