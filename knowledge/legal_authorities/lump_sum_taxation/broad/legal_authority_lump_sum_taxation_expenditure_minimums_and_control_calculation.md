# Swiss Lump-Sum Taxation Calculation Mechanics

## Executive legal summary

Swiss taxation according to expenditure, often called lump-sum taxation, is not a negotiated flat fee and not a separate tax rate. It is a special method for determining the tax base. At federal level, the direct federal tax remains an income tax on individuals, but for qualifying taxpayers the taxable base is measured by annual living expenditure rather than by ordinary worldwide income. The starting point is the taxpayer's annual worldwide living costs, includling the costs of persons maintained by the taxpayer, in Switzerland and abroad. Official federal guidance and the current federal forms show that this expenditure concept is broad: it covers housing, staff,food, clothing, education, travel, leisure, support payments, the upkeep of high-value assets such as boats or aircraft, and even the Swiss tax burden itself when modelling annual living costs. The amount ultimately taxed is then computed under the ordinary tariff, not under a bespoke preferential rate. 1

The federal base is subject to hard statutory floors. For a taxpayer with their own household, the floor includes the greater of the actual annual expenditure and seven times the annual gross rent or annual rental value of the Swiss home; for taxpayers living in a hotel or boarding arrangement, the comparator is three times the annual board-and-lodging price. In addition, the federal minimum amount under the expenditure rule is indexed for inflation. The official federal guidance for the 2025 return still uses CHF 434,700, while the Federal Tax Administration's 2025 circular letter on cold-progression adjustments states that the indexed amount under article 14 paragraph 3 letter a DFTA/LIFD rises to CHF 435,000 for tax year 2026.2

A further anti-avoidance layer is the control calculation. For direct federal tax, this is not a wealth tax; it is a comparison with the ordinary tax that would arise on specified gross income categories. The official federal materials require inclusion of gross income from Swiss immovable property, Swiss chattels, movable capital invested in Switzerland, intellectual property exploited in Switzerland, Swiss pensions and annuities, and foreign-source income for which relief under a Swiss double tax treaty is claimed. The federal forms and guidance make clear that these items are entered on a gross basis, and that deductions are tightly limited. In the control calculation, only property maintenance costs and the customary costs of managing taxable movable assets are deductible; debt interest, annuities and similar charges are not. Social deductions are also excluded. 3

The canton and commune layer is materially different and must be analysed separately. The federal direct tax contains no wealth tax for individuals, but many cantons either build a wealth-tax substitute directly into their expenditure-tax rules or compare the lump-sum result against ordinary cantonal income-and-wealth taxation on specified Swiss assets and treaty-relief income. The official canton materials reviewed for this pack show significant variation. Ticino currently fixes the minimum income-side amount at CHF 434,700, levies a substitute wealth tax on a wealth base equal to five times the retained expenditure base, and compares the result with ordinary income and wealth tax on listed Swiss assets and treaty-relief incomme. Wallis fixes the income-side minimum at CHF 250,000 by ordinance and computes wealth tax on at least four times the retained expenditure base. Geneva's official guidance explains that the cantonal and communal base may differ from the federal base, that a 10 per cent uplift is added to reflect wealth tax, and that the indexed Geneva cantonal/communal minimum is CHF 426,357 for 2026 before that 10 per cent uplift. 4

Treaty claims are one of the most important variables in real cases. As a rule, foreign tax credit relief is unavailable to individuals taxed according to expenditure unless they are within the modified form of lump-sum taxation that taxes all income from the relevant treaty state at the rate for total income. Current official Geneva guidance identifies Germany, Austria, Belgium, Italy, Norway, the United States and Canada as states that recognise residence status for forfait taxpayers under that modified approach. The current federal return form is aligned with that seven-state disclosure model. France is treated separately in current federal guidance: the 2025 federal instruction says that a person taxed on a lump-sum base measured by the rental value of the Swiss dwelling is not regarded as resident in Switzerland for French treaty benefits. For later drafting, treaty access should therefore be treated as state-specific and technically conditional, not assumed. 5

The compliance consequence is straightforward. There is no single permanent "forfait number" that safely answers future liability. The retained base has to be checked each tax year against actual expenditure, the rent or rental-value floor, and the control calculation; treaty claims can enlarge the taxable basket; and cantonal wealth-tax mechanics can shift the total burden materially. Any internal drafting that describes Swiss lump-sum taxation as a nominal annual fixed payment, or that ignores the control calculation and canton-specific wealth-tax layer, will be legally unreliable. 6

## Structural overview of calculation rules

## Expenditure-based tax base

The federal concept is a worldwide-lifestyle base. Official federal guidance describes the taxable base as the annual living costs incurred in Switzerland and abroad by the taxpayer and the persons maintained by them. The current federal supplementary budget sheet operationalises that concept with categories for housing, staff, education, leisure, support payments, upkeep of vehicles, boats, aircraft and animals, and the annual tax burden itself. This is the legal architecture behind the phrase "taxation according to expenditure": the tax base is measured by consumption and living standard, not by a negotiated lump sum. 7

## Federal minimum floors

The legal base is then tested against statutory or indexed minima. The official federal return instructions state that the retained federal amount must be at least the higghest of actual annual expenditure, the indexed federal minimum, seven times the annual rent or rental value for taxpayers with their own household, or three times the annual pension price for taxpayers without their own household. The 2025 form uses CHF 434,700; the Federal Tax Administration's 2025 circular letter increases the indexed article 14 amount to CHF 435,000 for tax year 2026. 8

### Control calculation

The control calculation is a mandatory comparison, not an optional treaty overlay. Official federal forms require disclosure of Swiss immovable income, Swiss-source movable-capital income, Swiss pensions, Swiss-source royalties and comparable rights, and treaty-relief foreign income. The guidance also states that these are gross items. Only limited costs may be deducted in the control calculation: property maintenance costs and the ordinary management costs of taxable movable assets; debt interest and similar deductions are excluded. The taxpayer may, however, refrain from claiming treaty benefits for all income from a treaty state if they wish to avoid bringing that state's income into the calculation. 9

<!-- 2 -->

### Canton and wealth layer

The federal direct tax analysis is incomplete on its own because cantonal and communal taxes often add wealth-tax mechanics. The EFD explains that the regime is implemented differently by canton. Official cantonal materials in this research set show three different models: Geneva adds a 10 per cent uplift to the retained cantonal/communal base to reflect wealth tax; Wallis taxes wealth on at least four times the retained expenditure base; Ticino substitutes a wealth tax on a base equal to five times the retained expenditure amount and also runs an income-and-wealth control calculation on listed Swiss assets and treaty-relief income. 10

### Annual recalculation and treaty-claim mechanics

Official federal guidance stresses that the taxable amount must be determined for each tax period.The current forms require annual reporting of the expenditure budget and of the specific income categories relevant to the control calculation. Where relief from certain treaty states is claimed, the forms require separate disclosures and, in those cases, the applicable rate is determined by reference to total income. In practice, that means that treaty planning and annual compliance are inseparable from the calculation exercise itself. 11

## Primary legal texts

### Federal Direct Tax Act

**Provision** **identified**: article 14 of the Federal Act of 14 December 1990 on Direct Federal Taxation. Official ESTV materials cite this provision as the federal legal basis for taxation according to expenditure. In close paraphrase, the rule allows qualifying individuals to pay, instead of the ordinary income tax, a tax measured by annual living expenditure incurred in Switzerland and abroad for themselves and the persons they maintain. The taxable amount must be at least the highest of the indexed article 14 minimum, seven times annual rent or rental value for taxpayers with their own household, three times annual board-and-lodging cost for those without such a household,or the control-calculation basket of specified gross income items. Official federal materials also treat article 14paragraph 5 as the legal basis for modified taxation where treaty relief depends on full Swiss taxation of all income from the relevant treaty state, and article 14 paragraph 6 as the indexation hook for the minimum amount. The current indexed figure is CHF 435,000 for tax year 2026. 23

**Why** it **matters**: article 14 is the core federal calculation rule. It establishes that the regime is base-driven, comparator-driven and indexed, and that treaty-relief income can enlarge the tax base rather than merely reduce foreign tax leakage. 24

### Tax Harmonisation Act

**Provision** **identified**: article 6 of the Federal Act on the Harmonisation of Direct Taxes of Cantons and Municipalities. I did not retrieve the current Fedlex wording of article 6 directly in this session, but official federal and cantonal sources consistently treat it as the canton-side analogue to article 14 DFTA/ LIFD.The foreign-tax-credit ordinance expressly refers to article 6 paragraph 7 as the cantonal modified-taxation provision, and the cantonal statutes reviewed here mirror the article 14 structure while adding cantonal minima and wealth-tax mechanics. 25

**Why** it **matters**: article 6 is the harmonisation anchor for the cantonal side of expenditure taxation. It explains why cantons broadly share the federal architecture but still diverge on minimum amounts, wealth-tax treatment and implementation details. 26

### Federal ordinance on taxation according to expenditure

**Provision** **identified:** Ordinance of 20 February 2013 on taxation according to expenditure for direct federal tax, cited by the ESTV in the official return guidance as the applicable ordinance. I did not retrieve the current Fedlex consolidated text directly in this session, so the wording below is a close paraphrase drawn from official ESTV guidance. Article 1 limits deductions in the federal control calculation to property mnaintenance costs and the customary costs of managing movable assets to the extent their returns aretaxed. Article 2 excludes social deductions. The current federal forms are consistent with that ordinance structure. 27

<!-- 6 -->

**Why** it **mnatters**: the ordinance is what turns the control calculation into a largely gross-income comparison. In practice, this sharply narrows planning room and prevents the regime from functioning as a mere low-flat-fee arrangement. 16

### Federal ordinance on the credit for foreign withholding taxes

**Provision** **identified:** article 4 of the federal ordinance on the credit for foreign withholding taxes, as reflected in the official Fedlex result and the ESTV DA-M notice. In close paraphrase, persons taxed according to expenditure may not claim a foreign tax credit for taxes relating to income that is not fully taxed in Switzerland; an exception exists where, under article 14 paragraph 5 DFTA or cantonal provisions based on article 6 paragraph 7 StHG/LHID, all income from the treaty state is taxed at the rate for total income. 28

**Why** it **matters**: this is the legal bridge between expenditure taxation and treaty-credit mechanics. It explains why "claiming treaty relief" is not a nneutral step in a forfait case. 29

### Selected cantonal statutes and ordinances

**Ticino,** **Tax** **Law** **article** **13.** The current official online law text states that the income-side expenditure base is the taxpayer's annual worldwide living costs, but at least the highest of CHF 434,700,seven times annual rent or rental value, or three times annual board cost. It then adds a substitute wealth tax on a wealth base equal to five times the amount under paragraph 3, subject always to the wealth determined by the control-calculation baskket. The control calculation itself compares the lump-sum outcome against ordinary income and wealth taxes on listed Swiss assets and treaty-relief income.The Council of State adjusts the article 13 paragraph 3 letter a amount to the CPI. 30

**Wallis, Tax Law article 11 and Ordinance 642.400.** The Wallis statute provides the same broad eligibility structure, but for calculation purposes it delegates the minimum amount to the State Council. The current ordinance sets that minimum at CHF 250,000. The statute also requires the wealth tax to be computed on at least four times the retained expenditure amount, and the control calculation compares the result with ordinary income and wealth tax on listed Swiss assets and treaty-relief income. The ordinance limits deductions to property maintenance and ordinary management costs and excludes other deductions, including debt interest. 31

**Why** **these** **cantonal texts matter**: they show, in black-letter form, how the harmonised federal architecture becomes materially different once cantonal income and wealth taxes are layered on top. They are also a useful reminder that the "same" Swiss lump-sum case can have very different effective outcomes in different cantons. 32

## Cantonal practice notes

**Geneva.** Geneva's official pages are the strongest cantonal explanatory materials located in this research set. They state that the retained base is whichever of three comparative norms generates the highest tax: expenditure itself, the seven-times-rent or three-times-board floor, or the control calculation based on Swiss-source income and wealth plus treaty-relief income. The pages also state that the canton-and-commune base can differ from the federal base, that a 10 per cent uplift is applied to reflect wealth tax, and that the ICC minimum is CHF 426,357 for 2026 before the wealth-tax uplift, with the federal direct-tax article 14 minimum separately shown as CHF 435,000 for 2026. Geneva's modified-taxation page identifies Germany, Austria, Belgium, Italy, Norway, the United States and Canada as the relevant states and says that all income from those states mmust be taxable in Switzerland and subjected to direct tax, with Geneva further stating that the maximum rate applies under its modified approach. These are official canton materials, but they remain explanatory guidance rather than federal black-letter law. 37

**Wallis.** Wallis offers both black-letter law and a practical administrative form. The law fixes the structure of the canton's income-and-wealth approach, while the tax administration's official joint form for the tax authority and migration service shows what the canton wants evidenced at the front end: detailed worldwide expense categories for housing, taxes, insurance, travel, education and lifestyle. The form is procedural and evidential, not a statutory statement of law, but it is useful for understanding how the canton operationalises the worldwide-expenditure concept in real files. 38

**Ticino.** Ticino's official online law text is current and unusually clear. ## Practical legal analysis

The legal mechanics are best understood as a three-stage filter. First, the tax authority looks at actual worldwide lifestyle expenditure. Secondly, it applies mandatory minimum comparators, principally the indexed federal or cantonal minimum and thehousing-based multiple. Thirdly, it runs the control calculation. The retained amount is whichever route yields the higher taxable result. That structure explains why the regime is often misdescribed in popular writing: a number may be negotiated at the ruling stage, but the underlying legal machinery is not a fixed negotiated tax liability. It is an annual highest-of calculation under ordinary rates. 39

The official forms also show why real-case modelling is more complex than the legislation alone suggests. Form 3b includes Swiss taxes themselves as part of annual living costs. That means the expenditure budget is, in practice, somewhat iterative: the tax burden helps constitute the expenditure base that can itself drive the tax burden. The official materials do not spell that out as a doctrinal rule, but it is a sensible professional inference from the structure of the form and should be borne in mind when checking whether an agreed ruling amount is still robust in later years. 40

The seven-times-rent rule deserves particular caution. It is not merely an evidential shortcut. In many high-value cases it is the operative floor. The official federal guidance further tightens it by requiring an arm's-length annual rent where the Swiss home is owned by a related person or related company and by taking the highest Swiss rent or rental value if more than one Swiss property is available to the taxpayer, with the other properties still relevant to total living expenditure. So a taxpayer who believes their case is driven purely by some notional "forfait amount" may actually be driven by Swiss residential facts instead. 41

The control calculation is the key anti-avoidance device and the point at which treaty strategy becomes tax-base strategy. Federally, the basket is mostly income-side and gross. Cantonally, it may become an income-and-wealth comparison. Either way, once Swiss-source returns or treaty-relief foreign income are significant, the control calculation can overtake the lifestyle base. That is why official materials let the taxpayer forgo treaty benefits if they wish to avoid pulling income from a treaty state into the calculation. It also explains why foreign tax credits are generally unavailable unless the case is moved into the modified system that taxes all income from the relevant treaty state at the rate foor total income. 42

The federal-cantonal split is especially important for wealth. A federal-only analysis misses a large part of the real burden because there is no federal wealth tax for individuals, while the canton-and-commune layer often adds substitute wealth bases or an ordinary wealth-tax comparison. Geneva's 10per cent uplift, Wallis's 4x rule and Ticino's 5x rule are not cosmetic differences; they can materially change the combined tax cost and, in some fact patterns, may matter more than the federal article 14amount. This is one reason why canton choice remains central even in a pack focused only on tax calculation. 43

<!-- 9 -->

Treaty-country drafting must remain conservative. Current Geneva guidance and the current federal forms point to a seven-state modified-taxation model. Current federal guidance also preserves a separate French caveat. That combination strongly suggests that treaty outcomes are not uniform across states and that older generic statements about "forfait taxpayers generally benefiting from Swiss treaties" are too crude for reliable drafting. For later content generation, the safe drafting posture is to say that treaty access and foreign tax-credit treatment are treaty-specific and often depend on whether the taxpayer accepts modified taxation. 44

Finally,annual compliance matters because the forms themselves are substantive. The taxpayer must report the annual expenditure budget and the defined control-calculation items every year.That means material changes in housing, dependants, expense patterns, Swiss-source asset mix or treaty claims can alter the retained base even where the taxpayer's immigration position or residence permit remains unchanged. In other words, the ruling is only part of the picture; annual facts continue to matter. 45

## Drafting cautions

·Do not describe Swiss expenditure taxation as a fixed annual fee or a nominal "flat tax"; the law uses a highest-of calculation under the ordinary tariff. 39

· Do not quote a federal minimum without stating the tax year. The official amount is CHF 434,700for 2025 and CHF 435,000 for 2026. 8

·Do not omit the seven-times-rent or rental-value rule, because in many files it is the real binding floor. 15

·Do not omit the control calculation or call it optional. It is a mandatory comparator and can displace the lifestyle base. 46

·Do not describe the control calculation as a net-income calculation. It is largely gross and permits only limited deductions. 47

·Do not collapse federal direct tax and cantonal taxes into one undifferentiated number. Federal direct tax is income-tax only; cantons may add separate wealth-tax mechanicS. 48

·Do not generalise one canton's wealth-tax model to all cantons. Geneva, Wallis and Ticino already show materially different approaches. 49

· Do not say that treaty benefits or foreign tax credits are automatically available to forfait taxpayers. They are not. Modified taxation and treaty-specific conditions matter. 50

·Do not state that "all tax treaties work the same way" for lump-sum taxpayers. Current official guidance treats France separately from the seven-state modified-taxation group. 51

·Do not present current Geneva explanatory pages as if they were federal law. They are official canton guidance and should be labelled as such. 52

·Do not assume that a ruling concluded on arrival freezes the tax base indefinitely. The official materials require annual recalculation and annual disclosures. 22

·Where the current Fedlex text of a cited federal ordinance has not been directly retrieved, say so and label any wording as a close paraphrase from official ESTV guidance rather than as a verbatim statutory extract. 15
