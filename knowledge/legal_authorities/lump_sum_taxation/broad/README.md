# Broad Lump Sum Taxation Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss lump sum taxation blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring lump sum taxation issues across more than one canton, applicant profile, residence scenario or long-term planning route.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_legal_framework_and_route_architecture.md`

This pack should cover the overall legal framework and route architecture for Swiss lump sum taxation, including the relationship between tax eligibility, immigration discretion, residence permit basis, canton selection, residence substance and long-term planning.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_tax_eligibility_and_conditions.md`

This pack should cover eligibility for expenditure-based taxation, including nationality-neutral tax conditions, first-time or returning residence issues, absence of gainful activity in Switzerland, qualifying residence status and the distinction between tax eligibility and immigration permission.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_expenditure_minimums_and_control_calculation.md`

This pack should cover expenditure minimums, control calculations, deemed expenditure, housing-based multipliers, federal and cantonal minimum tax bases, worldwide lifestyle expenditure, and drafting cautions where tax-base calculations affect client expectations.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_immigration_discretion_and_residence_permit_basis.md`

This pack should cover the immigration basis for residence under the lump sum taxation route, including cantonal and federal discretion, public interest / significant fiscal interest arguments, permit categories, admission limits where relevant and the distinction between tax agreement and residence authorisation.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_no_gainful_activity_foreign_business_and_board_roles.md`

This pack should cover the prohibition on gainful activity in Switzerland, foreign business interests, passive investment, directorships, board roles, management activity, remote work risks and drafting cautions where commercial activity may undermine lump sum taxation or residence positioning.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_physical_presence_tax_domicile_and_centre_of_life.md`

This pack should cover physical presence, Swiss tax domicile, centre of life, residence substance, principal home, family relocation, travel pattern, personal and economic ties, and risks where Swiss residence is too thin or another country may claim tax residence.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_family_members_and_derivative_residence.md`

This pack should cover spouses, partners, children and dependants in lump sum taxation residence cases, including derivative residence, household formation, schooling, family relocation, independent work restrictions, separate tax treatment and long-term planning issues for family members.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_canton_selection_availability_and_practice.md`

This pack should cover canton availability, cantonal abolition or retention of lump sum taxation, tax ruling practice, minimum thresholds, negotiation practice, immigration coordination, local evidential expectations and risks arising from treating cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_procedure_sequencing_and_evidence.md`

This pack should cover procedural sequencing, including pre-clearance, tax ruling or tax agreement steps, immigration filing, D visa or entry procedure where relevant, cantonal and federal authorisation, evidence preparation, timing and risks arising from incorrect sequencing.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_compliance_renewal_and_loss_of_status.md`

This pack should cover ongoing compliance, annual tax compliance, renewal of residence permits, continuing absence of Swiss gainful activity, maintenance of residence substance, changes in family or business circumstances, loss of lump sum taxation status and loss or non-renewal of residence status.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_c_permit_naturalisation_and_long_term_planning.md`

This pack should cover long-term residence planning, including ordinary residence progression, C permit considerations, naturalisation issues, integration requirements, language requirements, ordinary taxation risks, permanent residence expectations and the limits of lump sum taxation as a long-term planning tool.

### `legal_authority_lump_sum_taxation_comparison_with_other_hnwi_residence_routes.md`

This pack should compare lump sum taxation with other HNWI residence routes, including ordinary residence, entrepreneur or business routes, employment-based residence, retirement residence, EU / EFTA residence options where relevant, residence by investment-style misconceptions and alternative wealth-planning structures.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several lump sum taxation residence scenarios.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in HNWI residence, tax eligibility, canton selection, expenditure calculations, no-gainful-activity questions, family relocation, permit renewal, loss of status and long-term planning topics.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad lump sum taxation packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Federal Act on Direct Federal Taxation (DBG / LIFD); the Federal Act on Tax Harmonisation (StHG / LHID); cantonal tax legislation; cantonal lump sum taxation practice; tax ruling or tax agreement practice; the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG); the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE); SEM Directives and official procedure guidance; federal and cantonal case law where relevant; cantonal migration authority guidance and practice; consular or D visa procedure where relevant; tax domicile and residence rules; no-gainful-activity restrictions; expenditure-based taxation calculations; family residence rules; permit renewal practice; C permit and naturalisation rules.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; cantonal practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; tax ruling practice; cantonal practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe tax eligibility as a right to residence. Do not describe a cantonal tax agreement as an immigration approval. Do not describe discretionary residence routes as entitlements. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad lump sum taxation authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

applicant nationality; current residence status; prior Swiss residence history; tax eligibility; immigration admissibility; canton availability; cantonal tax practice; cantonal migration practice; federal approval where relevant; no gainful activity in Switzerland; foreign business activity; board roles; passive investment; expenditure base; control calculation; family composition; household formation; physical presence; tax domicile; centre of life; evidence of wealth and resources; accommodation; health insurance; entry clearance and D visa procedure; renewal conditions; loss of status; C permit planning; naturalisation planning; interaction with ordinary taxation; comparison with alternative HNWI residence options.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss lump sum taxation and residence system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several lump sum taxation residence scenarios, such as tax eligibility, expenditure minimums, immigration discretion, canton selection, evidence, physical presence, compliance, renewal and long-term planning.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific applicant category or residence scenario, such as third-country HNWI applicants, EU / EFTA applicants, retired applicants, families relocating together or clients moving from a specific country.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as foreign board roles, partial Swiss activity, weak residence substance, complex family structures, prior Swiss residence, canton abolition risk, dual tax residence, renewal after business changes or naturalisation while taxed on expenditure.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

lump sum taxation; taxation according to expenditure; expenditure-based taxation; Swiss lump sum taxation; HNWI residence; residence permit; B permit; C permit; naturalisation; no gainful activity in Switzerland; foreign business activity; board roles; tax domicile; centre of life; physical presence; canton selection; cantonal tax ruling; tax agreement; expenditure minimum; control calculation; DBG / LIFD for the Federal Act on Direct Federal Taxation; StHG / LHID for the Federal Act on Tax Harmonisation; LEI / AIG for the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act; OASA / VZAE for the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment; SEM Directives for SEM’s immigration directives.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss tax and immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad lump sum taxation packs should not flatten canton-specific, nationality-specific or status-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the applicant’s nationality, prior Swiss residence history, canton, tax ruling practice, immigration authority practice, business activities, family composition, physical presence, housing, wealth profile, evidence, entry procedure, renewal history and long-term objectives.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, implying that lump sum taxation is a residence-by-investment programme, or suggesting that a tax agreement alone guarantees a Swiss residence permit.
