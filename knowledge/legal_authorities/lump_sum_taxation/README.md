# Lump Sum Taxation Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains legal authority packs for the Swiss lump sum taxation blog automation workflow.

The packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts concerning Swiss lump sum taxation residence, including HNWI residence, taxation according to expenditure, canton selection, expenditure minimums, tax domicile, no-gainful-activity restrictions, family relocation, renewal, loss of status and long-term planning issues.

## Folder Structure

```text
lump_sum_taxation/
  broad/
  core/
  specific/
  README.md
```

## Folder Purposes

### `core/`

Use this folder for foundational authority packs that explain the overall Swiss lump sum taxation residence framework.

Core packs should cover cross-cutting legal architecture, including the relationship between:

the Federal Act on Direct Federal Taxation (DBG / LIFD);  
the Federal Act on Tax Harmonisation (StHG / LHID);  
cantonal tax legislation and practice;  
tax ruling or tax agreement procedure;  
the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);  
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);  
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;  
immigration discretion;  
tax domicile and residence substance;  
entry clearance, D visa, cantonal authorisation and residence permit issue.

A core pack should be broad enough to support general lump sum taxation topics across multiple applicant profiles, cantons and residence scenarios.

### `broad/`

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several lump sum taxation residence scenarios.

Examples include:

tax eligibility and conditions;  
expenditure minimums and control calculation;  
immigration discretion and residence permit basis;  
canton selection, availability and practice;  
no gainful activity, foreign business and board roles;  
physical presence, tax domicile and centre of life;  
family members and derivative residence;  
procedure, sequencing and evidence;  
compliance, renewal and loss of status;  
C permit, naturalisation and long-term planning;  
comparison with other HNWI residence routes.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise across more than one lump sum taxation scenario.

### `specific/`

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues, applicant profiles, canton-sensitive topics or complex sub-categories within lump sum taxation residence.

Examples include:

third-country HNWI applicants;  
EU / EFTA applicants;  
retired applicants;  
families relocating together;  
applicants moving from the United Kingdom, United States, Gulf states or other common source jurisdictions;  
foreign board roles;  
partial Swiss activity;  
weak residence substance;  
complex family structures;  
prior Swiss residence;  
dual tax residence;  
renewal after business changes;  
naturalisation while taxed on expenditure;  
canton abolition or availability risk.

Specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the core or broad thematic packs provide.

## Source Hierarchy

When drafting or maintaining authority packs, apply the following source hierarchy:

Federal legislation, ordinances and treaties;  
cantonal tax legislation;  
official federal tax guidance and administrative materials;  
SEM Directives, SEM instructions, official SEM factsheets and official federal immigration guidance;  
Federal Supreme Court authority and other relevant case law;  
official cantonal tax authority guidance;  
official cantonal migration authority guidance;  
tax ruling or tax agreement practice, where evidenced;  
internal legal analysis and drafting cautions;  
existing website editorial content, for positioning only.

Website editorial content must not be treated as legal authority.

## Drafting Standards for Authority Packs

Each authority pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;  
a structural overview of the relevant route, issue or framework;  
a table of legal propositions;  
primary legal texts;  
official guidance and administrative materials;  
key case law, where relevant;  
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;  
practical legal analysis;  
drafting cautions;  
source list;  
open questions or limitations.

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;  
eligibility only;  
discretion;  
facilitation;  
guidance;  
procedure;  
evidential practice;  
tax ruling practice;  
cantonal practice;  
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe tax eligibility as a right to residence. Do not describe a cantonal tax agreement as an immigration approval. Do not describe discretionary residence routes as entitlements. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Lump sum taxation should not be treated as a residence-by-investment programme.

The analysis usually depends on:

applicant nationality;  
prior Swiss residence history;  
current tax residence;  
canton availability;  
cantonal tax practice;  
cantonal migration practice;  
tax eligibility;  
immigration discretion;  
expenditure base;  
control calculation;  
business activities;  
foreign board roles;  
passive investment;  
family composition;  
physical presence;  
tax domicile;  
centre of life;  
housing;  
financial resources;  
health insurance;  
entry procedure;  
permit renewal history;  
ordinary taxation risk;  
C permit and naturalisation objectives.

Authority packs should preserve these distinctions so that public blog posts do not overstate rights, treat discretionary residence permissions as entitlements, imply that a tax agreement alone guarantees residence, or flatten canton-specific practice into uniform Swiss law.
