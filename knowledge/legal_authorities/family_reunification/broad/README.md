# Broad Family Reunification Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss family reunification blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring family reunification issues across more than one route, sponsor category or applicant type.

## Current Broad Packs

Legal_authority_family_reunification_financial_requirements_and_sponsor_stability_pack.md

This pack should cover financial eligibility, adequate resources, social assistance risks, sponsor stability, maintenance capacity and drafting cautions where financial requirements affect family reunification applications.

Legal_authority_family_reunification_after_separation_divorce_or_breakdown_pack.md

This pack should cover the residence consequences of separation, divorce, death of a spouse or family breakdown, including continuation of residence, hardship issues, integration, dependency and procedural risks.

This pack should cover applications from abroad, D visa sequencing, entry clearance, cantonal authorisation, consular steps, timing, document submission and risks arising from premature entry or incorrect filing.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several family reunification routes.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in spouse, child, partner, dependant, sponsor-status, evidence, timing, refusal and procedure topics.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad family reunification packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA);
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;
federal case law where relevant;
cantonal migration authority guidance and practice;
consular or visa procedure where relevant;
civil-status, identity and document rules where relevant;
sponsor-status distinctions;
applicant nationality distinctions;
entitlement, discretion and procedural sequencing.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
key case law, where relevant;
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad family reunification authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-route issues, including:

sponsor status;
applicant nationality;
relationship category;
age;
dependency;
residence history;
applicable legal basis;
whether the route creates an entitlement or is discretionary;
filing deadlines and time limits;
cohabitation and household formation;
housing requirements;
financial requirements;
integration or language requirements;
civil-status documentation;
identity and document reliability;
entry clearance and D visa procedure;
cantonal competence and evidential expectations;
refusal grounds, appeal options and reapplication strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss family reunification system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several routes, such as financial requirements, housing, evidence, timing, refusals, procedural sequencing or cantonal practice.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific family reunification route or applicant category, such as spouse, child, unmarried partner or adult dependant cases.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as late child reunification, L-permit sponsors, same-sex couples, inadequate housing, weak civil-status evidence or sham marriage concerns.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad family reunification packs should not flatten route-specific conditions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the sponsor’s status, the applicant’s nationality, the family relationship, age, dependency, residence history, applicable legal basis, filing deadline, housing, financial resources, integration or language requirements, civil-status evidence and cantonal procedure.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary applications as entitlements or implying that all family reunification cases are assessed under the same legal test.
