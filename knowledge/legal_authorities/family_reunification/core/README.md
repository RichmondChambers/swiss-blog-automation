# Core Family Reunification Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains core legal authority packs for the Swiss family reunification blog automation workflow.

Core packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that require an overview of the Swiss family reunification framework before moving into route-specific or issue-specific detail.

## Current Core Pack

legal_authority_core_swiss_family_reunification_framework_pack.md

This pack should provide the foundational legal architecture for Swiss family reunification, including the main statutory routes, sponsor-status distinctions, applicant categories, entitlement and discretion, procedural sequencing and recurring drafting cautions.

## Purpose of the Core Folder

Use this folder for authority packs that explain the overall Swiss family reunification framework.

Core packs should be broad enough to support general blog posts across multiple family reunification routes, including:

spouse and registered partner reunification;
child reunification;
family reunification for Swiss citizens;
family reunification for EU / EFTA nationals;
family reunification for third-country nationals;
family reunification involving C permit, B permit, L permit or provisionally admitted sponsors;
unmarried partner or intended marriage cases, where relevant;
dependent family member cases;
procedure from abroad and D-visa sequencing;
residence consequences after separation, divorce or family breakdown.

## Legal Framework to Cover

A core family reunification pack should explain the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA);
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;
federal case law where relevant;
cantonal migration authority practice and procedure;
ordinary family reunification routes;
sponsor-status distinctions;
applicant nationality distinctions;
entitlement, discretion and facilitation;
procedural sequencing between entry clearance, D visa, cantonal authorisation and residence permit issue.

## Recommended Structure for Core Packs

Each core pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of Swiss family reunification routes;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
key case law, where relevant;
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Core Analysis Points

A core family reunification authority pack should preserve the key distinctions that determine the legal analysis, including:

sponsor status;
applicant nationality;
relationship category;
age;
dependency;
residence history;
whether the route falls under LEI / AIG, AFMP / FZA, provisional admission or asylum law;
whether the route creates an entitlement or is discretionary;
filing deadlines;
cohabitation and household formation;
housing and financial requirements;
language or integration requirements;
civil-status documentation;
entry clearance and visa procedure;
cantonal competence and evidential expectations.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use the core pack for foundational framework points.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several routes, such as financial requirements, housing, evidence, timing, refusals or cantonal procedure.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific family reunification route or applicant category, such as spouse, child, unmarried partner or adult dependant cases.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as late child reunification, L-permit sponsors, same-sex couples, inadequate housing, weak civil-status evidence or sham marriage concerns.

Avoid using only one language abbreviation where both French and German references are commonly relevant in Swiss immigration practice.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Family reunification should not be treated as a single route.

The analysis usually depends on the sponsor’s status, the applicant’s nationality, the family relationship, age, dependency, residence history, applicable legal basis, filing deadline, housing, financial resources, integration or language requirements, civil-status evidence and cantonal procedure.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, flattening route-specific conditions or implying that all family members are assessed under the same legal test.
