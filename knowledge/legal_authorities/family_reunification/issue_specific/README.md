# Issue-Specific Family Reunification Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains issue-specific legal authority packs for the Swiss family reunification blog automation workflow.

Issue-specific packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern narrower risk issues, complex sub-categories or fact-sensitive problems within Swiss family reunification.

## Current Issue-Specific Packs

legal_authority_family_reunification_children_approaching_age_limits_pack.md

This pack should cover children approaching relevant age limits, including timing risks, filing strategy, residence deadlines, dependency, child-specific eligibility conditions and drafting cautions where delay may affect the application.

legal_authority_family_reunification_l_permit_sponsor_pack.md

This pack should cover family reunification where the sponsor holds an L permit, including the legal basis, discretionary character, temporary residence issues, financial and housing requirements, cantonal practice, evidential risks and drafting cautions.

legal_authority_family_reunification_same_sex_couple_pack.md

This pack should cover same-sex couple family reunification issues, including marriage or registered partnership recognition, civil-status evidence, countries where same-sex relationships are not formally recognised, evidential risks, privacy concerns and procedural cautions.

legal_authority_family_reunification_stepchild_and_blended_family_pack.md

This pack should cover stepchildren and blended family cases, including legal parentage, custody, consent from the other parent, dependency, household formation, timing, child welfare, civil-status evidence and cross-border documentation issues.

## Purpose of the Issue-Specific Folder

Use this folder for packs focused on narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories within family reunification.

Issue-specific packs should be used where a topic needs deeper treatment than the general route pack or broad thematic pack provides.

These packs should help the generator deal with legally sensitive, fact-dependent or practice-sensitive issues that may arise within spouse, child, partner, dependant or sponsor-status cases.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Issue-specific family reunification packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant;
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;
federal case law where relevant;
cantonal migration authority guidance and practice;
civil-status, parentage, custody or guardianship rules where relevant;
identity, document reliability and legalisation requirements;
sponsor-status distinctions;
applicant nationality distinctions;
entitlement, discretion and procedural sequencing.

## Recommended Structure for Issue-Specific Packs

Each issue-specific pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of the issue or risk category;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
key case law, where relevant;
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss family reunification system.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several routes, such as financial requirements, housing, evidence, timing, refusals, procedural sequencing or cantonal practice.

Use `route_specific/` for packs focused on a specific family reunification route or applicant category, such as spouse, child, unmarried partner or adult dependant cases.

Use this `issue_specific/` folder for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as children approaching age limits, L-permit sponsors, same-sex couples, stepchildren, blended families, late child reunification, inadequate housing, weak civil-status evidence, dependency disputes or sham marriage concerns.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Issue-specific family reunification packs should not treat narrow factual issues as if they produce the same result across all routes.

The analysis usually depends on the sponsor’s status, the applicant’s nationality, the relationship category, the child’s age, dependency, parentage, custody, family history, applicable legal basis, filing deadline, housing, financial resources, civil-status evidence, identity evidence and cantonal procedure.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary or practice-sensitive applications as entitlements, or implying that complex evidential issues can be resolved by a single uniform rule.
