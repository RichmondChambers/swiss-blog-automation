# Route-Specific Family Reunification Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains route-specific legal authority packs for the Swiss family reunification blog automation workflow.

Route-specific packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern a particular family reunification route, relationship category or applicant type.

## Current Route-Specific Packs

legal_authority_family_reunification_adult_dependent_relative_pack.md

This pack should cover adult dependent relative family reunification, including the legal basis, dependency threshold, sponsor-status distinctions, discretionary character where relevant, evidence of support, medical or care needs, financial requirements, housing, procedure and drafting cautions.

legal_authority_family_reunification_child_pack.md

This pack should cover child family reunification, including eligibility, age limits, filing deadlines, parental status, custody, dependency, best interests considerations, residence history, financial and housing requirements, procedure and evidential risks.

legal_authority_family_reunification_fiance_and_intended_marriage_pack.md

This pack should cover fiancé, intended marriage and post-marriage residence planning, including entry for marriage, marriage preparation, D visa sequencing, civil-status documents, timing risks, post-marriage residence options and drafting cautions.

This pack should cover spouse and registered partner reunification, including legal basis, sponsor status, marriage or partnership recognition, cohabitation, housing, financial requirements, language or integration issues, procedure, evidence and post-arrival residence consequences.

## Purpose of the Route-Specific Folder

Use this folder for packs focused on a specific family reunification route, relationship category or applicant type.

Route-specific packs should identify the legal basis, sponsor category, entitlement or discretion, eligibility conditions, procedure, evidence, risks and drafting cautions for the specific route.

These packs should help the generator avoid treating family reunification as a single route and ensure that public-facing analysis remains tied to the correct applicant category.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Route-specific family reunification packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act (LEI / AIG);
the Ordinance on Admission, Stay and Employment (OASA / VZAE);
the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP / FZA), where relevant;
SEM Directives and official procedure guidance;
federal case law where relevant;
cantonal migration authority guidance and practice;
civil-status, parentage, custody, marriage or partnership recognition rules where relevant;
identity, document reliability and legalisation requirements;
sponsor-status distinctions;
applicant nationality distinctions;
entitlement, discretion and procedural sequencing.

## Recommended Structure for Route-Specific Packs

Each route-specific pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary;
a structural overview of the relevant route or applicant category;
a table of legal propositions;
primary legal texts;
official guidance and administrative materials;
key case law, where relevant;
cantonal practice notes, where relevant;
practical legal analysis;
drafting cautions;
source list;
open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement;
eligibility only;
discretion;
facilitation;
guidance;
procedure;
evidential practice;
cantonal practice;
unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe discretionary routes as rights. Do not describe cantonal practice as uniform Swiss law.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss family reunification system.

Use `broad/` for recurring thematic issues that apply across several routes, such as financial requirements, housing, evidence, timing, refusals, procedural sequencing or cantonal practice.

Use this `route_specific/` folder for packs focused on a specific family reunification route or applicant category, such as spouse, registered partner, child, fiancé, intended marriage, unmarried partner or adult dependant cases.

Use `issue_specific/` for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as children approaching age limits, L-permit sponsors, same-sex couples, stepchildren, blended families, late child reunification, inadequate housing, weak civil-status evidence or sham marriage concerns.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Route-specific family reunification packs should not imply that all family members are assessed under the same legal test.

The analysis usually depends on the precise route, the sponsor’s status, the applicant’s nationality, the family relationship, age, dependency, civil status, parentage, custody, residence history, applicable legal basis, filing deadline, housing, financial resources, integration or language requirements, evidence and cantonal procedure.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, treating discretionary or exceptional routes as entitlements, or implying that a route is available where Swiss law recognises only a narrower or differently structured category.
