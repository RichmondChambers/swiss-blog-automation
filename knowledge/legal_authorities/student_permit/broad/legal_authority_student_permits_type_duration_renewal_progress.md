# Swiss legal authority pack on student residence permits

## Overview

### A. Executive legal summary

Swiss student residence is not one single "student visa" regime. It operates through two different legal tracks. For EU/EFTA nationals, residence for study sits principally under the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and its implementing rules for economically inactive persons, including students; that is a treaty-based position, but it remains conditional on sufficient resources, health insurance and admission to a recognised institution. For non-EU/EFTA nationals, the governing basis is Article 27 AIG together with Articles 23 and 24 VZAE/OASA, and that route is discretionary: the statute says the person may be admitted, and SEMI's own directives, citing Federal Administrative Court case law,state that Article 27 AIG confers no entitlement even where the statutory conditions are met. 1

For non-EU/EFTA study residence, SEM's 2026 directives are the key operational source. They state that the permit type follows course length: if the training lasts more than one year, the canton issues a residence permit B; if it lasts less than one year, the canton issues a short-stay permit L. The same directives state that study residence is ordinarily authorised for a maximum total periood of eight years, with exceptions only in sufficiently well-founded cases and subject to SEM approval, for example where the programme follows a logical progression. Renewal depends not only on continued enrolment and finances, but also on whether the programme remains coherent, full-time and genuinely pursued, including progress through intermnediate and final examinations within a useful period. 2

The principal non-renewal risks are conservative and evidence-driven. They include inadequate progress, failed or repeatedly delayed examinations, repeated years, drop-out, repeated study changes, "stacking" of programmes in a way suggesting migration circumvention, the point at which the residence purpose is treated as exhausted, and study interruptions or absences long enough to make the permit lapse. SEM's directives expressly say that if required examination/progress expectations are not met, the purpose of stay is treated as achieved and the permit is not renewed; they also list refusal scenarios upheld by the Federal Administrative Court, including inadequate results, drop-out, study changes, second training, exceeding the eight-year maximum and the absence of any entitlement under Article 27 AIG. 3

For EU/EFTA students, the treaty position should be described more carefully than the third-country route. SEM's student factsheet and FAQ say that students who prove sufficient means, health insurance and admission to a recognised institution are granted a residence permit for the duration of studies or for one year, and may seek annual renewal until completion. Federal SEM pages also define L as the short-stay permit for periods usually under one year and B as the residence permit for longer-term residence; current Ticino canton student pages expressly apply that split to study stays under and over one year. The safest internal drafting is therefore to speak of a treaty-based student residence position with conditional renewal, while treating the exact card category as duration-sensitive and operationally canton-specific. 4

### B. Structural overview of the legal framework

At federal level, the framework is layered. The SEM legal-basis page identifies the AIG (RS 142.20) and the VZAE/OASA (RS 142.201) as central residence texts. SEM's 2026 AIG directives then provide the main administrative interpretation for third-country student residence under Article 27 AIG and Articles 23-24VZAE. For EU/EFTA nationals, SEM's free-movement materials treat students as economically inactive persons under the AFMP, and the student factsheet expressly refers to the Ordinance on the Free Movement of Persons (VFP/OLCP; RS 142.203). 5

Competence is split vertically. Cantonal migration authoritiesissue and renew the permits and check ongoing compliance, but SEM remains materially important because its directives govern federal interpretation and because certain exceptions, in particular well-founded extensions beyond the normal eight-year ceiling, must be submitted to SEM for approval. Zurich's current cantonal guidance mnirrors that structure, stating that eight-year exceptions and some repeated-goal-change cases must be referred to SEM. 6

The framework also separates entry and stay. Third-country nationals may need an entry visa before travel and then a residence permit after admission, whereas EU/EFTA students who stay more than three months must register and obtain the relevant residence authorisation. That is why this pack should speak in terms of residence permits or residence authorisations, not a single Swiss "student visa" framework.7

#### Authorities

### D. Primary legal texts

The central third-country provision is Article 27 AIG, which governs admission for education and training. SEM's 2026 directives set out the operative conditions by reference to Article 27 AIG: schnool confirmation, appropriate accommodation, sufficient funds, and the necessary personal and educational suitability. The same directives also emphasise the temporary character of study residence and cite Article 54 VZAE for the proposition that, once education or training ends, any further stay requires a new residence basis. 19

Articles 23 and 24 VZAE/OASA are the key implementing provisions. In the official ordinance promulgation text, Article 23 addresses proof of financial means, indicators that departure is secured, the ordinary eight-year maximum, and part-time work cross-reference rules; Article 24 addresses school quality, fixed programme duration, and confirmation by the school that the applicant has the necessary linguistic and educational prerequisites. SEM's 2026 directives reproduce the same operational core and add the decisive practice points on permit type, full-time attendance and the exam/progress check. 20

For EU/EFTA students, the relevant legal layer is the AFMP free-movement regime for economically inactive persons, implemented domestically through the VFP/OLCP. SEM's AFMP factsheet states the treaty conditions for economically inactive persons, and, specifically for students, it requires convincing proof of sufficient means, admission to a recognised institution and actual capacity to follow a general or VET programme. The same factsheet cites Article 18 paragraph 2 VFP/OLCP in relation to students who commute daily from an EU/EFTA residence abroad. 21

The cooperation duty is also part of the operative legal background. Basel-Stadt's current suspension form quotes Article 90 AIG and reminds applicants and third parties of the obligation to provide truthful and complete facts and documentary evidence relevant to residence status. That matters for renewal and non-renewal risk analysis whenever finances, absences, programme changes or later challenges to candour arise. 22

### E. Official guidance and administrative materials

The single most important federal administrative source is the SEM AIG directives, version in force from 1 January 2026. In the study chapter, SEM explains the anti-circumvention rationale of Article 27 AIG, the requirement for a personal study plan, the eight-year default ceiling, the B/L split by course duration, the full-time threshold of at least 20 teaching hours per week, the obligation to sit exams within a useful period, the consequences of poor progress, and the principal case-law lines on no entitlement, diploma not being a formal statutory condition, age, and the temporary character of study residence. 23

For the EU/EFTA side, the most useful official federal materials are SEM's AFMP FAQ and the factsheet on residing in Switzerland without gainful activity. Together they show the treaty framing for students,the annual/duration-based renewal pattern, the requirement to apply for extension shortly before expiry, and the fact that the AFMP does not govern admission to Swiss universities or the award of scholarships. 9

SEM's permit-category pages remain useful for card-type architecture. The L EU/EFTA page defines the short-stay permit as the status for a fixed purpose usually lasting less than one year; the B EU/EFTA page defines the longer-term residence permit and confirms that economically inactive EU/EFTA persons can obtain it if they show sufficient means and health/accident insurance. Those pages are not student-specific, but they help explain whny canton practice often maps shorter study stays to L and longer ones to B. 24

### F. Cantonal practice notes

**Geneva.** Geneva's current student pages are useful for renewal mechanics and absence rules. Renewal of a student permit must be requested no later than 15 days before expiry, with a current enrolment certificate and, for non-EU/EFTA nationals, financial evidence; a motivation letter is required if there is a programme change or a new programme. Geneva's student FAQ also states that a permit becomes void after more than six months abroad for B holders and after more than three months abroad for L holders, including in semester-abroad scenarios beyond those thresholds. 25

**Zurich.** Zurich's current guidance is the clearest canton-level text on monitoring progress and refusal risk. It requires compliance with the programme and passing of interim/final exams within the prescribed time, allows only limited delay, treats repeated target changes with suspicion,generally refuses chained programmes, permits only one justified change of study direction, and repeats the eight-year total ceiling with SEM approval for justified exceptions. It alsodistinguishes between students at Swiss higher-education institutions, for whom no written departure undertaking is required, and other education/training cases where Zurich expects a written departure commitment. 26

**Ticino.** Ticino's current public pages are useful for the L/B duration split and timing formalities. They state expressly that students staying in Switzerland for less than one year should apply for an L permit, while students staying for more than one year should apply for a B permit. Ticino's current B-renewal page also requires renewal 14 days before expiry and notification within 14 days of changes to personal data, address and purpose of stay. 27

**Basel-Stadt** **and** **Vaud.** Basel-Stadt's current suspension form shows that some cantons have a distinct administrative mechanism for outgoing B-permit holders who interrupt residence for study or training abroad, requiring documentary proof of the foreign educational programme and setting a maximum period abroad of four years under the form's suspension framework. Vaud's official study form, although older and therefore to be used cautiously, remains useful as an example of explicit cantonal

## Practical analysis

### G. Practical legaI analysis

#### Non-EU/EFTA student residence

The third-country route should be analysed as a conditional discretionary admission to residence for a temporary study purpose. SEM's directives require a concrete and personal study plan, a clearly identified academic goal, confirmation from the institution that the applicant has the educational level and language skills needed to follow the programme, adequate accommodation and sufficient means. The same directives say thatthe route exists to allow genuine training, not to bypass the stricter general admission rules, and they expressly frame student residence as temporary from the outset. 29

That temporary logic drives both duration and renewal. Where the course length exceeds one year, the operational permit is B; where it is shorter, it is L. Renewal is usually carried out year by year,but the total study-residence period is ordinarily capped at eight years. Exceptions are possible only when the facts show a coherent, targeted educational sequence, and then only with SEM approval. Internal drafting should therefore treat any request involving a second degree, a late field change, a post-graduate extension or a long multi-stage academic path as an exception case that needs careful justification, not as routine continuation. 6

#### EU/EFTA students under the AFMP

The EU/EFTA position is materially stronger, but it is still conditional and should not be overstated. SEM's treaty materials say that students can reside in Switzerland if they show sufficient means, insurance and admission to a recognised institution. Student permits are then issued for the duration of studies or for one year, with annual renewals until completion so long as the conditions continue to be met. That is better expressed as a treaty-based right to residence upon proof of the conditions than as an automatic or unconditional entitlement to a fixed five-year student permit. 30

A further drafting trap is university admission. SEM's AFMP factsheet says that admission to higher-education and other tertiary-level institutions, and the award of scholarships, do not fall within the AFMP's scope. In other words, the AFMP protects residence once the treaty conditions are satisfied; it does not itself oblige a Swiss university to admit the student. 21

#### Progress monitoring, exams, attendance and evidence

At federal level, "progress" is monitored through programme adherence rather than a codified national credits rule. SEM's directives say that students must sit intermediate and final examinations within a useful period, and that cantonal migration offices must verify this. Zurich operationalises that by checking whether the student has complied with the programme and passed partial and final examinations within the prescribed time, allowing only limnited delay. Geneva's renewal page is document-based and asks at minimum for a current enrolment certificate; where there has been a change of programme, Geneva additionally asks for a motivation letter. (31

That has two drafting consequences. First, "credits" or ECTSpoints should not be presented as a federal statutory test unless the proposition is being sourced to a specific institutional or cantonal practice.

Secondly, "attendance" should be described cautiously. The federal sources do not impose a universal attendance percentage, but SEM's directives do require a full-time school with at least 20 teaching hours per week, exclude schools with only limited or evening programming from the standard Article 27route, and Zurich requires regular, serious attendance before extending certain intensive language-course permits. 32

## Failed examinations, repeated years, study interruption and purpose exhaustion

The federal practice on failed examinations is intentionally strict. SEM's directives state that if the exam/ progress expectation is not met, the purpose of the residence is treated as achieved and the permit is not extended. The directives then give refusal examples upheld in litigation: inadequate results, repetitions, drop-out, changes of study, second training and exceeding the eight-year maximum. Zurich's current guidance reaches the same operational endpoint, stating that failure to pass required examinations within the allowed time means either that departure is no longer secured or that the purpose of stay is exhausted, so the permit is no longer renewed. 33

Study interruption needs to be handled separately from simple renewal. Geneva states that absence from Switzerland beyond six months on a B permit or three months on an L permit causes the permit to lapse, and the same rule is applied to semester-abroad cases that exceed those periods. Basel-Stadt, by contrast, publishes a separate suspension/readmission mechanism for B permits during study or training abroad, but that is expressly a special procedure requiring pre-departure action and proof of the foreign course, not the ordinary renewal of a student permit. Zurich also notes that where at least one year lies between two study stays, its anti-stacking rule on chained programmes does not apply, which is helpful but does not itself create a right to re-admission. 34

Completion and "purpose achieved" are also more nuanced than they first appear. SEM's directives cite FAC case law for the proposition that ending a course with a diploma or professional certificate is not itself a separate statutory condition under Article 27 AIG or Article 24 VZAE. The practical question is not "has a diploma physically been issued?" but whether the authorised educational purpose is still live, coherent and realistically pursuable. Once the programme is completed, abandoned, or no longer realistically achievable, any further stay requires a new legal basis. 17

## Drafting cautions

### H. Drafting cautions

**Do not write as if there were a single Swiss student visa route.** Separate entry-visa analysis from residence-permit analysis, and separate EU/EFTA treaty residence from third-country discretionary study residence. Geneva and Ticino both present student admission as a residence-permit process, with visa issues only where separately relevant. 35

**Do not use entitlement language for non-EU/EFTA students.** Article 27 AIG is **a** discretionary admission provision. SEM's directives, quoting case law, go further and say that a restrictive admission policy can justify refusal even when the Article 27 conditions are satisfied. 8

**Do not generalise EU/EFTA students into the ordinary five-year economically inactive B-permit** **model without qualification.** SEM's student-specific treaty materials say that the permit is issued for the duration of studies or for one year, with annual renewals. If internal text needs to mention the card category, it should either cite a canton-specific source or make clear that duration and card type are practice-sensitive. 11

<!-- 7 -->

**Do not state a federal Swiss rule that students must earn a fixed number of credits or ECTS** **points.** The federal rule is about maintaining a coherent full-time programme and making timely progress through the relevant examinations. Credits may be evidentially useful, but they are not the statutory test in the sources reviewed. 14

**Do not say that a permit is renewed until the diploma is physically issued.** SEM's directives cite FAC case law saying that a diploma or professional certificate is not an independent statutory condition under Article 27 AIG/Article 24 VZAE. The correct analytical question is whether the authorised educational purpose continues to be genuinely pursued and remains achievable. 17

**Do not overlook cantonnal variation on interruption, renewal paperwork and study-plan changes.** Geneva currently asks for renewal 15 days before expiry and treats prolonged absence as making the permit void. Ticino requires B-renewal 14 days before expiry and requires notification of changes in the purpose of stay. Vaud's official form requires immediate notification of any study-plan modification. Basel-Stadt offers a separate suspension procedure for certain B-permit interruptions. 36

**Do** **not** **rely** **on** **age** **as** **a** **proxy** **refusal ground.** SEM's 2026 directives note that an administrative practice of generally refusing study permits to applicants over 30 violates the constitutional prohibition of discrimination, citing BGE 147 I 89. That matters especially when drafting "risk" sections or internal guidance notes on atypical applicants. 37

**Do not present post-study stay as student-permit renewal.** SEM's directives say that after the completion of education or training, further stay requires a new permnit. Internal content should therefore describe post-completion job-search or employment options, where relevant, as separate routes with separate legal bases. 17
