# Swiss legal authority pack on student permit procedure and sequencing

## Suggested filename:

legalauthoritystudentpermitsprocedurevisaconsularcantonsempack.pdf.This pack is drafted for internal legal-content use and deliberately avoids presenting Swiss study migration as a single "student visa" route. Swiss law separates the residence basis from any entry visa. For EU/ EFTA nationals, the governing position is the free-movement regime as reflected in SEM and ch.ch guidance: no entry visa is required, but for stays over three months the student must register and obtain the appropriate residence authorisation, while showing sufficient resources, adequate health and accident insurance, and proof of admission. For non-EU/EFTA nationals, the operative residence basis is Article 27 FNIA/AIG, which is discretionary rather than entitlement-based; depending on nationality, an entry visa may also be required, ordinarily a national type D visa for study or training exceeding 90 days. 1

For third-country student cases, the practical sequence is: obtain school admission and supporting documents; initiate the residence/entry process from abroad; where a visa is required, submit the national visa application to the competent Swiss representation for the applicant's legal domicile; the representation forwards the file to the competent cantonal migration authority; the canton examines the residence conditions; SEM approval is required only in defined categories or where the case is referred; only after approval may the representation issue the visa. After arrival, the student must register with the locally designated Swiss authority within the applicable deadline, usually 14 days. 2

A legally cautious internal position should therefore distinguish four separate questions in every draft: the residence basis, whether an entry visa is required, which authority is first-instance competent, and whether SEM approval is additionally required. Internal drafting should also avoid categorical statements that a personal interview or document legalisation is always mandatory: federal ordinance text is more flexible, while SEM student directives and representation practice make personal appearance and authenticity checks common, especially for visa-subject applicants and in higher-risk files. 3

## Structural overview of the legal framework

The legal architecture has three layers. First, the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and EFTA free-movement arrangements govern EU/EFTA students; SEM's 2026 directives state that the FNIA/AIG applies to free-movement beneficiaries only subsidiarily where the treaty framework does not provide otherwise or where the Act is more favourable. Secondly, third-country students rely on the ordinary foreign-nationals regime, principally Article 27 FNIA/AIG, read with the OASA/VZAE provisions on means, personal qualifications, school requirements, duration and change of purpose. Thirdly,where entry clearance is needed, the visa layer is governed by the Ordinance on Entry and the Granting of Visas and by SEM/FDFA visa guidance; that layer regulates the long-stay visa, the competent representation, personal appearance rules, and forwarding of the file to the canton. 4

The route is therefore not a single unified "student visa" system. For EU/EFTA nationals, the operational question is residence authorisation after or upon arrival, not a Swiss entry visa. For third-country

nationals, the residence question and the visa question interact but remain distinct: the visa is an entry instrument, whereas the residence status depends on satisfaction of Article 27 FNIA/AIG and related ordinance conditions, as examined by Swiss migration authorities. FDFA material expressly states that a national visa for stays over 90 days is subject to authorisation by the cantonal migration authority competent for the intended place of residence, and SEM's visa FAQ states that the representation forwards the category D file to the cantonal migration office for examination. 5

Within the third-country regime, Article 27 FNIA/AIG sets the core statutory criteria: school confirmation of admission, appropriate accommodation, necessary financial mneans, and the personal and educational suitability for the proposed course; after completion or discontinuation, any further stay is governed by the general admission rules. OASA/VZAE Articles 23 and 24 elaborate those criteria by specifying acceptable evidence of means, the anti-circumvention logic behind the personal-qualification test, the ordinary eight-year ceiling for education and training, and minimum requirements for schools and teaching programmes. OASA/VZAE Article 54 separately provides that a new permit is required if the purpose of stay changes. 6

The competence structure is shared. Swiss representations issue visas on behalf of the competent Swiss authority, but for long-stay study cases the cantonal migration authority is the decisive examining authority in the ordinary course. SEM's role is not universal first-instance approval in every student matter; it enters the process where federal approval law requires it, where the case is one of the categories reserved to SEM approval, or where the canton refers the matter for a federal-law check. SEM may then refuse approval, limit it in time, or attach conditions.7

### Primary legal texts

The core statutory residence basis for third-country student permits is FNIA/AIG Article 27. The key associated procedural and substantive ordinance provisions are OASA/VZAE Articles 5 to 11 on entry authorisation, arrival declaration and visa extension for non-working stays, Articles 23 and 24 on student means, personal qualifications and school requirements, Article 54 on change of purpose, and Articles 85 and 86 on SEM approval procedure. On the visa side, the key ordinance provisions are OEV Articles 9 and 22 to 24, dealing with the long-stay visa obligation, competent Swiss representation, personal appearance, and supporting documents. For EU/EFTA students, the treaty-level basis is the free-movement regime, reflected in SEM and ch.ch materials under the heading of residence without gainful activity. 22

<!-- 4 -->

A further statutory provision that matters to sequencing is FNIA/AIG Article 17:where someone lawfully enters for a temporary stay and later seeks a permit for a longer-term residence basis, the decision must ordinarily be awaited abroad unless the admission conditions are obviously fulfilled and the canton allows the stay during the procedure. This is particularlyrelevant when an internal draft contemplates converting a short-stay or other temporary entry into a different long-stay residence purpose from within Switzerland. 23

## Official guidance and administrative materials

The most operational federal source is the SEM's 2026 directives on education and training. They spell out the anti-circumvention rationale, the personal-study-plan requirement, the expectation of suitable school confirmation and language capacity, the representation interview, the forwarding of complete files to the canton, and the circumstances in which representation comments are especially desirable. They also summarise relevant case law and explain when SEM approval is triggered for "approval-required students" and unusually long education paths. 24

The SEM visa FAQ and FDFA visa pages establish the federal procedural sequence from the visa side: category D visas are for study or training over 90 days; they must be filed with the competent Swiss representation abroad; the representation forwards the file to the cantonal migration office for examination; the processing time varies by canton and purpose; and Country-of-residence-specific document lists are found on representation websites. A current FDFA representation page for national visas states approximately 8-12 weeks in ordinary cases and emphasises that the representation issues the visa only after Swiss approval. 25

The official ch.ch portal and SEM free-movement FAQ provide the operational treaty-side position for EU/EFTA students and the public-facing federal summary for non-EU/EFTA residents without gainful activity. Those materials are useful asconservative authority for nationality distinctions, registration after arrival, funds and insurance, and the need for non-EU/EFTA persons to initiate the residence process before arrival. 26

## Cantonal practice notes

The federal materials reviewed make clear that canton selection is not an administrative afterthought;it is central to the route. The responsible canton is the canton of intended residence, and it is that canton's migration authority that examines the study-residence file once the representation has transmitted it. A current FDFA national-visa page expressly telIs applicants that status enquiries during processing should be directed to the responsible cantonal migration authority, because the representation will not give procedural updates. 27

Post-arrival formalities are likewise canton-shaped. OASA/VZAE Article 10 requires the foreign national entering for a non-working stay of more than three months under an entry authorisation to declare arrival within 14 days to the service designated by the canton. FDFA study guidance for EU/EFTA students describes this in practice as registration with the local residents' registration office at the place of residence. That means internal drafting should avoid naming a single office generically for all cantons without checking local terminology. 28

The public federal materials do not provide a single uniform canton-by-canton student checklist. Instead, they repeatedly point applicants back to the destination canton and the responsible representation for operative requirements, timing and accepted documnentary formats. The legally safe internal approach is therefore to treat canton-specific documentary practices, appointment handling and turnaround estimates as variable implementation matters rather than national rules. 29

## Practical legal analysis

For EU/EFTA students, the correct internal framing is a free-movement residence analysis. The student does not ordinarily need a Swiss entry visa, may enter on a valid passport or national identity card, and must then register within 14 days if staying loonger than three months. The core checks are admission to a recognised institution, sufficient means, and adequate health and accident insurance. ch.ch and SEM both state that the permit is issued for one year or for the duration of studies and renewed annually while the conditions remain met. That is materially different from the third-country Article 27 route and should not be collapsed into it. 30

For third-country students, the residence case should be built first and the visa question second. Article 27 FNIA/AIG requires the school's confirmation that the course can be undertaken, appropriate accommodation, sufficient means, and personal and educational suitability. SEM directives then operationalise those statutory items: there should be a personal study plan with a clearly identified target qualification; the school should confirm the candidate's educational level and language ability; fee payment or fee-exemption evidence should be available; the applicant should provide a motivation letter, CV and language information sheet; and suitable accommodation should be shown. This is the material that allows the canton to authorise the residence purpose. If the nationality is visa-subject for long stay, the same substantive package is then channelled through the representation as a D-visa application. 31

On personal appearance and interview, the safest internal statement is nuanced. The visa ordinance says that personal appearance is not generally required to submit a long-stay visa application,although SEM may demand it for identification or verification and certain cases require it as a rule. However, the SEM's student directives go further for visa-subject study applicants: they say such applicants must appear personally at the competent Swiss representation, subject to exceptions where language capacity is obvious and migration-risk concerns are absent, and they prescribe a representation interview that is "very helpful" to the migration authorities in assessing background, future intentions and language skills. In doubtful cases, either the representation or the canton may insist on personal appearance. Internal copy should therefore say interviews are common and often expected in visa-subject student files, not that the ordinance makes them universally mandatory in every study case. 15

Document authentication should likewise be described conservatively. The OEV leaves the formal list of long-stay supporting documents to SEM. SEM's directives then explain that diplomas and financial confirmations may be checked for authenticity by the representation, for example through over-legalisation by the foreign ministry or confirmation from the issuing university or bank. The same directives indicate that such checking is particularly relevant in higher-fraud or higher-migration-pressure contexts. OASA/VZAE separately authorises competent authorities to require original identity documents and, in concrete-risk cases, to deposit them. The correct internal proposition is therefore that authenticity verification is available and used case by case, not that apostilles or legalisations are invariably required nationwide. 32

As to decision-making, the visa desk abroad is not the substantive permit authority. Federal guidance states that the long-stay study application is forwarded to the competent cantonal migration authority for examination, and the representation can issue the visa only after the competent Swiss authorities have approved the case. In substance, the canton assesses whether the Article 27 and OASA conditions are met. The representation contributes by checking file completeness under local-country

requiremments, carrying out any interview, assessing language and authenticity issues, and sending comments to the canton where appropriate. 33

SEM approval is a separate and limited overlay. OASA/VZAE Articles 85 and 86 establish SEM's approval competence and the possibility for SEM to refuse, time-limit or condition approval. SEM's directives then identify two study-specific triggers of practical importance: first, certain first residence permits for students and related academic categories from listed higher-risk states must be submitted to SEM for approval; secondly, education/training that exceeds the ordinary eight-year maximum under OASA/ VZAE Article 23(3) requires SEM approval. In addition, the canton may submit any case to SEM for a federal-law check. Internal drafting should therefoore avoid saying that "SEM approves student permits" without qualification. 34

Timing is administrative rather than fixed by a short statutory deadline. The SEM visa FAQ says that D-visa applications are processed by the cantons and usually take one to several months depending on canton and purpose. A current FDFA representation page gives an indicative 8-12 week range, and at least eight to ten weeks for long-stay visas initiated abroad, while warning that timing may vary considerably by canton and by the content of the application. Because representation-specific document lists differ by country of residence, a complete-file assumption is essential; internal content should therefore avoid promising uniform Swiss processing times. 35

After arrival, the post-entry obligation is immediate registration rather than a freshsubstantive re-application on the merits. For non-working stays over three months carried under an entry authorisation, OASA/VZAE Article 10 requires declaration of arrival within 14 days to the canton-designated office. For EU/EFTA students, SEM and ch.ch also use the 14-day registration rule. FDFA study guidance describes the documents commonly presented at that stage for EU/EFTA students, including the passport or identity card, university registration proof, funds, local address and photographs. 36

Finally, post-study continuity must be handled with care. Article 27(3) FNIA/AIG states that any further stay after completion or discontinuation is governed by the general admission rules, and OASA/VZAE Article 54 saysthat a new permit is required when the purpose of stay changes. The one clear special post-study opening identified in the materials is the separate six-month job-search admission for third-country nationals with a Swiss higher-educationdegree, which SEM describes as a distinct short-stay regime rather than a simple extension of the study permit. 20

## Drafting cautions

Do not describe the subject as a single "Swiss student visa" route. The legally correct structure is:free-movement residence for EU/EFTA students; Article 27 FNIA/AIG residence for third-country students; and, where applicable, a separate long-stay type D visa as the entry mechanism. 37

Do not imply entitlement for third-country students. SEM's own directives state that Article 27 FNIA/AIG does not confer a claim to a permit, and they cite BVGer authority allowing refusal even where the Article 27 conditions are met in a restrictive admission policy context. 11

Do not overstate the role of the Swiss representation. The representation receives the visa application, may interview the applicant, may verify documents, and forwards the file to the canton; but the residence-authorisation examination for ordinary long-stay study files is conducted in Switzerland by the competent cantonal migration authority, with SEM becoming decisive only where approval law requires or where the canton refers the case. 38

<!-- 7 -->

Do not write that a personal interview is always legally mandatory. The better formulation is that personal appearance and interview are standard SEM-directed practice for visa-subject student cases, subject to exceptions, and may be insisted upon where the representation or canton considers that necessary. 15

Do not write that document legalisation or apostille is universally required. The authorities may verify authenticity and may require originals or additional validation, especially in higher-risk files; whether legalisation is demanded is case-and post-specific. 17

Do not present SEM approval as automatic or universal in student cases. The safer wording is that SEM approval is required only in specified categories, including certain first permits for listed-state students, unusually long education cases, and any case that the canton submits for federal review. 39

Do not suggest that completion of studies gives a free-standing right to remain. Any further stay is subject to the general admission rules, save for specific separate regimes such as the post-Swiss-degree job-search permit. 20

## Open questions and limitations

The browser-accessible copies of some primary legal texts were obtained through Refworld-hosted reproductions of Swiss official texts. In the retrieved environment, the accessible copies of the FNIA/AIG and some ordinance PDFs displayed statuses of October 2023 or early 2024, while the related Refworld landing pages indicated later amendment coverage. The propositions relied on here are cross-checked against 2025-2026 official SEM, FDFA and ch.ch materials, and no conflicting later amendment to the cited study-permit provisions was identified in those official materials. Nonetheless, for formal filing or litigation use, the final pack should be checked against the current consolidated French, German or Italian text on Fedlex immediately before publication. 40

This pack does not include a canton-by-canton digest of student filing practice because the official federal materials reviewed repeatedly direct applicants back to the destinationn canton and the relevant representation for operative document lists and timing, and a full survey of all cantonal student pages was not completed in the retrieved record. The safe federal-level conclusion is that canton-specific practice exists and should not be generalised without local confirmation. 29
