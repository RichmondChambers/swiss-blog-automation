# Executive legal summary

Swiss "student permits" are not a self-standing visa category in the way the phrase is often used in general commentary. In Swiss law, the central distinction is between **entry** **permission** **and** **residence** **status.** For stays without gainful activity of more than three months, the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act requires a residence authorisation; if the person is visa-required, a national **type** **D** **visa** is ordinarily the entry mechanism for a longer study stay, but the underlying legal status remains a cantonally issued short-stay or residence permitfor a specific purpose. Short study stays of up to three months may fall outside the residence-permit requirement, although a visa can still be required depending on nationality. 1

The framework is split between twvo legal logics. For **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the starting point is the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and the ordinance/directive framework implementing it. FNIA is only subsidiary where the treaty regime does not provide otherwise or where FNIA is more favourable. EU/EFTA students are treated within the broader category of economically inactive persons: if they have sufficient means, full sickness and accident insurance, and are admitted to a recognised institution for their principal course of study, they have a treaty-based residence entitlement rather than a purely discretionary Article 27 FNIA case. Official SEM guidance states that, for persons in education, the permit is normally issued for one year at a time and renewed until ordinary completion so long as conditions remain met. 2

**For non-EU/EFTA nationals,** the route is governed principally by **Article 27 FNIA and Articles 23-24OASA.** Article 27 is framed in discretionary language: foreign nationals **may** be admitted for education or training if the institution confirms eligibility, suitable accommodation is available, the necessary funds are available, the applicant satisfies the personal and educational requirements, and, for minors, supervision is guaranteed. OASA develops this architecture by specifying acceptable forms of financial proof, by linking "personal requirements" to anti-abuse screening, by imposing an **ordinary** **eight-year** **cap** for education stays subject to justified exceptions, and by setting institutional requirements for schools. The SEM's 2026 directives make clear that this is not a mechanical "offer letter equals permit" route: authorities are expected to assess credibility, educational progression, language ability, risk of route abuse, and whether the institution and programme genuinely justify a study residence. 3

Permit type follows duration and purpose. Under FNIA, a **short-stay** **permit** L covers stays of up to one year; a **residence** **permit** B covers stays of more than one year. SEM guidance expressly applies that logic to study cases: educational stays exceeding one year are handled as B permits, while shorter

programmes are handled as L permits. Both remain purpose-bound and temporary. The temporary character is doctrinally important. Article 27(3) FNIA states that any continued stay after completion or discontinuation is governed by the **general admission** **rules,** and SEM guidance, supported by Federal Supreme Court case law cited in those directives, treats study residence as known from the outset to be temporary. 4

Work&lt; rights during studies are strictly ancillary. For third-country students, OASA permits side work only for students at Swiss universities or universities of applied sciences, normally **no** **earlier than** **six** **months** after studies begin, limited to **15** **hours per** **week** **outside** **holidays,** and only with an employer request plus compliance with Swiss pay and employment conditions. Mandatory internships are governed separately and may not exceed half the overall duration of the course. Postgraduate scientific work during postgraduate study is also separately regulated. None of this converts the route into an ordinary labour-migration route. 5

Post-study residence is a different legal question. Article 27 itself gives no automatic right to remain. A narrow facilitation exists for foreign nationals with a **Swiss** **university** **degree**: Article 21(3) FNIA permits a six-month period after graduation to find suitablework and relaxes the ordinary priority rule where the work is of high academic or economic interest. SEM guidance treats that six-month job-search stay as cantonal competence, not as an automatic bridge to long-term settlement. 6

For long-term planning, student residence should be treated cautiously. FNIA expressly classifies education/training residence as temporary for settlement-permit counting purposes, and SEM guidance explains that educational residence is only credited later under additional conditions after the person has transitioned into a durable residence pattern. The route can therefore be part of a broader immigration trajectory, but it should not be drafted as if it were itself a direct settlement route. 7

# Structural overview and table of legal propositions

**Legal nature** **of** **student** **residence.** Swiss law does not create a freestanding "student visa" status. The relevant legal architecture is: admission to Switzerland if needed by visa, followed by residence authorisation for a specific educational purpose if the stay exceeds three mmonths. For non-EU/EFTA nationals, the core legal basis is Article 27 FNIA. For EU/EFTA nationals, the primary architecture is free movement law, with FNIA operating only subsidiarily. 8

**EU/EFTA** **education** **residence.** EU/EFTA students are treated within the AFMP/VFP regime for economically inactive persons. They must show sufficient means and comprehensive health and accident insurance; for students specifically, official SEM guidance addds admission to a recognised institution for principal vocational or educational training. This is substantially more rights-based than Article 27 FNIA, although students remain subject to temporary-purpose logic and financial sufficiency checks. 9

**Non-EU/EFTA** **student** **admission.** Article 27 FNIA and Articles 23-24 OASA create a condition-heavy discretionary admission route. School confirmation, accommodation, finances, personal and educational suitability, and (for minors) supervision are cumulative conditions. The OASA and SEM directives add anti-abuse scrutiny, school-quality requiremnents, language and interview practice, and an ordinary eight-year outer limit. 10

**Permit** type **and** **temporary** **purpose.** The L/B distinction follows anticipated duration. SEM guidance applies that general permit logic to education stays. The student route is purpose-bound and

SEM guidance and cantonal practice add that authorities are alert to private schools, new institutions and non-serious programmes. Student residence differs sharply from ordinary work residence. In labour migration, the central questions are economic interest, employer sponsorship, labour-market protection and the applicant's professional qualifications for a job. In student residence, the stay is justified only because the educational project itself is accepted as real, coherent and temporary. That is why Article 27 FNIA is framed around school confirmation, accommodation, funds and personal/educational suitability rather than around quota and labour-priority rules. It is also why Article 27(3) immediately routes any post-study stay back into the general admission system. 46

Article 27 is therefore not an admissions-letter route. OASA turns "personal and educational requirements" into a substantive test, expressly stating that personal qualifications are not sufficient where previous stays, previous applications or other indicators suggest that the education route is being used merely to evade ordinary immigration rules. The SEM directives add the practical tools for that assessment: personal study plan, precise target diploma, CV, language-material review, and, where useful, a consular interview about future intentions and language ability. That combination explains why second studies, abrupt changes of course, weak educational progression or implausible motives can become refusal points even where a school has issued an offer. 47

Finances must also be analysed practically, not formally. The federal ordinance is permissive in the sense that it accepts several forms of proof, including Swiss-based sponsors, Swiss bank confirmations and scholarship support. But it does not reduce financial sufficiency to a single universal figure. On the EU/EFTA side, the benchmark is whether the person would become welfare-dependent by Swiss standards, with the VFP guidance linking that to Swiss social-assistance measures. On the third-country side, a file that is technically documented but economically implausible can remain weak, particularly once accommodation, tuition, food, insurance and non-classroom costs are considered. 48

School status and programme structure are central because Swiss immigration law does not give equal weight to every institution branding itself as "Swiss". OASA requires a suitable and fixed programme, with authorities able to restrict admissions to recognised schools. The safest official higher-education check is the Swissuniversities accreditation list. For private schools, the 2024 SEM circular is important because it narrows one part of the inquiry: a school in the Swiss Private School Register is presumed to satisfy the school-quality limb of Article 24 OASA, but that presumption is rebuttable and it does **not** relieve the authorities from checking the individual applicant's credibility, progression and finances. 49

Student work remains ancillary. The law does not treat study permits as hybrid work permits. Side work for third-country students is available only in the narrower higher-education sphere, only after the initial six-month period, only within time limitsoutside holidays, and only with an employer-side filing

<!-- 11 -->

plus compliance with Swiss employment conditions. Mandatory internships must be assessed separately. A course may contain a practical component, but that does not mean all internships are automatically permitted; the ordinance imposes its own cap and conditions. That is an important drafting distinction because non-compliant work can undermine both renewal and later transition applications. 5

Renewal is not automatic continuation. The federal texts do not create a self-renewing status: permits remain purpose-bound, and the study purpose must still exist. The ordinary eight-year framework in OASA confirms that the system expects progression and completion rather than indefinite educational residence. Cantonal forms that demand disclosure of changes of school or study plan are consistent with that architecture, and Zurich's and Geneva's practice materials show how closely cantons monitor those changes. 50

Post-study residence is best analysed as a fresh immigration problem. Article 27(3)makes that explicit for everyone, and Article 21(3) creates only a narrow facilitation for graduates of Swiss universities. Even there, the graduate receives time to look for suitable work, not a guaranteed work permit or an automatic bridge to permanent residence. This distinction matters in drafting because "graduate route" language borrowed from other jurisdictions can easily overstate Swiss law. 51

Cantonal and consular handling can materially affect outcomes even where the federal legal test is constant. The SEM directives expressly use foreign-representation interviews and comments as part of the fact-finding process, especially around language ability, credibility and future intentions. Cantons then implement the federal structure through local forms, filing channels, document lists and renewal practice. Internal drafting should therefore distinguish carefully between **federal legal conditions, SEM** **administrative practice, and cantonal procedure/evidence.** 52

Long-term planning should remain cautious. Article 34(5) FNIA and the SEM directives treat education/ training residence as temporary for settlement counting and later crediting. The SEM directives also cite Federal Supreme Court authority for the proposition that persons present on study permits cannot rely on the same private-life logic as persons whose residence was never known to be temporary from the outset. Student residence can still be strategically relevant-particularly where a Swiss university degree later opens Article 21(3) and then a durable work-based stay - but the student route itself should not be described as a normal direct track to C permit or citizenship. 24

## Drafting cautions and source list

## Drafting cautions

·Do not describe the route simply as a **"student** **visa"** without explaining that Swiss law distinguishes between an entry visa and a purpose-bound residence permit. 53

·Do not collapse **EU/EFTA** and **non-EU/EFTA** student cases into a single framework; the former are primarily analysed under AFMP/VFP, the latter under Article 27 FNIA / OASA. 54

·Do not imply that a **school admissions** **letter alone** is sufficient. Accommodation,funds, suitability, and the legitimacy/structure of the programme are all part of the legal test. 16

·Do not present Article 27 FNIA as an entitlement route. Its wording is discretionary, annd OASA expressly embeds anti-abuse review. 16

·Do not state that all students can work freely. Third-country student work is ancillary, delayed, capped, and permission-based; internships are separately regulated. 5

<!-- 12 -->

·Do not treat the **20-hours-per-week** full-time concept as if it were a statutory rule in FNIA or OASA; it is best treated as official administrative practice unless a more specific source is identified for the setting in question. 22

·Do not overstate **language** **requirements** by importing a fixed CEFR threshold for all students. The federal student route is based on school confirmation and justified authority checks, not a universally stated level. 20

· Do not assume that every private school is equivalent to an officially accredited Swiss higher-education institution. Check Swissuniversities for accredited higher education institutions and the SEM circular for the narrower Private School Register presumption. 36

·Do not omit the route's **temporary** **nature.** Continued stay after completion or discontinuation is governed by general admission law, not by Article 27 itself. 15

·Do not overstate the post-study position. The six-month graduate job-search facilitation is separate, limited, and tied to Swiss university graduates. 6

·Do not present cantonal filing requirements or evidence lists as if they were federal black-letter law. They are practice materials implementing federal law. 55

·Do not suggest that student residence automatically leads to **C** **permit** or **citizenship.** Settlement counting is qualified, and the route is treated as temporary. 24
