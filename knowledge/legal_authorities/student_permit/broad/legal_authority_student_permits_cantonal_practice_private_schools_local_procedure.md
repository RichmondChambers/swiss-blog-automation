## A.Executive legal summary

Swiss student-residence matters do not operate as a single, uniform "student visa" regime. The correct analytical split is between, first, **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** whose residence position is treaty-based under the Agreement on thne Free Movement of Persons and its implementing framework if the relevant conditions are met, and, secondly, **non-EU/EFTA** **nationals,** whose admission for education or training is governed primarily by the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act and the Ordinance on Admission, Residence and Employment. Official SEM guidance states expressly that, for third-country study cases, the governing federal provisions are Art. 27 **LEI** and **Arts. 23-24 OASA.** Thesame guidance also stresses that educational stays must not be used to evade stricter admission rules. 1

For **third-country** **nationals,** the route is discretionary in nature. SEM guidance summarising Federal Administrative Court case law states that **Art. 27 LEI does not confer a right to the grant of a permit** and that an application may be refused even if the formal conditions are met, as part of a restrictive admissions policy. Official guidance also confirms that study permits are strictly temporary and that holders cannot ordinarily rely on private-life protection under Article 8 ECHR to transform that temporary stay into a general residence claim. 2

By contrast, **EU/EFTA students and other economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals** are treated under the AFMP framework. SEM's official factsheet states that EU/EFTA citizens may reside in Switzerland without taking up gainful employment if they have sufficient means and full health insurance, and that persons undergoing education and training must also show admission to a recognised Swiss institution. The same factsheet makes a crucial boundary point: **admission** **to** **Swiss** **higher education** **institutions and scholarship decisions do not fall within the scope of the AFMP.** In other words, **the** treaty may ground residence, but it does not compel the school or university to admit the applicant. 3

Administration is highly federalised in execution. The **cantonal migration** **authority** is the principal case-handling body, but the exact local pathway differs. In Geneva, the OCPM centrally handles both the residents' register and permit functions. In Vaud, the communal residents' office and bureau des étrangers act as the reception and transmission point for permit files to the cantonal SPOP. In Zurich, new arrivals must register with the municipality within 14 days and, withinthat same period, apply for a residence permit if they do not already hold one. 4

Private schools are a recurrent scrutiny point. SEM's 16 May 2024 circular creates a **rebuttable** **presumption** that schools listed in the **Swiss** **Private** **School** Registersatisfy the institutional standard in Article 24 OASA, but it does **not** remove the need for individual scrutiny of the student's own qualifications under Article 27 LEI / Article 23 OASA. If concrete indications suggest the school no longer meets the standard, SEM recommends that no study permit should, in principle, be issued pending

clarification. The circular also preserves the continued application of cantonal rules on private-school admission and recognition. 5

Document handling is local and quite granular. Geneva requires, among other things, the school admission letter, a study plan stating weekly teaching hours and course content, proof of funds, accommodation evidence, CV, motivation letter, and a written undertaking to leave Switzerland after the planned studies, subject to a higher-education exception. Vaud's official form is even more explicit, requiring confirmation of academic and language suitability, evidence of first-semester fees, financial evidence in specified forms, accommodation proof, a signed departure undertaking, and special documentation for minors. Zurichn's practice note requires a detailed study programme,reasons for choosing Zurich, translated diplomas and work references, future plans after study, passport validity, and proof of funds and accommodation. 6

Where official thresholds and timelines are published, they vary. Zurich states that **CHF** **21,000** **for** **12months** is treated as sufficient for maintenance and appropriate accommodation. Vaud states that means are generally sufficient where the student has **about** **CHF** **2,000** **per** **month, plus** **housing** **costs,** if not boarding. Geneva publishes an average processing time of 6-8 **weeks** for a student-permit application, plus a separate **2-4** **weeks** for biometric appointments. Vaud states roughly **four** **weeks** **for** a complete application once received by SPOP, plus **two** **to** **three** **weeks** for visa transmission from the Swiss representation, with longer perioods in peak academic intake periods or where federal approval is required. 7

## B. Structural overview of the legal framework

At the federal level, the framework is layered. SEM's foreign-national directives identify the **AFMP** as the key bilateral instrument for EU nationals and explain that persons benefitting from free movement are only subsidiarily subject to the LEI where the AFMP and implementing rules do not regulate the matter, or where the LEI is more favourable. The same directives place **education** **and** **training** **for** **third-country nationals** under **Art. 27 LEI and Arts. 23-24 OASA.** OfficialSEM material separately identifies the principal RS references as **LEI, RS 142.20, OASA, RS 142.201,** and the **AFMP, RS 0.142.112.681.** 8

That federal framework is then executed through cantonal and local administration. Geneva's OCPM describes its foreign-service arm as the competent cantonal authority for examining, granting and renewing residence and work permits. Vaud's communal guidance describes the bureau des étrangers as the reception desk and transmission channel for permit requests to the cantonal SPOP. Zurich's migration-office page states that the cantonal office monitors and regulates the presence of foreign nationals in the canton. The structure is therefore federal in norm-making, but cantonal and often communal in intake, registration, file assembly and day-to-day case progression. 9

A third layer is the **school-recognition and education-administration layer.** SEM's directives and 2024circular explicitly involve cantonal departments of education, the Swiss representations abroad, cantonal migration services, communes and the Swiss Private School Register in the administration of study cases involving private schools. Zurich's practice note also shows direct reliance on school-level confirmation of admission, language competence and academnic suitability. This is why "student permit" case administration in practice is not just migration-law enforcement; it is also a recognition and quality-control exercise around the educational provider. 10

Finally, there is a federal **approval** / **oversight** layer: SEM's directives refer to the approval mechanism under **Art.** 99 **LEI** and **Art.** 85 **OASA.** Vaud's official study form says that the federal authority may take accepoted cantonal applications under its control and refuse entry notwithstanding cantonal approval. Zurich's practice note states that the cantonal migration office may submit a decision to SEM at any time and that SEM maintains a list of nationalities for which approval is required as a risk-control measure. The safest reading is therefore that cantonal administration is primary, but not always final. 11

## D and E. Primary legal texts and official guidance/ administrative materials

D. **Primary** **legal texts.** For third-country cases, the operative statutory and ordinance framework is the **Federal Act on Foreign Nationals and Integration (LEI/ AIG / FNIA, RS 142.20)** together with the **Ordinance on Admission, Residence and Employment (OASA / VZAE, RS 142.201).** Official SEM guidance expressly links study admissions to **Art.** 27 **LEI** and institutional / personal suitability to **Arts.** **23-24** **OASA.** Vaud's official study form cites the same provisions as the legal basis for temporary study residence. 21

For EU/EFTA cases, the relevant primary instrument is the **Agreement** **on** **the** **Free** **Movement** of **Persons** **(AFMP,** RS **0.142.112.681)** together with its implementing framework, including the **Ordinance** **on the Free Movement of Persons (OLCP, RS 142.203).** SEM's factsheet and permit pages make clear that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals, including students, may obtain residence if they have sufficient funds and insurance, and, for study cases, if they are admitted to a recognised institution. The factsheet also refers expressly to **Art. 18(2) OLCP** in relation to sufficient funds for a job-seeking short stay,confirming the implementing-ordinance layer in the AFMP system. 22

The federal approval layeralso matters. SEM's directives refer to **Art.** 99 **LEI** **and** **Art.** 85 **OASA** as the basis for SEM approval in reserved categories, and cantonal material from Vaud and Zurich confirms that study cases may, in practice, be delayed or escalated because of federal approval requirements. That point should be framed carefully: it is not correct to say that every study file requires SEM approval, but it is equally unsafe to ignore SEM approval as a live procedural variable. 11

E. **Official** **guidance** **/** **administrative** **materials.** The most useful federal administrative source is the **SEM** **Directives** I. **Domaine** **des** **étrangers,** especially chapter 5.1 on education and continuing education. Those directives cover purpose of stay, anti-circumvention logic, personal qualifications, school confirmation, course intensity, permit type, work alongside study, school-register issues and selected case law. The associated **SEM** **circular** **of** 16 **May** **2024** gives the best current official federal material on the treatment of private schools in the Swiss Private School Register. 23

For the EU/EFTA side, the most useful federal administrative materials are the **SEM** **factsheet** **on** **residing in Switzerland without gainful activity** and the **SEM page on the B permit for EU/EFTA** **nationals.** Together they draw the line between a general five-year EU/EFTA B permit for non-active persons and the more study-specific residence logic for those undergoing education and training, including the need for sufficient means, full insurance and institutional admission. 14

## F. Cantonal practice notes

This section is **illustrative** **rather** **than** **exhaustive.** It identifies selected cantonal and local materials that show meaningful procedural variation.

**Geneva.** Geneva's public student-permit portal is highly operationalised. It differentiates between EU/ EFTA and third-country study routes, allows filing online or by post, and lists a detailed third-country evidence package: ID, photo, school admissibility attestation, CV, motivation letter,study plan with weekly teaching hours and programme detail, departure undertaking, proof of funds or support undertaking, accommodation evidence, and additional material for stage/activity or prior cantonal residence. Geneva also states that study poermits are issued for the course duration if under one year, otherwise for one year renewable, that such permits are strictly temporary, and that only studyor training of up to eight years is in principle admitted. It publishes an average processing time of 6-8**weeks** for student permits and **2-4** **weeks** for biometric appointments. 24

**Vaud.** Vaud remains form-driven. Its official study-application form for third-country nationals is internally valuable because it states, in one document, the local evidence list, the approximate processing time, the indicative financial level, the role of the canton, the possibility of federal intervention, the obligation to report changes in the study plan, and the requirement to announce oneself to the communal authority when already in Switzerland. It also shows a local administrative feature noot always visible elsewhere: the student may authorise the school to follow the permit or visa application administratively. Vaud's communal guidance further shows that the bureau des étrangers and residents' office are not merely passive registrars; they receive, assemble and transmit permit files to SPOP and maintain the local residents' register under SPOP supervision. 25

**Zurich.** Zurich provides the clearest published cantonal practice note. It treats schools in the Swiss Private School Register as presumptively compliant with Article 24 OASA, but it also lists extra questions for new private schools, including courses offered, interim examinations, diplomas issued and teaching staff. Zurich's guidance is particularly strict on language-school cases: only **full-time** **German** **intensive** **courses of at least 20 lessons per week** are recognised, they are granted for at most **12 months,** and permits are not issued for the study of other languages in that "isolated" language-school model. Zurich also expressly publishes a local financial benchmark of **CHF** **21,000** **for** **12** **months,** a documentary list for initial and renewal cases, a written return undertaking for non-Swiss-higher-education cases,and a local practice on cross-cantonal study residence, allowing residence in Zurich with studies in another canton only where travel time is no more than two hours. Its practice note also records that SEM approval may be required for cases involving certain nationalities. 26

**Ticino.** Ticino's student pages show a simpler public mnodel but still reveal important local points. For study stays of **less** **than** **one** **year,** the page statesthat an **L** **permit** must be requested **within** **14** **days;** for stays of **more** **than** **one** **year,** a **B** **permit** must be requested **before** **entry.** Ticino also publicly states that students who **return** **daily** to **a** **residence** **abroad** do not need a residence permit and may attend public or private institutions in the canton without a specific migration-office authorisation. In a separate notice to third-country holders of L/B study permits, Ticino requires address, nationality, name, civil-status and family-composition changes to be notified within **14** **days,** and foreign departure to be notified at least **14 days in advance.** 27

## G and H. Practical legal analysis and drafting cautions

G. **Practical legal analysis.** The safest internal model is a **four-stage** **pipeline.** First, identify the legal lane:AFMP-based EU/EFTA residence or third-country Article 27 LEI discretion. Secondly, identify the institutional lane: federally/cantonally recognised higher education, recognised vocational institution,or private school / language school. Thirdly, identify the local intake lane: centralised cantonal handling, communal registration plus cantonal decision, or mixed models. Fourthly, identify the federal oversight lane: whether the case is likely to remain purely cantonal or may require, or be referred for, SEM approval. Treating those four questions as separate prevents the common drafting error of compressing everything into a single "student visa" pathway. 28

School scrutiny is not just a gatekeeping function; it conditions the entire evidence package. SEM and cantonal guidance show that the authority wants to see a real, structured, time-bounded educational project: weekly hours, course content, total duration, intended diploma, prior qualifications,language competence, continuity with prior education or career, and a plausible post-study plan. That explains why CVs, motivation letters, translated diplomas, and detailed study plans recur across cantons.The task is not only to prove admission to a school; it is to prove that the **project** **is** **academically** **coherent** **and migration-law compliant.** 29

Private-school cases require a further split between **institutional reliability** and **individual suitability.** The SEM circular gives schools listed in the Swiss Private School Register a presumption of adequacy under Article 24 OASA, but that presumption is expressly rebuttable and does not displace scrutiny of the foreign national underArticle 27 LEI / Article 23 OASA. Internal drafting should therefore treat "registered private school" as one positive factor, not as a complete solution. Where the school is new, niche, or only privately certified, expect more education-department involvement, more document requests and a greater risk of SEM or cantonal escalation. 10

**The** **commune** **dimension** is often underestimated. Vaud's materials show the commune as a formal intake and file-transmission actor; Zurich requires prompt municipal registration and permit application; Geneva centralises arrival notification at OCPM but still treats registration as a residents'-register matter. This matters for content systems because a page that accurately states the residence-law test may still be operationally incomplete if it does not tell the user where and when local registration must occur, or whether a change of address, school, canton or household must be separately notified. 30

H. **Drafting** **cautions.** Do **not** write as if Switzerland has one generic "student visa" route. For third-country nationals, the visa is only the **entry** **instrument** where required; the substantive issue is the cantonal / federal grant of an L **or B residence permit for education or training.** For EU/EFTA nationals, the correct framing is residence under the AFMP / EU-EFTA permit system, usually after registration. Using one umbrella label obscures materially different legal basesand levels of discretion. 31

Do **not** imply entitlement for third-country nationals. SEM's own directives, drawing on FAC case law, say that Article 27 LEI does not create a right to issuance, and restrictive-admissions logic remains relevant even when formal conditions appear satisfied. This point should be stated expressly anywhere the system generates explanatory or comparative content. 13

Do **not** use school recognition as a surrogate for immigration-law eligibility. A school may be on the Private School Register, or locally recognised, and the student may still fail on personal qualifications, financial sufficiency, accommodation, anti-circumvention indicators, or departure credibility. Conversely, for EU/EFTA cases, admission to the institution is still a separate question from the residence right. 32

Do not rely on one national documentary list, onenational financial amount or one national processing time.Officially published amounts and timings are canton-specific in the materials reviewed. Zurich and Vaud publish financial guidance, Geneva publishes student-specific average times, and other cantons may publish only generic process pages or no student-specific timings at all. Content should therefore either stay general or attribute local detail very specifically to the relevant canton.7

Do not forget the "change" triggers. Geneva requires immediate reporting of change of school or study plan. Vaud requires immediate reporting of study-plan modification, school change or extension. Ticino requires address and civil-status changes to be notified within 14 days. SEM's private-school circular and directives also assume active reporting of irregularities and address changes between schools, communes and migration authorities. For a content system, this means that any page on renewals, transfers, interruption, part-time work, or extension should be linked to a "fresh authorisation / immediate notification may be required" rule. 33

**Open** **questions** **/** **limitations.** This pack is based on high-confidence official SEM and cantonal materials, but it is not a complete canton-by-canton survey. In addition, the web interface used here did not yield a clean extract of every live **Fedlex** provision in full text. The underlying article references and RS numbers were, however, verified through official SEM and cantonal materials. Any litigation-sensitive or citation-heavy drafting should still be checked against the current consolidated texts on **Fedlex** for LEI (RS 142.20), OASA (RS 142.201), OLCP (RS 142.203) and the AFMP (RS 0.142.112.681). 34
