# A Executive legal summary

For **non-EU/EFTA** **nationals,** study residence and student work are not one single "student visa" entitlement. Residence for education is governed principally by **Article** **27** **FNIA/AIG** (admission for education or continuing training), while student work sits in the **derogation** **framework** **of** **Article** **30(1)(g) AIG** and the implementing rules in **Articles** **38** to **40** **VZAE/OASA.** That structure matters:the legal test is not "students may work", but rather that the competent authority **may** **authorise** specified categories of work if the statutory conditions are met. 1

The core federal rule for **ancillary work during studies is Article 38 VZAE.** **It** applies to foreign nationals undertaking education or continuing training at a **Swiss** **university** **or** **university** **of** **applied** **sciences.** Ancillary work may be authorised **no earlier than six months after the start of studies,** only if the school confirms that the work is compatible with the course and will not delay completion, the student works **no more than 15 hours per week outside holidays,** the **employer files the** **application,** and the work complies with Swiss **salary** **and** **employment-condition** requirements. SEM's current guidance adds that the studies must remain the **main** **purpose** **of stay,** that the route must not be used to bring people to Switzerland chiefly to work, and that work in the **semester** **holidays** may be authorised on a **full-time** basis if the institution gives written consent. 2

**Mandatory internships** are legally distinct. They fall under **Article** **39** **VZAE,** not Article 38.Accordingly, the federal text for mandatory internships does **not** impose the six-month waiting period. Instead,the internship may be authorised where it forms part of a **full-time** **course,** does **not exceed** **half** of **the** **total training duration,** the **employer applies,** salary and working conditions comply with **Article 22AIG,** and the student has suitable accommodation. SEM's directives add that the programme and diploma must be recognised by the relevant supervisory authority, that the training must remain the main purpose of the stay, and that **pre-study** **internships** required for admission are generally **not** authorisable under Article 39 and should in principle be completed abroad. 3

For **higher-education continuing training and specialist work, Article 40 VZAE** allows employment during further training at a university or university of applied sciences where the work lies inthe person's **scientific specialism,** the **employer applies,** pay and conditions comply with **Article 22 AIG,** and the continuing training is not hindered. SEM guidance treats this as the special route for doctoral candidates, postdoctoral researchers, scholarship holders, academic guests and certain **Master** of **Advanced** **Studies** programmes. For MAS students, SEM states that field-specific work may proceed where it forms part of or is related to the course and does not delay completion. 4

**For EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the legal position is structurally different. Their student residence comes from the **Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons** and **Annex I, Article 24:** student residence is granted where the person is enrolled in a recognised institution, has sufficient financial resources and comprehensive sickness insurance, with a permit for the **duration** **of the** **studies** or for **one** **year** if **the** **course** **exceeds** **one** **year,** renewable to completion. The AFMP text itself does **not** reproduce the six-month waiting period or the 15-hour cap found in Article 38 VZAE. Where EU/EFTA nationals take up gainful employment, SEM's AFMP material treats this under the **worker** **regime**: employment of up to **three months per calendar year** is handled through the **online notification procedure,** and longer employment is dealt with by permit issuance on proof of employment, with geographic and occupational mobility. 5

A cautious drafting point follows. Some cantonal pages, especially **Geneva,** present an "ancillary activity" workflow for student permit holders that applies a **15-hour** / **40-hour** **holiday** cap even to **EU/** **EFTA** **students.** That is an important **cantonal practice** **note,** but it should **not** be elevated into a general federal AFMP proposition without canton-specific sourcing. 6

# B Structural overview of the legal framework

At federal level, the framework is layered. For **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the starting point is the **AFMP** and its annexes, especially **Annex** I, **Article** 24 on economically inactive persons and students, plus the AFMP's general worker-rights architecture. The **AIG/FNIA** applies only subsidiarily where the treaty does not govern the matter or where Swiss law is more favourable. For **non-EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the starting point is the **AIG/FNIA,** especially **Article** **27** for education stays and **Article** **30** for derogations from ordinary admission requirements. 7

The implementing ordinance for non-EU/EFTA study-related work is the **VZAE/OASA,** especially **Articles** **23** **to** **24** on study residence conditions and school requirements, and **Articles** **38** **to** **40** on ancillary work, mandatory internships and employment in a scientific specialist field during higher-education continuing training. These provisions are more precise than the Act and should carry the main weight in the content system's rule logic. 8

The general work-permit rules remain relevant because the student-work provisions expressly retain some of them. **Article** **11** **AIG** makes gainful activity permit-based regardless of duration and states that, for employed work, the permit is to be applied for by the **employer. Article** 22 **AIG** requires compliance with salary and working conditions customary for the locality, profession and sector. **Article** **24** **AIG** requires suitable accommodation. Articles 38 to 40 VZAE then selectively import those requirements into the student-work setting. 9

A further structural point is definitional. Swiss migration law interprets **gainful activity** **broadly.** Under **Article** **1a** **VZAE,** employed gainful activity includes work for an employer even if it is only hourly, daily or temporary, and expressly includes activity as an **intern** **or volunteer.** SEM's directives add that the distinction is objective and cite case law for the proposition that activity can count as gainful even when unpaid or only minimally remunerated. This is important for internship drafting, because "unpaid" does not take an arrangement outside migration control by itself. 10

# Primary legal texts and official guidance

# D Primary legal texts

The most important **primary federal** **legislative** **texts** for this topic are the following. First,Article 27**AIG** governs admission for education and continuing training. Secondly, **Article** **30(1)(g) AIG** authorises derogations from the ordinary admission requirements to facilitate international economic, scientific and cultural exchange and professional education/training; **Article 30(2) AIG** delegates the framework and procedure to the Federal Council. Thirdly, **Articles** **11,** 18, 22 **and** **24** **AIG** provide the general architecture for permit requirement, employer-filed applications for employed work, labour-condition review and accommodation. 19

The key ordinance provisions are **Articles** **23** **to** **24** **VZAE** **and** **Articles** **38** to **40** **VZAE.** Article 23 covers proof of financial means, abuse-prevention concerns and the general rule that education and continuing training are usually authorised for a maximum of **eight years,** with work governed by Articles 38 to 40. Article 24 sets requirements for schools, including structured programmes and confirmation of the student's linguistic and educational prerequisites. Articles 38 to 40 then create the three distinct study-work categories**: ancillary work, mandatory internships,** and **scientific specialist** **employment during higher-education continuing training. 8**

For **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the decisive primary text is the **AFMP.** At treaty level, the AFMP states that persons not exercising gainful activity receive residence rights according to **Annex** I, **and** **Annex** I, **Article** **24(4)** specifically regulates students: the student must demonstrate sufficient means, recognised enrolment and comprehensive insurance; **Article** **24(5)** provides annual renewal for the remaining period of study. The treaty also gives EU/EFTA workers equal access and mobility rights once they qualify under the worker regime. 20

# E Official guidance and administrative materials

The most authoritative administrative source is SEM's current **FNIA/AIG** **directives,** Chapter 4, in force with status **1 January** **2026.** For Article 38, SEM states that the study programme must remain the **main** **purpose** **of stay,** that the route must not be used to bring people to Switzerland mainly for work, that changes of employer remain authorisation-bound, that a foreign bachelor graduate starting a Swiss master in the same or a similar subject may work at the relevant institute **without** the six-month waiting period, and that full-time work in semester holidays may be authorised with the institution's

written consent. SEM also states that evening-school students, language-school students and persons taking a language course before their regular studies may not take ancillary employment under this route. 21

**For mandatory internships,** SEM's directives sharpen the federal rule: Article 39 is intended for schools offering **full-time** **vocational education,** the programme and diploma should be recognised by the relevant supervisory body, the internship must be mandatory under the school or study programme, and anything longer than half the course ceases to count as a school programme and becomes an apprenticeship or other work arrangement. SEM also states that internships required **before** **admission** to a professional or higher school are not authorisable under this provision and should generally be completed abroad. 22

**For Article** **40,** SEM's directives explain that the route is for persons who continue higher-education studies or research beyond basic university education in view of a **specialisation.** The guidance gives operational rules for doctoral candidates, postdoctoral researchers, scholarship holders, academic visitors and MAS students. For doctoral candidates, SEM accepts related work at or outside the university if it is linked to the dissertation and does not delay completion, and it permits ancillary work not related to the dissertation up to **15** **hours per** **week** outside the university if itdoes not delay completion. For MAS students, field-specific work may proceed where it forms part of or relates to the programme and does not delay completion. 23

SEM's public **FAQ** **on** **work** restates the non-EU/EFTA ancillary-work rule in English and confirms the practical elements that matter most for an operational content system: a foreign student at a Swiss university or university of applied sciences may work **no sooner** **than** **six** **months** after the start of the course, for a **maximum** **of** 15 **hours** **per** **week** **outside** **holidays,** with a university confirmnation,an **employer** **application** and compliance with pay and working conditions. The same FAQ states that an internship requires authorisation and that, where the intern is studying abroad, the internship must form an **integral** part of the studies. 24

Relevant case law appears mainly through SEM's own guidance and later federal explanatory materials. SEMI's Chapter 4 directives cite **ATF** **110** **Ib** **63** for the objective and broad concept of gainful activity. For post-study employment based on a Swiss higher-education qualification, the SEM consultation report on the 2021 reform project expressly relies on **BVGer** **C-674/2011** and **C-3859/2014** for the interpretation of "high scientific or economic interest", taking a deliberately broad view of "scientific interest". Those decisions matter more for **post-study** **employment** than for **ancillary** **work** **during** **studies,** but they are relevant wherever the content system touches continuing training and specialist work after or around advanced studies. 25

# F Cantonal practice notes

**Zurich** currently restates the federal third-country rules in a relatively clean way. Its official page says non-EU/EFTA students at a Swiss university or university of applied sciences may obtain ancillary work authorisation **from** **six** **months** **after** **the** **start** of **studies,** if the school confirms compatibility and no delay, the work remains within **15** **hours** **per** **week** **outside** **holidays,** and the **employer** files the application. Zurich aIso states that students enrolled in Switzerland may be authorised for a **mandatory** **internship** or for writing their final thesis with a Swiss company if a training programme is submitted, the work does not exceed **half** **of** **the** **total** **training** **duration,** and salary and employment conditions are respected. The same page adds that pre-study internships before admission to a Swiss higher-education institution should **as** a **rule** **be** **completed** **abroad.** 26

**Geneva** is particularly useful as a procedural source. Its current student-permit pages require an **application** for "ancillary activity" by student permit holders and list the documents to be attached, including a form signed by both student and employer and, for non-EU/EFTA students, an approval letter from the Swiss higher-education institution. **Vaud** has an official student information sheet and current work-permit form that together remain useful for practice. The student sheet states that accessory work may be authorised **at** **the** **earliest** **six** **months** **after** **the** **start** of studies,that the school must confirm it does not delay the training,that the limit is 15 **hours per** **week** or full time during school holidays, and that the activity must be **notified** in **advance** to the Population Service and authorised by the cantonal Employment Service.The same sheet says that mandatory internships are authorised only for students in a school providing **full-time** **vocational training** and that the aggregate duration of internships must not exceed **half** of **the** **training** **duration.** Vaud's work form requires employer details, weekly hours, salary, the employer's signature, and annexed **employment contract,** and expressly states that employment may start **only** **after** the cantonal decision. The caution here is chronologicaI: the Vaud information sheet is older than the Geneva and Zurich pages, but it remains an official cantonal material that aligns with the federal model. 28

# G Practical legal analysis

The content system should first split the subject into **two legal branches**: **EU/EFTA** **and** **non-EU/EFTA.** That is not a cosmetic distinction. EU/EFTA student residence is treaty-based and centres on enrolment, insurance and self-sufficiency under **AFMP** **Annex** I, **Article** **24.** Non-EU/EFTA study residence is a domestic-law permit under **Article** **27** **AIG,** with work allowed only through the special derogation scheme in **Articles** **38** **to** **40 VZAE.** Treating the topic as a single "student permit" framework would flatten a legally important dividing line. 29

Within the **non-EU/EFTA** branch, the next split should be among **ancillary** **work,** **mandatory** **internships,** and **scientific-specialist work during higher-education continuing training.** The six-month waiting period is a rule of **Article** **38** only. It should therefore be applied to ordinary ancillary student employment at a university or university of applied sciences, but **not automatically** **copied** into **Article 39 mandatory internships or Article 40 specialist work.** That is the single most important drafting correction to many oversimplified summaries of Swiss student work law1. 30

For **holiday work,** the safest formulation is this: for non-EU/EFTA students under **Article** **38** VZAE,the ordinance caps work at 15 **hours per** **week** **outside** **holidays;** SEM currently interprets the holiday period as allowing **full-time** **employment** if the educational institution gives **written** **consent.** That is strong official guidance, but it remains guidance-level elaboration of an ordinance that is framed negatively by reference to the non-holiday period The system should therefore avoid coding holiday full-time work as an unconditional statutory right. 31

**For employer-side** **procedure,** the system should treat the employer application as mandatory wherever the work is employed gainful activity. That follows both from **Article** **11(3)** **AIG** in general and from the student-work provisions themselves. The same logic applies to **changes** **of** **employer**: SEM expressly says that the ordinary worker mobility rule in **Article** **38(2)** **AIG** does **not** extend to people

<!-- 6 -->

holding a study permit, so a change of employer dduring ancillary work remains authorisation-dependent. 32

**For salary** **and** **conditions,** conservative drafting should say more than "salary must be lawful". The controlling rule is that the work must comply with **local, occupational and** **sectoral standards** under **Article** **22 AIG,** and that requirement is expressly repeated in **Articles** **38** **to** 40 **VZAE.** Because Swiss migration law also defines "gainful activity" broadly enough to cover internships and volunteering, the system should not assume that an internship may lawfully be unpaid merely because it is labelled educational. In many cases, the migration authorities will still ask whether the arrangement satisfies the wage-and-condition test applicable to the relevant route. 33

**For** **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** the safest operational phrasing is that there is **no** **federal treaty** **rule** **equivalent to Article 38 VZAE's six-month wait and 15-hour cap.** Instead, EU/EFTA students may reside as students if they satisfy the AFMP student conditions, and where they take up gainful employment the applicable regime is typically the AFMP worker framework, under which short work is notified and longer work is permit-based on proof of employment. That said, the content system should reserve room for **cantonal practice** **notes** where a canton, like Geneva, administers "student ancillary activity" through its own student-permit workflow and announces hour caps in that setting. 34

# H Drafting cautions

Do not describe this area as a single Swiss "student visa" framework. For non-EU/EFTA nationals, residence for study, ancillary work, mandatory internships, advanced specialist work, post-study job search and post-study qualified employment rest on **different** **legal provisions** with different conditions. For EU/EFTA nationals, the governing source is the **AFMP,** not the non-EU/EFTA student-work derogation scheme. 35

Do **not** use entitlement language for non-EU/EFTA student work. The operative verbos in the AIG and VZAE are discretionary: the student **may** **be** **admitted** for study and the work **may** **be** **authorised** if conditions are met. Internal drafting should therefore prefer formulations such as "may **be** **authorised", "subject to approval",** and **"requires employer application and school confirmation".** 36

**Do** **not** extend the **six-month** **waiting** **period** to every study-related work scenario. It clearly appears in **Article** **38** **VZAE** for ancillary work during higher education, but it does **not** appear in **Article** **39**mandatory internships or **Article** **40** specialist work during higher-education continuing training. 30

Do **not** state as a general federal proposition that **EU/EFTA** **students** are limited to **15** **hours** **per** **week.** Geneva's current studdent-permit pages say so for that canton's ancillary-activity process,but the treaty text and SEM's general AFMP worker materials do not formulate an AFMP-wide student-hours rule in those terms. If a canton-specific page is being summarised, label it clearly as **cantonal** **practice.** 6

Do **not** simplify "holiday work" into an automatic right to full-time work. The federal ordinance fixes the cap **outside** **holidays;** SEM guidance says full-time holiday work may be authorised with the institution's written consent. The better drafting is therefore **"full-time** **work** **during** **holidays** **may** **be** **authorised** **under SEM guidance if the institution consents in writing".** 37

Do **not** overlook the possibility that apparently educational placements are still migration-law **gJainful** **activity.** Internships and volunteering are drawn into the definition broadly, and the wage-and-condition rules remain relevant. Equally, do not treat **pre-study** **internships** in Switzerland as generally available under Article 39, because SEM's guidance says those are, as a rule, to be completed abroad. 38
