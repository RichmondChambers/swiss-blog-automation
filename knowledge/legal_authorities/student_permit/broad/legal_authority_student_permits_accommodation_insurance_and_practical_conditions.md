## Executive legal summary

Swiss student residence is not a single undifferentiated "student visa" route. The first structural divide is between **EU/EFTA** **nationals,** whose position is governed principally by the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons and its implementing practice, and **non-EU/EFTA** **nationals,** whose student stay is governed principally by **Article** 27 **AIG/FNIA** and the OASA/VZAE. For non-EU/EFTA students, Article 27 is framed as a **discretionary** ground of admission - the foreign national may be admitted if the conditions are met-and SEM guidance, citing case law, expressly notes that Article 27 AIG does not confer an entitlement even where the statutory criteria are satisfied. By contrast, SEM states that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals, including students, have a **right** to enter and stay if treaty conditions are met, notably sufficient means and full health and accident cover. 1

**For non-EU/EFTA students,** suitable accommodation is an **express federal admission criterion.** Article 27 AIG requires accommodation "appropriate to needs", sufficient funds, school connfirmation and the necessary educational/personal qualifications; for minors, adequate care must also be guaranteed. Federal and cantonal materials show that acceptable evidence is not confined to one housing model: it may be a private lease, sublease, host declaration, student-room allocation by the school, or boarding-school accommodation where applicable. The public materials reviewed do **not** support reducing the accommodation test to a single nationwide room metric; instead, they show a legally general federal standard operationalised by canton-specific documentary checks. 2

**On** **health** **insurance,** the baseline federal rule is that anyone settling in Switzerland must take out compulsory health insurance within **three** **months** of taking up residence, unless a lawful exemption applies. BAG/FOPH states that EU/EFTA students who are **not** **working** and remain insured in the social security system of their state of residence do not have to take out Swiss health insurance and may receive treatment with the EHIC; if they start work, Swiss insurance becomes required under the place-of-work principle. For students from outside EU/EFTA/UK, BAG/FOPH states that an exemption may be granted **on** **application** where equivalent private cover exists, normally for three years and extendable for a further three; after that, Swiss insurance is required. Accident cover must also be addressed: economically inactive EU/EFTA persons mnust be fully covered for health and accident risks, and students who are not fully insured under the accident-insurance legislation must keep accident cover in their health insurance. 3

Residence address and practical compliance matter throughout the student stay. Under federal migration rules, foreigners who need a permit for stay without gainful employment must register **within** **14** **days after** **entry** where the stay exceeds three months; movers must notify the new commune and de-register from the old one within the relevant time limits; permit holders must notify departure to another commune, canton or abroad. If the student changes canton, the rules are materially different from a mere intra-cantonal address update: a holder of an L or B permit who wants to transfer residence to another canton must first obtain authorisation from the **new canton.** Renewal is also structured, not informal: at federal level, an extension application must generally be filed no later than **14 days before expiry** and may be filed up to **three months in advance.** 4

# Structural overview of the legal framework

The legal framework is best read in **three** **layers.** First, there is the **nationality** **route:** EU/EFTA nationals benefit from the treaty framework on free movement and residence without ggainful activity; non-EU/ EFTA nationals rely on the ordinary foreign-nationals regime, especially Article 27 AIG and OASA provisions governing education and training. Second, there is the **subject-matter** **layer**: migration admission and residence, mandatory health insurance and accident cover, and local registration/ address administration are legally distinct even though they interact in practice. Third, there is the **cantonal implementation** **layer**: the federal legislation states the legal criteria, but cantonal migration offices and cantonal health-insurance authorities specify the documentary package and day-to-day procedure. 5

For non-EU/EFTA students, the key federal rule is Article 27 AIG. OASA/VZAE then fleshes this out with rules on acceptable proof of funds, school requirements, duration of study stays, change of purpose and renewal timing. SEM's 2026 AIG directives add interpretive guidance on abuse-prevention, temporary character, full-time study, the role of accommodation, the treatment of minors and the effect of case law. Cantonal materials then show which documents are actually expected in practice, such as a lease, a host declaration, school-room confirmation, bank statement or guarantee form. 6

For EU/EFTA students, the residence analysis should begin from the **treaty-based** **residence** **of** **economically inactive** **persons,** not from Article 27 AIG. SEM's AFMP material states that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals have a right to live in Switzerland if they have sufficient means and full health insurance, and its student-specific factsheet adds that persons undergoing education and training must also show admission to a recognised institution and ability to attend the programme. The health-insurance analysis then runs in parallel through BAG/FOPH, which treats non-working EU/EFTA students as remaining in their home system if the coordination conditions are met. 7

## Official guidance and cantonal practice notes

SEM's **2026** **AIG** **directives** are the most useful federal interpretive source for non-EU/EFTA student residence. They state that student applicants must file a personal study plan, identify the exact educational objective, and show educationaI and language suitability; they also state that a student permit may not be refused **solely** **because** **of** **age,** citing **BGE** **147** I 89. The same directives emphasise the temporary character of study residence, note that a further stay after studies needs a new permit basis, and cite case law that Article 27 AIG confers no **entitlement.** They further state that applicants must show a needs-appropriate dwelling, and that full-time schools include **boarding** **schools.** 22

SEM's AFMP material should be used as the federal guidance anchor for **EU/EFTA students.** It states that economically inactive EU/EFTA nationals have a right to live in Switzerland if they have adequate means and full health insurance including accidennts; it then says specifically that persons undergoing education and training must show sufficient means, admission to a recognised institution, and ability to attend the programme. It also records an important commuter point: students who do **not** take up residence in Switzerland and return daily to the EU/EFTA do not need a residence permit,unless they also take a part-time job in Switzerland. 23

<!-- 4 -->

**Zurich** is useful as a German-speaking practice note. The canton's public page for third-country nationals studying without gainful activity requires a detailed study programme, justification of the educational goal and the Zurich study location, secured re-departure, and proof of adequate means; Zurich states publicly that **CHF 21,000** **for** **twelve** **months** counts as sufficient funds for subsistence and that the confirmation must come from a Swiss-authorised bank. Zurich's practice paper further ties that amount to both subsistence and the "needs-appropriate accommodation", requires proof of accommodation on both first application and extension, states that minors under 18 are generally not admitted outside narrow exceptions, and requires care arrangements and parental consent where minors are admitted. 24

**Geneva** is useful as a French-speaking practice note. For third-country students, Geneva requires, among other documents, a study plan, admissibility letter, motivation letter, proof of means, and **either** **a copy of the lease or a host declaration undder form AL** with the principal lease and identity documents. Geneva's FAQ shows procedural flexibility where the student has **no** **definitive** **address** **yet**: the initial form may be returned without the address field completed, or with a temporary hotel/Airbnb address, but definitive address proof must later be produced and any permanent-address change notified. Geneva also states that any change of school or study plan requires a **new** **authorisation** and immediate notice to the cantonal office, and that third-country student-permit renewals should be filed no later than **15 days before expiry.** 25

**Vaud** is particularly useful for accommodation evidence. A Vaud student checklist states,under "conditions de logement", that students who are **not** in boarding school should indicate the planned place of accommodation by providing a **copy of a lease, confirmation from a host, or confirmation** **that** **the** **school** is **providing** **a** **room,** with the monthly rent stated. The same Vaud material indicates that, for that checklist, sufficient means are considered to be about **CHF** **2,000** **per** **month,** **plus** **housing** **costs.** This should be treated as **cantonal checklist** **practice,** not as nationwide federal law. 26

**Ticino** is useful for Italian-language practical conditions. Canton Ticino states that students staying for **less** **than** **one** **year** for study reasons must apply for an **L** **permit within** **14** **days** and that those staying **more** **than** **one** **year** must obtain a **B** **permit before** **entry.** Ticino also states, from the health-insurance side, that for holders of permits exceeding three months, the compulsory-insurance obligation begins when they **register with** **the** **residents' control office,** and that affiliation to the basic scheme must occur **even if the permit has not yet been issued.** 27

## Practical legal analysis

**Suitable** **accommodation** **as** **a** **legal criterion.** For non-EU/EFTA students, accommodation is not merely a practical convenience: it is an express federal admission criterion under Article 27 AIG. The statutory wording is deliberately broad- accommodation appropriate to the student's needs-and the OASA provisions focus elsewhere on funds, school quality and programme structure. That combination matters for drafting: the law requires **appropriateness,** while practice materials show that cantons usually test that requirement by asking for documentary proof of actual occupation rights and the real housing arrangement. It is therefore safer to state that the applicant must show **secured and** **suitable** **accommodation,** not to over-specify an unwritten national size standard. 28

<!-- 5 -->

**Private rentals, subletting and student residences.** The public materials reviewed support the proposition that Swiss student accommodation can be evidenced through several legally workable models. Geneva accepts a lease or, where the student is housed by another person, a host declaration under **form** **AL** plus the principal lease and identity documents. Vaud accepts a lease,host confirmation, or school room. Geneva's FAQ goes further by allowing an initial filing with no final address yet, or with a temporary hotel/Airbnb address, provided the definitive address is later supplied and the change is notified. These materials support a conservative internal rule: a studenthall contract, private lease, lawful sublease or host-family declaration can all work, but each should be documented in the format expected by the canton. 29

**Boarding-school accommodation.** Boarding schools are not a marginal category. SEM's directives expressly state that **boarding** **schools** **count** **as** **full-time schools.** Vaud's checklist likewise treats students "en internat" separately by carving them out from the ordinary housing-document section. The practical inference is straightforward: where the student is housed through the school itself, the accommodation evidence should usually be a **school accommodation confirmation** rather than a standard private lease. 30

**Accommodation** **for** **minors.** Federal law requires that somebody must provide adequate care for minors admitted for education or training. Zurich's practice note shows how this can become document-heavy in practice: where a child under 18 is taken into a household, a foster-care authorisation may be required; where minors are admitted exceptionally, the host or school leadership must undertake care in writing, and the parents must provide written consent. That is not the text of Article 27(2) itself; it is canton-level implementation of the "adequate care" requirement. Internal drafting should therefore separate **thefederal criterion** from **cantonal evidential implementation.** 31

**Health** **insurance** **and** **accident cover.** For immigration-content purposes, the safest rule is to start with compulsory insurance and then test for a lawful exemption. BAG/FOPH says that anyone settling in Switzerland must insure within three months after taking residence. EU/EFTA students who are not working and remain in their home social-security system do not need Swiss health insurance and may rely on the EHIC for treatment; if they begin working, the place-of-work principle brings them into Swiss insurance. For students from outside EU/EFTA/UK, exemption is possible on application only where equivalent private cover exists, normally for three years extendable by three.Accident cover should not be handled as an afterthought: economically inactive EU/EFTA residents must be fully covered for health and accident risks, and BAG/FOPH states that students or others working **less than** **eight** **hours** **a** **week** are not fully insured under compulsory accident insurance and therefore must keep accident cover through health insurance. 3

**Residence address, renewal and change of purpose.** Federal law and ordinance make address compliance part of the legal framework, not merely an administrative nuisance. For non-working stays exceeding three months, registration is required within 14 days after entry; on moving commune or canton, the person must register with the new authority and de-register from the old one within the prescribed period. A move to another canton is legally more substantial: under Article 37 AIG, an L or B holder who wants to transfer residence to another canton must first obtain authorisation from the new canton. On renewal, OASA requires filing at least 14 days before expiry, while Geneva publicly states 15days for student renewals. If the student wants to remain after completing studies, or materially changes school or study plan, the better view is that a **new** **permit** **basis** or at least a fresh cantonal authorisation analysis is required. 32

<!-- 6 -->

**Family** **members** **and** **household** **composition.** The reviewed materials show that family members matter in at least two distinct ways. Under the AFMP framework, economically inactive EU/EFTA persons have a family-reunification component if the relevant conditions are met. In health insurance,BAG/ FOPH's FAQ also treats some accompanying family members of non-EU students as potentially exempt from compulsory Swiss insurance if equivalent cover exists and exemption is granted.On accommodation, the clearest operational point is documentary rather thandoctrinal: where the student is housed by a host or in subletting, cantonal materials requiring the main lease and host/subtenant identity documents indicate that the **actual** **household** **arrangement** must be transparent. As a drafting inference, any internal template should make room to record who else occupies the address and on what legal basis the student is housed there. 33

## Drafting cautions

**Do not collapse everything into a "student visa" label.** For third-country nationals, the entry visa (where required) and the residence authorisation are distinct steps. For EU/EFTA nationals, the analysis is treaty-based residence and registration, not an Article 27 AIG student-permit analysis in the first instance. 34

**Do** **not** **write** **Article** **27** **AIG** **as** if it **creates** **a** **right.** The federal statute says the foreign national may be admitted, and SEM's AIG directives cite case law confirming that Article 27 AIG does not confer an entitlement. Use discretionary language such as "may beadmitted" or "is assessed under Article 27AIG", not "is entitled to admission". 35

**Do not import canton-specific financial figures into nationalI propositions.** Zurich's **CHF 21,000 / 12months** and Vaud's **aboout** **CHF** **2,000** **per** **month** **plus** **housing** are useful practice indicators, but they are not the wording of Article 27 AIG or OASA Article 23. Internal content should label them expressly as **cantonal practice notes.** 36

**Do not state that only a private lease counts as accommodation evidence.** Geneva and Vaud both accept host declarations; Vaud also accepts school accommodation; SEM treats boarding schools as full-time schools.Hosted, sublet and school-based housing can therefore be legally workable if properly documented. 37

**Do not treat minors as a standard student-accommodation scenario.** Federal law requires adequate care for minors, and Zurich practice shows that additional welfare and consent documents can become necessary. Any internal drafting on minors should flag the need for **separate** **canton-specific** **verification.** 31

**Do not say that Swiss health insurance is either always required or always unnecessary.** The legally correct position is conditional. BAG/FOPH allows exemptions in dlefined cases; EU/EFTA students not working may remain in their home system; non-EU students may obtain exemption only on application and only if equivalent cover exists; accident cover must also be checked separately. 38

**Do not assume post-study stay or study-plan changes are automatic.** Geneva requires immediate notice and fresh authorisation analysis for change of school or study plan1. Federal ordinance also requires a **new** **permit** when the purpose of stay changes. Post-study job search should therefore be handled as a **new** **legal** **basis,** not as passive continuation of the student stay. 39

**Do not conflate UK students with EU/EFTA residence rights.** BAG/FOPH groups UK students with EU/ EFTA students for some health-insurance coordination points, but SEM states that the AFMP nolonger applies to the UK from **1 January** **2021.** Internal immigration logic should therefore keep **UK** **residence** **analysis** **ooutside** **the** **AFMP** **student** **route,** even if insurance coordination may still resemble the EU/ EFTA position in some cases. 40
