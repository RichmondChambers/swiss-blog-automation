# Swiss Legal Authority Pack on EU and EFTA Self-Employment and Genuine Economic Activity in Switzerland

Internal authority pack for immigration-law content use. Scope: current Swiss law and official administrative practice on EU/EFTA nationals seeking residence in Switzerland as self-employed persons, with emphasis on residence rights, evidential expectations, and the distinction between genuine self-employment, employment, sham activity, and economically inactive residence. The analysis is based on the current AFMP framework, SEM guidance and selected official cantonal materials in force or current as at 24 May 2026. SEM also publishes separate materials on an updated AFMP that is **not** **yet** in **force;** those future-law materials should not be mixed into current-route content.1

## Executive legal summary

The core residence right for EU **nationals** who establish themselves in Switzerland as self-employed persons arises under the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons, especially Article 4 of the main Agreement and Annex I, Chapter III, Article 12. That provision grants a residence permit valid for at least five years to a national of a contracting party wishing to become established in the other state in order to pursue a self-employed activity, provided the person produces evidence to the competent authorities thnat he or she is established or wishes to become so; renewal is for at least five years on proof that a self-employed economic activity is being pursued. The same basic structure exists for **EFTA** **nationals** through Annex K to the EFTA Convention and Appendix 1, Chapter III, Article 11, and SEM states expressly that the same rules apply to EFTA citizens as to EU citizens, subject to special rules for Liechtenstein. 2

**In** **current Swiss administrativepractice,** however, the route is not treated as satisfied by a mere statement of future intention. SEM's 2026 VFP directives state that EU/EFTA nationals entering Switzerland to take up self-employment receive an initial permit only if, when the application is submitted, they can prove the self-employed activity; the directives add that, where there are serious doubts about the real and sustainable exercise of the activity and about the generation of a regular, subsistence-level income, the authorities may require further proof during the permit's validity or revoke it if the conditions are no longer met. SEMM's public factsheet and FAQ use the same operative language: a B EU/EFTA permit is issued if the person cann show an **effective** **self-employed** **activity,** and self-employment documents must show an activity that is **subsistence-generating** for the applicant and family members. 3

The decisive immigration-law test is therefore whether there is a **real, active** **and** **economically** **viable** **self-employed** **activity** in **Switzerland,** not merely a business shell. SEM's directives say that proof is supplied by establishing an undertaking or place of business in Switzerland with **effective** **and** **subsistence-generating** **business** **activity,** evidenced by business books, accounts, orders and similar materials. They also say that commercial-register entry is ggenerally expected for commercial undertakings or legal entities, but cannot be reqquired in every case, notably for certain liberal professions and artists. Thecantons may not impose prohibitive evidential hurdles, but they may insist on enough evidence to show active business operations, regular income and freedom from social-assistance dependence. 4

Swiss practice also insists on a real distinction between **self-employment and** **employment.** SEM's directives say the question is case-specific, but decisive factors are that the activity is carried on **for** **one's own** **account** **and** **at** **one's own** **riskk,** without being bound by third-party instructions, integrated into another undertaking's organisation, or placed in a subordinate relationship. SECO's official guidance uses materially the same indicators: investment, own name and own account, own premises, bearing costs and loss risk, freedom over working methods and hours, and work for multiple clients point towards self-employment; acting in another's name, following instructions, fixed hours, assigned workplace and regular work for the same employer point towards employment. 5

The route must also be kept distinct from both **cross-border service provision and economically** **inactive** **residence.** EU/EFTA nationals established in another EU/EFTA state may provide services in Switzerland for up to 90 days through the notification regime, but that is not the same as becoming established in Switzerland as a self-employed resident. For the service-provider route, SECO requires control documents such as the notification or permit, an A1 social-securityform, and a contract or client confirmation; this is specifically aimed at verifying self-employed status and detecting sham self-employment. By contrast, if a person in Switzerland is no longer genuinely self-employed and is not covered by some other AFMP status, the case moves towards the rules for persons not pursuing an economic activity, where sufficient means become central. 6

Failure, low income or cessation matters because SEM distinguishes the position of workers from that of self-employed or non-active persons. For self-employed persons who have ceased work and are not covered by another retained status, SEM states that **sufficient** **financial means** are a condition of residence; if such persons draw public social assistance, their residence right lapses and an existing permit may be withdrawn. At the same time,the treaty and SEM both preserve residence where the person is temporarily unable to work because of illness or accident. 7

For family members, SEM states that EU/EFTA nationals holding a Swiss short-stay or residence permit as employees, self-employed persons or economically inactive persons may bring qualifying family members, but **self-employed persons and economically inactive persons** must have the financial means necessary to support them, and suitable accommodation is required. The family members' rights are derivative and normally last only while the principal's underlying AFMP right continues. 8

## Structural overview of EU and EFTA self-employment and genuine activity

A. **Legal** **basis.** The residence right flows from the AFMP for EU nationals and from the EFTA Convention for EFTA nationals. The main operative treaty provisions are Article 4 AFMP and Annex I, Chapter III, Articles 12 to 15 AFMP for self-employed persons, mirrored for EFTA nationals in Annex K and Appendix 1, Chapter III, Articles 11 to 14. Article 7 AFMP and Article 7 Annex K also matter because they secure equal treatment, occupational and geographical mobility, post-activity stay rights and family rights. 9

B. **Implementing** **structure.** Swiss implementation is operationalised through the Ordinance on Free Movement of Persons and, in practice, through SEM's current VFP directives and public factsheets/FAQs. In this route, the directives are especially important because they state how the treaty standard is applied by cantonal authorities: proof of existing self-employment, effective and subsistence-generating activity, continuing review of genuineness, and loss of status if conditions cease to be met. 10

**C.Immigration-law** **question** **being** **asked.** The real question is not whether the person has opened a company in the abstract, but whether they have established a **genuine** **economic activity** in **Switzerland** that is being pursued independently, on their own account and risk, and at a level capable of supporting residence without reliance on social assistance. SEM's directives expressly connect permit grant and maintenance to effective business activity, regular income and the absence of social-assistance dependence. 11

<!-- 2 -->

D. **Main** **route** **distinctions.** Four separate categories must not be conflated in drafting:

settled self-employment in Switzerland under Annex I Article 12 AFMP;

cross-border self-employed service provision for up to 90 days under the notification/service regime; employment under the worker provisions; and

economically inactive residence, where adequate means and insurance are the core conditions. Official Swiss materials repeatedly separate those categories and attach different evidential burdens to each. 12

### D.Primary legal texts

The principal treaty texts are: the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons between Switzerland and the EU, especially Article 4 and Annex I, Chapter III, Articles 12 to 15; the parallel EFTA Convention materials in Annex K and Appendix 1, Chapter III, Articles11 to 14; and the Swiss implementing framework reflected in the current VFP/OLCP directives and federal factsheets. Article 7 AFMP and Article 7 Annex K are also central because they extend equal treatment, mobility, post-activity stay rights and family rights to persons within the agreement's scope. 27

The Swiss implementing ordinance itself is the **Verordnung über den freien Personenverkehr /** **Ordonnance sur la libre circulation des personnes / Ordinanza sulla libera circolazione delle** **persone** **(SR** **142.203),** repeatedly identified as the governing implementing instrument by SEM and official cantonal pages. In this pack, operative propositions from that ordinance are grounded principally through the current SEM directives and cantonal materials, which are the most usable official implementation sources in this record. 28

## Official guidance and cantonal practice

### E. Official guidance / administrative materials

SEM's 2025 federal factsheet says EU/EFTA citizens wishing to work in a self-employed capacity in Switzerland initially receive a **B EU/EFTA permit valid for five years** if they can show an **effective self-employed** **activity** when they submit the application. SEM's FAQ adds that EU/EFTA nationals must register with the commune within **14** **days** **after** **arrival** and request residence authorisation, producing identity documents plus documents proving that they exercise or will exercise a self-employed activity that is **subsistence-generating** for themselves and their family; the activity may be taken up only after the documents required by thecanton have been produced. 29

SEM's 2026 VFP directives are the most precise federal administrative source. They state that self-employment must be proved by a Swiss undertaking or place of business with **effective** **and** **subsistence-generating** **business** **activity,** evidenced through business books, including accounting and orders; they state that commercial-register entry usually accompanies commercial undertakings or legal entities, but is not universally mandatory; they prohibit cantons from imposing **prohibitive** evidential hurdles; and they make regular income and freedom from social-assistance dependency decisive both for grant and for ongoing maintenance of status. 4

SEM also provides the main federal statement on what happens if the route fails over time. The directives say that self-employed persons lose residence rights where they can no longer maintain themselves and become dependent on social assistance, and that persons admnitted as self-employed but no longer economically active or job-seeking must meet the sufficient-means standard applicable under the AFMP. By contrast, workers retain a different and generally more protective position under the worker provisions. 30

SECO's official materials are especially valuable on **status** **verification** **and** **sham** **self-employment.** For temporary self-employed service providers, SECO requires a copy of the notification or permit, an A1form from the home-country social-security authority, and a client contract or equivalent client confirmation. SECO's distinction guidance further states that self-employment points towards work under one's own name and account, with investments, own premises, loss risk, independence from instructions, control over working time and multiple clients, whereas employment points towards subordination, working in another's name, fixed hours, assigned workplace and regular work for the same employer. 31

Official AHV/OASI guidance is relevant, but only **as** **evidence** **and** **status-indicator** **material,** not as a substitute for immigration-law residence rules. The AHV leaflet defines self-employed persons for social-insurance purposes as persons who work under their own name and account, operate independently and bear their own economic risk; it emphasises case-by-case assessment based on **economic** **facts** **rather** **than** **contractual labels.** That aligns closely with SEM and SECO's immigration-law distinction and explains why cantonal migration offices commonly ask for AHV affiliation or recognition,while still requiring immigration-specific proof of genuine Swiss activity. 32

### F.Cantonal practice notes

The cantonal materials show a broad common core but not a single universal document list. **Geneva** requires proof that the independent activity is genuinely exercised; for new self-employed EU/AELE applicants it asks for identity documents, a **complete** **project/business-plan** **file** and proof of **professional address** such as commercial-register entry or a business lease. Geneva also states that work may begin once the **complete** **file** has been submitted, save where the profession is regulated and requires an additional state authorisation. For an already resident EU/AELE permit holder switching to self-employment, Geneva again asks for proof of effective activity, business plan/project materials and professional-address evidence. 33

<!-- 7 -->

**Vaud** uses an official EU/AELE residence form that expressly lists the self-employed annexes: a copy of **the** **lease** **for** **business** **premises,** detailed explanations of the administrative steps taken or planned to start the business with all necessary authorisations, including affiliation to a **compensation** **fund,** a **business plan,** and proof of **financial resources** such as bank statements or other assets. Vaud's form also states expressly that the grant of residence authorisation is **not** itself a professional licence and does not remove the need to obtain any necessary sectoral authorisations. 34

**St Gallen** states that applicants must prove an **active** **andI subsistence-securing** self-employed activity. Its checklist asks for a passport or identity document, **business** **plan, lease** of **business** **premises,** proof of self-employed subsistence-generating activity such as **order volume,** **bookkeeping** **extracts** or **start-up** **capital,** commercial-register extract where applicable, and proof of **health** **and** **accident** **insurance.** 35

**Aargau** is one of the clearest German-language cantonal authorities. It says that newly arriving EU/EFTA self-employed persons must prove self-employment with the application; if they do,they receive a five-year permit. Aargau also states that the authority may request **new** **evidence** **at any** **time** **during** validity or revoke the permit if conditions cease to be met. Its guidance requires **sufficient** **financial** **means** and comprehensive health/accident insurance, and gives evidential examples including commercial-register extract, **business plan,** company accounting, **market analysis,** a **motivational** **explanation,** a lease or purchase contract for business premises, **bank** **and** **insurance** **documents,and** **AHV compensation-office registration.** 36

**Schaffhausen** similarly states that self-employed applicants must prove an **active** **and** **subsistence-securing** activity and that the canton may demand further evidence whenever doubts arise. Its document examples are particularly useful for future drafting because they include **business** **plan,** **order volume, bookkeeping extracts, bank records, asset evidence, lease of business premises,** commercial-register extract, and health/accident insurance, with sector-specific permits where necessary. 37

**Ticino** confirms the same basic pattern in Italian. It describes a five-year B UE/AELS permit for independent activity, states that in case of serious doubts about the **effective** **and** **durable** character of the activity the authority may demand **new** **means** **of** **proof** during validity and revoke the document if the original conditions are no longer met, and stresses that separate obligations towards tax, social-insurance and municipal authorities still apply. Ticino also notes, for family reunion, that the sponsor must have **sufficient** **financial means** and suitable housing. 38

The safest general proposition is therefore this**: cantonal evidence lists differ in detail, but the** **recurring official themes are business plan/project description, business address or premises,** **proof of actual or imminent operations, financial-resource material, insurance,social-security or** **compensation-fund evidence, commercial-register evidence where relevant, and profession-**specific **authorisations where the activity is regulated.** 39

## Practical legal analysis

### G. Practical legal analysis

<!-- 8 -->

The immigration-law treatment of EU/EFTA self-employment in Switzerland is best understood as a **two-level enquiry.** The first level asks whether the person falls within the AFMP/EFTA establishment route at all: are they genuinely pursuing an economic activity in Switzerland as a self-employed person? The second level asks whether the activity is **real enough** **to** **sustain** **residence,** rather than a nominal set-up used to obtain a permit. SEM's language of "effective", "active", "regular" and "subsistence-generating" activity captures that second enquiry. 40

On the evidence, the official materials support a **layered** **approach** rather than a single mandatory checklist. The strongest files will usually show several kinds of objective material together: identity and registration material; a concrete business model and market explanation; evidence of business premises or a plausible professional address; operational documents such as contracts, orders, invoicing chains, accounting extracts or planned accounting structure; financial evidence such as start-up capital, bank records or other resources; social-security evidence such as AHV/AVS affiliation where applicable; insurance; and profession-specific licences where required. That is why it is wrong to present **company** **registration** **alone** as sufficient, and equally wrong to suggest that there is one standard federal list of documents binding every canton in every case. 41

The distinction from **employment** is legally central. Swiss official sources converge on classic subordination indicators: if the person works in another entity's operational structure, follows instructions, has fixed working methods or times imposed by others, bears little real economic risk, or effectively serves one principal in a dependent manner, the case may be classified as employment or sham self-employment. By contrast, independent pricing, real commercial risk, own investment, autonomy over operations, own client base, and the ability to accept or refuse projects point towards genuine self-employment. The label chosen by the parties is not decisive; economic reality is. 5

That same logic explains why **platform-based** **or single-client** **models** need careful drafting. SEM's directives expressly note that persons offering taxi or food-delivery services through digital platforms are not treated as self-employed in the relevant example from Swiss case law, and SECO's own markers emphasise multiple clients and freedom from instruction. Content for internal use should therefore avoid broad statements that "one can simply freelance through a platform" without examining operational control and subordination. 42

The distinction from **economically** **inactive** **residence** is equally important. A person who is not genuinely self-employed cannot repair the self-employed route by pointing to capital, savings or private wealth alone; that is a different AFMP residence basis with different conditions. Conversely,official canton materials often request financial statements or bank evidence from self-employed applicants not because the route is economically inactive residence, but because the canton is testing whether the claimed business is viable enough to avoid welfare dependence. In other words, financial resources may be relevant both as proof of business viability and, if the business fails, as the bridge to a different residence category. 43

**On** **permit** **duration** **and** **renewal,** the route is straightforward in principle but strict in operation. The treaty model is a permit of at least five years, renewable for at least five years on proof that self-employed economic activity continues. Official Swviss materials reflect that five-year structure. But because SEM allows the cantonal authorities to revisit the evidence if there are serious doubts about genuine activity and subsistence-level income, the five-year permit should not be described as insulated from later review. It is better understood as recognition of a right that continues only while the route conditions remain met. 44

**On** **business** **failure, low** **income** **and** **cessation,** the safest statement is that there is no single published federal income threshold for the self-employed route equivalent to a fixedsalary floor. Instead, the official materials focus on whether the business is effective, real and sufficient to support the person without social assistance. If self-employment ceases and the person is not protected by temporary incapacity or some other retained-status rule, SEM treats the case by reference to sufficient means; if public social assistance is then requiredd, the residence right can lapse and the permit can be revoked. That makes the concept of **financial viability** real, but practice-led rather than reducible to a single national numeric threshold in thematerials reviewed here. 45

<!-- 9 -->

**On** **family** **members,** the route is available but should be drafted with care. SEM's family factsheet makes clear that self-employed sponsors need suitable accommodation and the financial means to support the family members they sponsor. The family members' residence position is derivative and normally lasts only while the principal AFMP right exists. Family members who qualify through reunion gain labour-market access, including self-employment, but relatives in the ascending line do not acquire a right to work. 8

## Drafting cautions and source list

## H. Drafting cautions

Do **not** say that an EU/EFTA national qualifies simply by saying they intend to become self-employed. Current Swiss official practice requires documentary proof of an **effective and** **subsistence-generating** self-employed activity, and authorities may revisit the evidence later. 16

Do **not** say that a Swiss company, sole proprietorship or commercial-register entry **by** **itself** proves the immigration-law test. Official Swiss guidance treats registration as relevant but not conclusive, and many cantons ask for much more: business plans, premises, orders, bookkeeping, bank evidence, insurance and compensation-fund material. 46

**Do** **not** conflate settled self-employment in Switzerland with the separate regime for **cross-border** **service** **providers** operating up to 90 days in a calendar year. The latter uses the notification route and SECO's control documents, including A1 and client-contract evidence. 22

Do **not** blur immigration status with **tax, AHV/social-security, V/AT or company-law compliance.** Those areas matter as evidence of reality and independence, but none of them alone determines residence rights. AHV recognition is evidentially important, not dispositive; professional-sector authorisations are separate from residence permission. 47

Do **not** universalise canton-specific document checklists. The official materials show a strong national core, but the exact supporting documents and process steps remain partly **canton-led.** Use wording such as "authorities commonly ask for" or "official canton materials indicate", rather than "Swiss law always requires". 39

Do **not** assume that low income is harmless merely because the person has a permit. SEM's worker case law is more tolerant of low income and some public support than the self-employed/non-active line.For self-employed persons who cease activity or cannot maintain themselves, sufficient means become decisive and social-assistance dependence may break the residdence right. 48

**Do** **not** overlook the possibility of **disguised** **employmennt,** especially where there is one client, tight operational control, platform dependency, fixed schedules or little genuine commercial risk. The official Swiss sources treat that as a substantive classification issue, not a drafting technicality. 49

Do **not** mix in materials on the **updated** **AFMP** **not yet** **in** **force** when drafting present-law content for this route. SEM publishes separate future-law comparison materials; this pack concerns the current agreement and current Swiss guidance. 1

## Open questions / limitations

The published official materials are strongest on the existenceof a genuine, active and subsistence-generating business and weaker on precise federal numeric thresholds for "viability" short of social-assistance dependence. The most accurate current drafting line is therefore that viability is assessed **case** **by** **case** by reference to actual business activity, regular income and independence from welfare, with canton-specific evidential practice playing a significant role. 45
