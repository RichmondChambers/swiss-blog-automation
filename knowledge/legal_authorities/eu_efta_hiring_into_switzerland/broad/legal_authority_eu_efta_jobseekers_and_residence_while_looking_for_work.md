# Swiss legal authority pack on EU/EFTA

## jobseekers and residence while looking for work

Prepared for internal immigration-content use concerning Switzerland, with focus on federal legislation, the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons, SEM guidance, and public cantonal practice materials reviewed as at 24 May 2026.

## Executive legal summary

The core Swiss legal framework for EU/EFTA nationals who come to Switzerland to look for work is treaty-based rather than purely domestic. The relevant route starts with the Agreement on the Free Movement of Persons (AFMP/ALCP), especially Annex I, and is implemented in Swiss law primarily through the Ordinance on the Free Movement of Persons (OLCP/VFP, RS 142.203). The Federal Act on Foreign Nationals and Integration (LEI/AIG) applies only subsidiarily to persons benefiting from free movement, unless the treaty and implementing rules are silent or LEI is more favourable. SEM's official annexes identify the AFMP and OLCP as the governing legal texts, and SEM's LEI guidance expressly states the subsidiarity point.1

For an EU/EFTA national entering Switzerland solely to seek work, the federal position is conservative and structured. SEM's 2026 OLCP directives say the person may enter Switzerland to look for work and, if the stay does not exceed three months, no permit is required. SEM further states that the "reasonable period" for job search under Annex I AFMP is, following the case law applied in Switzerland, a period not exceeding six months. If the search lasts longer than three months, a short-term EU/EFTA permit may be issued for a further three months, bringing the total to six months, provided the person has sufficient financial means. 2

Residence rights are not unlimited after that point. SEM's directives and SEM's 2014 official commentary explain that, after the initial six months, the canton may extend the short-term jobseeker permit up to one year if the person proves genuine job-search efforts and a real prospect of being hired within that extended period. Lucerne's current migration guidance expresses the same point in operational terms and adds that this further extension is granted "without legal entitlement", which is a useful drafting caution for internal content. 3

Sufficient means matter throughout the jobseeker route. SEM's official commentary states that a jobseeker cannot in principle claim residence rights under the AFMP if they lack sufficient means, and that the point was expressly carried into OLCP art. 18. SEM's 2026 directives add that,for jobseekers, the evidential threshold can be lower than under the economically inactive residence route in Annex I art. 24, because the stay is temporary; the person mainly needs to make it plausible that they can support themselves without recourse to social assistance, with the suitable proof left to the canton. Examples given by SEM and cantonal forms include bank statements, income confirmations, tax documents, applications, rejection letters, interview invitations, and records of job-search efforts. 4

Official sources are equally clear that pure jobseeker status is not the same as worker status. SEM states that EU/EFTA nationals who enter Switzerland only to look for work, and their family members, have no entitlement to social assistance under LEI art. 29a, and that if their means prove insufficient and they

resort to social assistance, their right of stay ends; however, the constitutional minimum guarantee remains reserved and may in practice be limited to emergency support such as return-travel funding. This is materially different from the residence basis of an actual worker, and it is also different from the residence basis of an economically inactive person under Annex I art. 24, which is built around a five-year B permit, sufficient means, and comprehensive health and accident insurance. 5

Once employment is actually found, the legal basis changes. SEM's public guidance states that a contract of more than three months requires residence authorisation; contracts up to three months are handled through the notification procedure. A contract of up to 364 days leads to an L permit; a contract of at least one year or an indefinite contract leads to a B permit. If the person switches into self-employment instead, that is a different residence basis again: SEM andcantonal Geneva/Basel materials require proof of actual or planned self-employed activity, typically through business-plan and business-address evidence, and the activity is linked to a residence permit for self-employment rather than to continuing jobseeker status. 6

## Structural overview of EU/EFTA jobseeker residence

The federal structure can be read as a staged route rather than as a single open-ended status. First, an EU/EFTA national may come to Switzerland to look for work and remain for up to three months without a permit. SEM's guidance links this to the treaty right to seek employment in another contracting state, while Zurich and Lucerne publicly state that the initial three-month job-search stay is permit-free.7

Secondly, if the search continues beyond three months, the person moves out of the permit-free phase and into a short-term EU/EFTA permit framework. SEM says that the person may receive a three-month L permit for job search, so that the total stay reaches six months, provided sufficient means are shown. SEM's FAQ also states that, where a person first spent three months in Switzerland without gainful activity, for example while looking for work, any residence authorisation needed thereafter must be applied for once that exempt period ends. 8

Thirdly, there may be a further extension, but only on proof. SEM's directives and commentary say that the permit may be extended up to one year if there are evidenced search efforts and a real prospect of engagement. Cantonal practice notes are not identical in how they describe this: Basel-Stadt's public page presents the post-three-month jobseeker L permit as nine additional months total,while Lucerne states the extension from six to twelve months as discretionary and lists concrete evidence to be filed. The better internal drafting position is therefore that the outer limit may reach twelve months in practice, but only on the qualified conditions stated in federal guidance. 9

A separate track applies once employment begins. Short employment of up to three months in a calendar year is handled through the notification procedure. Longer employment changes the residence basis to employment, with the contract duration determining whether the person receives an L or B permit. A further, separate track applies if the person instead establishes self-employment and can prove it. 10

A different legal regime also applies if the person already had worker status in Switzerland and then lost employment. SEM's 2026 directives explain that LEI art. 61a governs the ending of residence rights after involuntary job loss, including six-month search periods and rules on unemployment benefits and ORP registration. That post-employment regime should never be conflated with the initial-entry jobseeker route. 11

### Authorities and guidance

**Primary** **legal texts.** The legally important texts for this topic are the AFMP, especially Annex I, and the OLCP/VFP implemennting ordinance. SEM's official annexes to the OLCP directives identify the AFMP as RS 0.142.112.681 and the OLCP as RS 142.203, and also identify the official legal-text repositories for those instruments. On the substance relevant to jobseekers, the operative focal points are Annex I art. 2on seeking employment and residence for a reasonable period; Annex I art. 3 on family members; Annex I art. 24 on residence without economic activity; OLCP art. 16 on sufficient means; OLCP art. 18on job-search stays; and LEI arts. 29a and 61a on social-assistance exclusion and post-employment residence respectively. In this research environment, the most accessible official articulationof those provisions was through SEM's current directives and commentary, which quote and apply them directly. **Official guidance** **and** **administrative** **materials.** SEM's January 2026 OLCP directives are the single most useful official administrative source for this topic. They set out the structure of entry for job search, the three-month permit-free phase, the further three-month L-permit phase, the possible extension up to one year, the need for sufficient means, the exclusion from social assistance, evidential flexibility,the constitutional minimum guarantee, and the distinct treatment of ex-workers under LEI art. 61a. SEM's public FAQ and permit pages then provide shorter but still authoritative public-facing confirmation of the same core points, especially on permit categories, contract-duration thresholds, mobility into self-employment, economically inactive B-permit conditions, and the practical statement that a person who loses employment may in principle remain six months to seek new work. SECO's work.swiss materials are directly relevant only on unemployment-benefit export and ORP/RAV steps, but they are important where the person is carrying foreign unemployment benefits into Switzerland or where Swiss unemployment administration becomes engaged. BAG's materials are relevant to the general health-insurance consequences of taking up residence in Switzerland. 22

**Cantonal practice** **notes.** Public cantonal materials are useful but uneven. Zurich states clearly that EU/ EFTA nationals may remainin Switzerland for three months to seek work without a permit, and that residence formalities follow where the stay or work exceeds the permit-free threshold. Basel-Stadt publishes a more simplified statement that a jobseeker L permit may cover nine additional months after registration, giving a total stay of twelve months, and publicly states that claiming social assistance is a ground for removal. Lucerne's public model is more legally nuanced: it repeats the three-month permit-free phase, the subsequent three-month L permit based on sufficient means, and then describes any six-to-twelve-month extension as dependent on concrete job-search proof, justified prospects, proof of funds, and confirmation from the social office that no benefits are being received. Geneva's published materials are particularly useful for the self-employment transition, requiring proof of the effective independent activity through a full project file or business plan and proof of the professional address. These materials are best used as practice illustrations rather than as substitutes for the federal legal framework. 23

### Practical legal analysis

The safest internal legal framing is that Switzerland recognises a defined, evidence-sensitive, and limited residence route for EU/EFTA jobseekers, not a free-standing right to remain indefinitely until employment is eventually obtained. The treaty baseline is the right to seek work and remain for a reasonable period, treated in Swiss guidance as upo to six months. Swiss implementing law then operationalises that by separating an initial permit-free phase of up to three months from a further permit-based phase, and by attaching means and evidence requirements to any continued stay beyond the first three months. 24

Registration must therefore be explained with care. For the initial permit-free job-search phase, federal guidance does not require a residence permit, and Lucerne states expressly that no registration with the Einwohnerkontrolle is required during that period. Once the person remains beyond three months, however, the residence situation moves into cantonal permit practice. SEM's FAQ states that a person who previously stayed in Switzerland for three months without activity, for example while seeking work, must then file the required authorisation once the exempt period ends. Zurich and Basel-Stadt publicly present the same threshold in practical terms. By contrast, where a person is exporting foreign unemployment benefits into Switzerland, formal registration with the Swiss employment service is clearly required: Zurich requires RAV registration within seven days of the start of the boenefit-export period, and the SECO U2 document explains the same seven-day rule at the destination state's employment service. 25

<!-- 7 -->

The evidence package also needs careful treatment. Federal guidance does not impose a single national checklist for initial-entry jobseekers; instead, SEM says the canton determines the appropriate evidence case by case. That is why cantonal documentary practice matters. Basel-Stadt asks for proof of job-search efforts such as receipts of applications, rejection letters, interview invitations, RAV job-search lists,annd unemployment statements. Lucerne asks for evidence of funds, copies of applications and rejections, and social-office confirmation that no benefits are being received. This supports a conservative drafting line: evidence of both subsistence and genuine search activity may be required, but the exact checklist is canton-facing rather than uniformly fixed by one federal form. 13

The most important conceptual distinction is between pure jobseeker residence and worker residence. An initial entrant who is merely looking for work is not in the same position as an EU/EFTA national already employed in Switzerland. Once actual employment begins, the person moves into the worker-permit framework, and contract duration determines whether the result is notification only, an L permit, or a B permit. By contrast, if the person does not find work, the jobseeker basis expires at the end of the relevant period, unless an extension is justified. A further distinction must then be drawn for people who already worked in Switzerland and later became involuntarily unemployed: SEM treats those cases under LEI art. 61a, not as ordinary initial-entry jobseeker cases, and the consequences can differ depending on whether the original work lasted less than or more than twelve months and whether unemployment benefits continue beyond six months. 26

Health-insurance issues should be framed narrowly. The non-active B-permit route expressly requires sufficient means plus health and accident insurance covering all risks. The federal jobseeker materials reviewed do not present that same insurance condition as the defining legal test for the initial L jobseeker route; they focus instead on sufficient means and exclusion from social assistance. Separately, BAG states that anyone who settles in Switzerland must take out health insurance within three months of establishing residence. Internal content should therefore avoid saying that the art. 24non-active insurance test simply governs the entire pure jobseeker route, but it may safely note that general Swiss compulsory insurance obligations can arise once residence in Switzerland is actually established. 20

Family issues are real but should be handled cautiously. SEM's directives indicate that jobseekers and persons who have lost worker status must have means sufficient to maintain family members,and that family reunion under the AFMP is not recognised if social benefits are or would need to be provided. SEM also states that the exclusion from social assistance for initial-entry jobseekers applies to their family members. The public user-facing federal material is thinner on this point than the directives, so internal drafting should present family consequences as qualified and source-specific rather than as a broad headline entitlement. 27

### Drafting cautions

Do not say that EU/EFTA jobseekers have an unrestricted or open-ended right to reside in Switzerland while looking for work. The federal structure is initial three months permit-free, then a further three-month L-permit phase if means are shown, then only a qualified possibility of extension up to one year on proof of genuine efforts and real prospects. 28

Do not equate jobseeker status with worker status. Pure jobseeker residence, post-employment residence after prior Swiss work, economically inactive residence, salaried employment, and self-employment are separate legal bases with different conditions and effects. 29

<!-- 8 -->

Do not write as though the economically inactive B-permit route is interchangeable with the jobseeker L-permit route. The non-active B route is explicitly tied to sufficient means and all-risks health and accident insurance; the jobseeker route is framed in federal materials around limited duration, sufficient means, proof of search activity, and exclusion from social assistance. 30

Do not overstate social-assistance consequences into broader propositions than the authorities support. The official position is that pure jobseekers and their family members have no entitlement to social assistance and that recourse to it ends the stay right, but SEM also reserves the constitutional minimum guarantee and expressly contemplates emergency-level support. 12

Do not present ORP/RAV registration as a universally explicit federal requirement for every initial-entry jobseeker. It is clearly required in the foreign-benefit-export context and in certain post-employment situations under LEI art. 61a. For the initial-entry jobseeker route, the federal sources reviewed instead emphasise evidence of search activity and leavedetailed proof design to cantons. 31

Do not treat canton-facing documentary lists as uniform national law. Basel-Stadt and Lucerne publish particularly detailed evidence requirements, while Zurich is more general and Basel-Stadt's public twelve-month formulation is more simplified than SEM's fuller federal explanation. Federal SEM guidance should therefore be treated as the legal baseline, with cantonal documents used as operational examples only where clearly labelled as such. 32

**Open** **questions and** **limitations.** Direct machine-readable access to some current consolidated Fedlex/ admin legal texts was incomplete in the research environment, so the pack relies heavily on official SEM annexes identifying the controlling RS texts, together with SEM's current directives and commentary quoting and applying the relevant provisions. Public cantonal materials also differ in the level of detail they provide, especially on extension conditions and local documentary practice. Where canton pages simplify the position, this pack follows the more conservative federal SEM formulation. 33
