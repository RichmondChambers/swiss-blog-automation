# Broad Swiss Schengen Visa Legal Authority Packs

This folder contains broad thematic legal authority packs for the Swiss Schengen visa blog automation workflow.

Broad packs are used by `generate_and_publish.py` to ground legal analysis, drafting and validation for blog posts that concern recurring Schengen visa issues across more than one travel purpose, applicant category, evidence profile or procedural scenario.

## Current Broad Packs

### `legal_authority_swiss_schengen_visa_legal_framework_and_architecture.md`

This pack should cover the overall legal framework and route architecture for Swiss Schengen visas, including the relationship between Schengen law, Swiss implementation, Type C visa rules, entry conditions, competent state rules, procedure, discretion and border checks.

### `legal_authority_swiss_schengen_visa_permitted_purposes_and_short_stay_limits.md`

This pack should cover permitted short-stay purposes, including tourism, family visits, private visits, business visits, conferences, medical visits and the limits of activities permitted under a Type C visa.

### `legal_authority_swiss_schengen_visa_entry_conditions_and_border_checks.md`

This pack should cover Schengen entry conditions, border checks, possession of a valid visa, purpose and conditions of stay, financial means, no-alert requirements, public policy and security issues, and the distinction between visa issue and admission at the border.

This pack should cover visa requirement exemptions, nationality-based visa obligations, competent Schengen state, main destination, first entry, place of filing and risks arising from filing with the wrong consulate or state.

## Purpose of the Broad Folder

Use this folder for broad thematic packs that apply across several Swiss Schengen visa scenarios.

Broad packs should help the generator deal with recurring issues that arise in tourism, family visit, private visit, business visit, host-sponsored, financially sponsored, repeat-travel, refusal, reapplication and overstay-risk topics.

A broad pack should usually be narrower than the core framework pack but wider than a route-specific or issue-specific pack.

## Legal Framework to Cover

Broad Swiss Schengen visa packs may need to address the relationship between:

the Schengen Borders Code; the Visa Code; Swiss implementation legislation and ordinances; SEM guidance and official procedure materials; consular guidance and practice; competent state and jurisdiction rules; Type C visa requirements; short-stay limits; 90 / 180-day calculation; permitted purposes of stay; entry conditions; border control discretion; financial means; sponsorship and declarations of sponsorship; accommodation and invitation evidence; travel medical insurance; biometrics and VIS; EES and digital travel records; refusal, appeal and reapplication procedure; comparison with Type D visas and residence permits.

## Recommended Structure for Broad Packs

Each broad pack should, where possible, include:

an executive legal summary; a structural overview of the theme or recurring issue; a table of legal propositions; primary legal texts; official guidance and administrative materials; key case law, where relevant; consular or authority practice notes, where relevant; practical legal analysis; drafting cautions; source list; open questions or limitations.

## Legal Proposition Categories

Legal propositions should distinguish clearly between:

entitlement; eligibility only; discretion; facilitation; guidance; procedure; evidential practice; consular practice; border practice; digital record practice; unresolved or practice-sensitive points.

Do not describe guidance as law. Do not describe visa eligibility as an entitlement to entry. Do not describe a Type C visa as permission to reside. Do not describe consular practice as uniform Schengen law. Do not describe possession of a visa as guaranteed admission at the border.

## Broad Analysis Points

Broad Swiss Schengen visa authority packs should preserve the distinctions that affect recurring cross-scenario issues, including:

applicant nationality; visa requirement or exemption; residence country; competent Schengen state; main destination; first entry; place of filing; consular jurisdiction; travel purpose; itinerary; duration of stay; 90 / 180-day calculation; prior Schengen travel history; previous refusals; previous overstays; single-entry or multiple-entry visa; financial means; sponsorship; declaration of sponsorship; accommodation; invitation and host evidence; employment or study ties; family ties; return evidence; medical insurance; biometrics; VIS records; EES records; document credibility; intention to leave; public policy or security issues; border control discretion; appeal and reapplication strategy.

## Relationship with Other Folders

Use `core/` for foundational framework points that explain the overall Swiss Schengen visa system.

Use the broad packs for recurring thematic issues that appear across several Schengen visa scenarios, such as entry conditions, permitted purposes, 90 / 180-day rules, financial means, accommodation, invitation evidence, document credibility, medical insurance, biometrics, refusals, appeals, EES, VIS and comparison with Type D visas or residence permits.

Use `specific/` for packs focused on a specific applicant category, nationality group, factual profile or travel scenario, such as family visitors, business visitors, repeat travellers, applicants with prior refusals, applicants with weak ties, applicants relying on Swiss hosts or applicants at risk of overstay.

Use `issue_specific/` if added for narrower risk issues or complex sub-categories, such as overstay calculation errors, insufficient funds, unreliable documents, unclear purpose of stay, weak return evidence, host credibility problems, medical insurance gaps or business-visit / work-boundary issues.

## Terminology

Use both English and Swiss / Schengen legal references where useful.

Preferred public-facing terminology includes:

Schengen visa; Swiss Schengen visa; Type C visa; short-stay visa; 90 / 180-day rule; permitted purpose of stay; tourism; family visit; private visit; business visit; invitation letter; host declaration; accommodation evidence; financial means; sponsorship; declaration of sponsorship; travel medical insurance; biometrics; VIS; EES; border checks; entry conditions; refusal; appeal; reapplication; competent Schengen state; main destination; first entry; Type D visa; residence permit; SEM; consulate; Schengen Borders Code; Visa Code.

Avoid using informal terminology that suggests a Schengen visa guarantees entry, permits residence, or authorises work in Switzerland.

## Important Drafting Cautions

Broad Swiss Schengen visa packs should not flatten purpose-specific, nationality-specific, evidence-specific or consular-practice distinctions.

A thematic issue may apply differently depending on the applicant’s nationality, residence country, competent Schengen state, travel purpose, itinerary, prior travel history, previous refusals, financial means, sponsorship, accommodation, host evidence, employment or study ties, family ties, medical insurance, document credibility, overstay risk and border control issues.

Authority packs in this folder should help prevent public blog posts from overstating rights, implying that a Type C visa permits residence or work, treating possession of a visa as guaranteed entry, or suggesting that all Schengen visa applications are assessed under the same factual test.
