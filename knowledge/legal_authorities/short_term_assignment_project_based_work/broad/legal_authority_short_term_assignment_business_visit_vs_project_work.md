# Swiss authority pack on business visits, short-term work and project assignments

## Executive legal summary

Swiss law does not draw the operative immigration line by asking whether a trip is labelled a "business visit" in ordinary commercial language. The controlling question is whether the person will carry out an **Erwerbstätigkeit / activité lucrative / attività lucrativa** in Switzerland. Under Article 11 FNIA, foreign nationals who wish to exercise gainful activity in Switzerland require authorisation regardless of the intended duration, and the statutory concept of gainful activity is broad: it covers any activity usually carried out for remuneration, even if the individual case is unpaid. The implementing ordinance reinforces that point by treating work for an employer based in Switzerland **or abroad** as gainful activity, irrespective of whether the wage is paid in Switzerland or abroad and even if the work is only hourly, daily or temporary. The canton's labour-market authority decides classification in the first instance, with doubtful cases referred to SEM. 1

The official materials therefore distinguish sharply between **non-productive, information-based** **or** **representational visits and productive, operational or implementation work.** SEM's official annex on the notion of gainful activity treats short business discussions as non-gainful and gives examples such as representational visits by senior executives, contract negotiations, strategic meetings, information-only coordination meetings, preparatory visits before a future transfer, product-sourcing and process-review visits, and supervision of negotiations in major projects. The same annex also treats passive attendance at congresses and conferences, and strictly theoretical or technical training not integrated into work or production, as non-gainful. 2

By contrast, the same official SEM materials treat the following as gainful activity requiring work authorisation analysis**: on-the-job training integrated into work and production,** internships, repair/ maintenance/warranty work, project missions, IT implementation, reorganisation programmes carried out at the Swiss entity, and recurring managerial activity in Switzerland such as conducting personnel interviews, giving feedback and setting objectives. SEM's cross-border service factsheet separately treats the performance of a **montage** **contract** in Switzerland as a service assignment within the work-authorisation framework, not as a mere business visit. 3

Short duration does not convert productive work into a businessvisit. SEM's own directives say that, for the qualification of gainful activity, duration is irrelevant; the real question is whether the activity is of a kind ordinarily remunerated on the Swiss labour market. The much-cited **eight-day** and **ninety-day** thresholds are authorisation mechanics for certain categories, especially cross-border service provision and short EU/EFTA work, not substantive safe harbours turning productive work into non-work. EU/EFTA posted workers and self-employed service providers may, in the appropriate case, use the notification procedure for up to ninety effective working days per calendar year; in many sectors there is still an eight-day notification trigger, and in listed sectors notification starts from day one. 4

Visa and entry rules must be kept separate from activity classification. SEM's entry guidance states that entry requirements depend on nationality, intended duration and purpose, including whether the person plans to work in Switzerland. For third-country nationals, a visa may be needed in **addition** **to**

any work authorisation. Conversely, a trip may be visa-permissible yet still involve gainful activity for work-authorisation purposes. Immigration authorisation must also be kept separate from tax, payroll, social-security and regulated-profession analysis. 5

### Primary legal texts

The core federal texts are these.

**FNIA** **/ AIG** **/** LEI / **LStrI** **(SR** **142.20).** Article 11 is the foundation: foreign nationals who want to exercise gainful activity in Switzerland need authorisation regardless of duration; gainful activity means any

<!-- 7 -->

dependent or independent activity usually carried out for remuneration, even if unpaid in the specific case. Article 14 lets the Federal Council create more favourable rules for temporary cross-border services. Article 22 also matters in posted-work and secondment cases because it ties admission to local wage and working-condition compliance. 32

**OASA** **/ VZAE** **/** **OASA** **(SR** 142.201).Article 1a says that dependent gainful activity includes any work for an employer based in Switzerland or abroad, regardless of where wages are paid and regardless of whether the work is hourly, daily or temporary. Article 3 defines cross-border service provision as a temporary service provided in Switzerland under a contractual relationship by a person or undertaking based abroad. Article 44 gives the canton the first-instance decision on whether conduct is gainful activity and sends doubtful cases to SEM. Article 14 deals with cross-border gainful activity up to eight days and the sectoral first-day exceptions. 33

**AFMP /FZA** / **ALCP** / **ALC** **(SR** **0.142.112.681).** The treaty's objective is to facilitate the provision of services and liberalise the provision of services of brief duration. Article 5 gives service providers the right to provide a service in the other contracting party's territory for up to ninety days of actual work in a calendar year, subject to the treaty structure and implementing rules. 34

**Ordinance on the introduction of free movement / VFP / OLCP (SR 142.203)** and theSEM **notification** **regime.** These implement the AFMP service route for EU/EFTA movements. SEM's official notification pages explain the practical result: up to ninety effective working days per calendar year, EU/ EFTA posted workers and self-employed service providers use the notification procedure; beyond that, permit analysis applies. 35

**Posted Workers Act / Entsendegesetz / LDét / LDist (SR 823.20).** This is not the source of the basic immigration classification, but it overlays work and wage standards for postings into Switzerland. SECO's official explanation and the statutory text both make clear that a posting involves workers sent to Switzerland by an employer based abroad to perform work here for a period,while the foreign employment relationship remains in place. 36

**Visa-side** **rules.** The SEM annex on the "notion of gainful activity" is framed partly through visa administration, but it is still highly relevant because it gives official examples of what counts, and what does not count, as gainful activity for short visits. It is therefore an important interpretive aid even though it is not itself the primary statutory definition. 16

### Official guidance and cantonal practice notes

At federal guidance level, three SEM materials are central. First, SEM's formnal directives on "stay with gainful activity" explain that the concept of gainful activity is intentionally broad, apply an objective labour-market test, and state that duration is irrelevant to the classification. Secondly, SEM's official notification pages explain the ninety-day EU/EFTA service route, the eight-day filing mechanics, and the difference between posted workers, self-employed service providers and short local hires with a Swiss employer. Thirdly, SEM's annex on the notion of gainful activity gives the most useful concrete examples for business discussions, conferences, training, maintenance and project work. 37

SECO's materials are most useful for the **posted-worker** **layer.** SECO defines posting as a foreign employer sending workers temporarily to another country to work there, while the employment relationship continues and the workers remain under the home-state social-security system. SECO's "correctly report gainful activity" materials also show how filing duties and permit duties differ by route, by nationality and by sector, including the treatment of EU/EFTA postings,third-country postings and the first-day sectors. 38

<!-- 8 -->

Cantonal practice notes are practical rather than doctrinal, but they reveal how authorities actually operationalise classification. Zurich expressly separates three buckets for EU/EFTA nationals: Swiss-employer commencement of work, secondment/posting, and self-employment. For third-country nationals, Zurich also describes secondment as foreign-employer, temporary and generally project-based. Geneva likewise distinguishes missions of up to ninety days or three consecutive months from longer missions, counts only **effective** **working** **days,** and requires detailed application documentation once permit analysis is triggered. Fribourg, Bern and Aargau use the same general notification logic for short EU/EFTA services. 39

The cantonal materials also show what evidence matters in practice. Geneva asks for the signed service contract, signed employment contract, motivation letter stating duration, place of performance and reasons for the service, CVs and diplomas, posting letter, remuneration details and A1 for independents. Zurich's own documentation for posted-worker cases requires the assignment contract to specify the home company, host company, function, scope of duties, worksite, beginning and duration of the assignment, base salary, posting allowance, travel/accommodation/food expenses, insurance and social-security arrangements. Bern and Aargau short-service pages likewise focus on identity, company details, assignment location, duration, contract and A1. 40

### Practical legal analysis

The safest organising principle is this: if the visitor is **observing, negotiating, discussing, inspecting,** **coordinating or receiving purely theoretical training,** the activity may fall on the business-visit side; if the visitor is **delivering, implementing, repairing, maintaining, supporting, training on the job,** **managing staff operationally or producing substantive project output** in Switzerland, the activity is likely to be gainful activity. That conclusion does not rest on a single slogan but on the way SEM's examples line up across meetings, conferences, training and project missions. 41

**Meetings, negotiations and business discussions.** These are the strongest non-gainful examples in the official material. SEM clears executive representation, negotiations, strategic meetings and information-only coordination. It also treats preparatory visits before a future transfer as non-gainful where the trip is for orientation, contract signature and settling-in arrangements, rather than actual job performance. The internal drafting consequence is that meeting-based descriptions are only safe where the facts are truly limited to discussion, observation or preparation. 25

**Conferences, seminars** **and** **workshops.** The official materials clearly protect attendance and passive participation. SEM also clears seminar participation and workshop attendance, and states that passive participation in congresses and conferences, notably without giving a presentation, is not gainful activity. What the official sources do **not** do is create an eqqually clear safe harbour for delivering workshops, speaking at conferences, or using an event format to provide billable substantive services to Swiss clients. That area should therefore be treated with caution. 16

**Training.** Swiss practice draws one of its clearest lines here. If the individual is merely attending theoretical or technical training that is not integrated into a work process or productive activity, SEM treats that as non-gainful. But if the person is embedded in a Swiss workplace and learns by participating in work or production, SEM treats it as gainful activity. This distinction is particularly important for intra-group training at a Swiss headquarters, system handover programmes, "shadowing", and technical familiarisation that happens on real systems or live operations. 16

<!-- 9 -->

**Implementation, consulting, technical support, installation and maintenance.** Official Swiss materials are strongest on implementation and maintenance, and thinner on the generic label "consulting". SEM expressly treats IT roll-outs, reorganisation programmes, and maintenance/warranty work in Switzerland as gainful activity. SEM's cross-border service factsheet also uses the performance of a montage contract in Switzerland as a textbook example of service work within the work-authorisation regime. A conservative synthesis is therefore that where "consulting" means substantive delivery at or into a Swiss operation -implementation, operational support, troubleshooting,process redesign, configuration, training tied to delivery, or recurring managerial intervention -it should be analysed as gainful activity, not as abusiness visit. 3

**Remuneration, payroll and** **Swiss** **client benefit.** Remuneration is legally important, but in a limnited way:Swiss law asks whether the activity is one that is **usually** remunerated, not whether this particular visitor happens to be paid, paid extra, or paid in Switzerland. Home-country payroll therefore does not stop work from being gainful activity. The officiallabour-market test also indicates that what matters is whether the Swiss-side activity affects the Swiss labour market. Although the sources do not use the phrase "Swiss client benefit" as a stand-alone legal test, the examples show a clear pattern: information gathering and negotiation are treated more leniently, while on-site delivery that directly serves Swiss operations is treated as work. That is a strong inference from the official examples, not a separate statutory rule. 42

**Physical presence at a Swiss client or project site.** Physical presence is not by itself the test, but it is **a** powerful factual indicator because the Swiss sources are concerned with labour-market participation in Switzerland. The safest view is that a site visit confined to observation, process review, information exchange or negotiation may still fit the business-discussion examples, while a site visit that involves doing installation, maintenance, supervision integrated into execution, system go-live work, user support or personnel management is very likely to be classed as gainful activity. 18

**Short duration and the eight-day or ninety-day thresholds.** This is where internal drafting most often goes wrong. A three-day installation visit can still be gainful activity.An eight-day or ninety-day threshold does not change the legal nature of the conduct; it changes whether a permit or a notification route is potentially available. SEM's own directives say duration is irrelevant to the underlying classification, while the notification materials explain when short work can proceed by notification instead of permit. In listed sectors, even very short assignments trigger first-day filing or permit rules. 43

**Evidence** **and** **misdescription** **risk.** Swiss authorities look behind labels. The strongest evidence in classification disputes will usually be the service contract, the posting letter or intra-group assignment letter, task list, worksite details, dates, reporting lines, remuneration/allowance arrangements, expense coverage, A1/social-security position, and any project documents showing whether the visitor is attending discussions or actually delivering output. Cantonal forms and checklists make that plain. If a file uses words such as "meeting", "workshop", "training", "support" or "oversight" but the attachments show real implementation or service delivery, the business-visit wording is unlikely to carry much weight. 40

**Open** **questions** **and** **limitations.** Official materials are strong on the poles of the analysis, but thinner on mixed or hybrid cases. In particular, active conference speaking, paid workshop delivery, short-form advisory consulting, and "site visits" that mix observation with some troubleshooting are not exhaustively codified in official guidance. SEM and the ordinance make clear that doubtful cases are for the canton, with referral to SEM where needed. For internal content drafting, those areas should be marked as fact-sensitive rather than turned into bright-line rules. 44

### Drafting cautions and source list

**Drafting** **cautions.** Internal content should avoid saying or implying that: a short stay is automatically a business visit; foreign payroll means no Swiss work analysis is needed; a "meeting" label is enough where the person will install, implement, maintain or support systems; any conference or workshop attendance is automatically exempt regardless of role; or notification thresholds are substantive permissions for non-work. It should aIso avoid collapsing separate questions: work authorisation, visa entry, regulated-profession filing, tax, payroll and social security all run on different legal tracks. 45

Where future content needs examples, the safest official "business visit" phrases are **representation,** **negotiation, strategic meetings, information exchange, preparatory visits, passive event** **attendance and theoretical training not integrated into production.** The safest official "work/ project" phrases are **on-the-job training integrated into work, project mission, implementation,** **reorganisation work, repair, maintenance, warranty work and recurring operational** **management activity** **in** **Switzerland.** "Consulting", "technical support", "site visit", "training", "workshop" and "supervision" should never be treated as self-classifying labels; they require an activity-level description. 2
