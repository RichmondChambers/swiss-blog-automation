# Swiss legal authority pack on refusals and file rebuilding in non-EU local hire work permit cases

## Executive legal summary and structural overview

### A. Executive legal summary

Swiss admission of non-EU and non-EFTA nationals for local hire is an employer-led,discretionary route governed by cumulative admission criteria rather than by any general right to a permit. The official SEM materials and cantonal practice pages consistently frame the route around overall economic interest, labour-market priority, candidate qualification, compliance with local salary and working conditions, quotas, and a complete supporting file. A positive cantonal position does not, by itself, secure the permit where SEM approval is required. 1

For refusal analysis, it is essential to separate **appealable** **legal** or **procedural error** from **weak-file** **or** **changed-circumstance** **problems.** The materials reviewed support the following internal distinction: appeal where the authority used the wrong legal test, ignored relevant evidence, chose the wrong procedural route, or issued an unlawful non-entry decision; rebuild and re-file where the problem is thin recruitment evidence, an over-tailored role profile, insufficient qualification proof, salary concerns, missing documents, quota exhaustion, or a better-evidenced business case. SEM's own reconsideration guidance is explicit that reconsideration is not meant to cure missed cooperation or to bypass appeal deadlines. 2

The official procedural route is structurally split.The employer files with the competent canton; the canton screens the case and may refuse it directly; approved cantonal cases are sent to SEM where federal approval is required; only after the federal and cantonal sequence is completed can entry, registration and lawful commencement of work follow. SEM states that its approval decision does not itself yet authorise entry, and for longer-stay cases it also states that work may begin only after registration. 3

### B. Structural overview of refusals and file rebuilding

A practical Swiss refusal analysis works best if the file is triaged into five buckets.

**·Bucket one: legal** or **procedural** **error.** Typical examples are misapplication of the statutory test, failure to engage with submitted recruitment evidence, requiring documents not actually mandated by the governing framework, or issuing a non-entry decision where the merits could and should have been assessed. Those points potentially support appeal. 4

**·Bucket** **two:** **labour-market** **proof** **failure.** This includes late or token advertising, insufficient reach into Switzerland and the EU/EFTA area, missing RAV/EURES evidence where expected, weak candidate-rejection logs, or criteria that appear designed to exclude the priority labour pool. These are usually file-quality problems, not pure legal-error points. 5

**·Bucket three**: **candidate** **and** **role** **mismatch.** Swiss official guidance remains restrictive: the route is for managers, specialists and other qualified workers, with some flexibility in occupations with pronounced shortages. Missing diploma evidence, weak translation practice, thin proof of specialist experience, or lack of profession-specific recognition usually call for rebuilding the evidence base rather than an abstract appeal. 6

**·Bucket four: salary, conditions and employercredibility. A** signed contract is minimum evidence, but SEM guidance and case law show that real compliance matters. Inconsistencies between approved and actual terms, unexplained part-time arrangements, or unclear payroll conditions can damage the whole file and may justify adverse factual inferences. 7

**·Bucket** **five**: **quota** **or** **timing** **barrier.** Quotas are a live, independent admission condition and can block approval even in otherwise strong cases. Timing also matters procedurally: the employer must file first, Geneva expects filing roughly six to eight weeks before entry, and work must not begin before the route allows it. These points often require changed timing or a new filing window, not simply better argument. 8

#### Primary texts and official guidance

### D. Primary legal texts

The core federal texts are the **Federal Act** on **Foreign** **Nationals** **and** **Integration** (FNIA; AIG/LEI/LStrI, SR 142.20) and the **Ordinance** **on** **Admission, Stay and** **Employment** (ASEO/VZAE/OASA, SR 142.201). SEM's legal-framework page identifies both as the central statutory instruments for immigration and work-admission issues, and SEM's Chapter 4 directives map the work-permit refusal framework onto arts. 18-24 AIG and the corresponding ordinance provisions. 26

For refusal and file-rebuild work, the most operationally relevant statutory hooks are: employer-led application and work-authorisation basics; overall economic interest; quotas; labour-market priority and vacancy notification; salary and working conditions; personal qualifications; housing where relevant; the consent procedure between canton and SEM; and the rule that profession-specific regulatory permissions do not replace immigration permission. Those hooks are repeatedly applied in SEM's own directives and in the public cantonal practice pages. 27

The refusal-and remedy-side procedural framework is supplied by the consent procedure under AIG art. 99 and VZAE arts. 83, 85 and 86, together with the federal administrative appeal framework applied by SEM and the Federal Administrative Court. SEM's directives set out the operative route more clearly than any single public statute page accessible in this session. 28

### E. Official guidance and administrative materials

SEM's public **"Basis for admission to the Swiss employment market"** page is the most concise official summary of refusal law for non-EU local hire. It confirms the role of overall economic interest, quotas, labour-market priority, salary and employment conditions, and qualification; it also links operationally to the directives and procedure pages. 29

SEM's **"Procedure"** and **"Application** **documents"** pages are central for refusal-risk analysis because they translate the law into file architecture. They specify who files, which authority acts first, when SEM approval is needed, and which minimum documents must be included for precedence, qualifications, reasons for the application, project material, and salary/conditions. For internal rebuilding work, these pages are often more useful than abstract legal commentary because they show what an authority expects to see in the dossier. 30

<!-- 8 -->

The SEM **AIG** **directives,** especially Chapter 4 and the remedies chapter, are the strongest official interpretive source reviewed. They explain genuine labour-market search, non-artificial job criteria, statistical wage assessmnent, shortage-occupation flexibility, the federal consent procedure, appeal routes, and reconsideration doctrine. On the SECO side, **Arbeit.swiss** provides the current vacancy-notification regime and annual occupation lists, while SECO also publishes the labour-force indicator system referenced by the directives for shortage analysis. 31

## Cantonal practice and case law

### F. Cantonal practice notes

Zurich's official guidance is a useful German-language practice note because it compresses the federal tests into a practical filing standard. It states that the employer must apply, that the criteria are cumulative, and that the employer must show overall economic interest, qualification, labour-market priority and compliance with local wage and working conditions. Zurich's downloadable guidance also identifies concrete evidence for local-hire cases: RAV and EURES posting evidence, dated advertisements, and a detailed applicant list including nationality, qualifications, application date, and rejection reason. 32

Geneva's official French-language page is especially helpful on refusal-risk framing because it is unusually explicit. It says the employer must file roughly six to eight weeks before entry; that the Geneva employer must notify the vacancy to the cantonal employment office as quickly as possible, regardless of the unemployment rate in the branch; that wider searches in Switzerland and the EU/AELE area must be documented; that the employer must show the role could not reasonably be filled by training someone already available on the Swiss labour market; and that only managers, specialists and qualified workers are admissible. It also says that quotas must be available, that customary salary and conditions must be respected, and, as Geneva practice, that the post is full-time. 33

Cantonal pages are useful but must be classified correctly in internal drafting. They are **practice** **notes,** not standalone sources of federal law, and some elements are canton-specific rather than universal. Geneva's insistence on OCE notification regardless of the branch threshold and its full-time formulation are best treated as Geneva practice indicators, not as bare federal rules. 34

### G. Case law notes, if relevant

**BVGer** **C-2638/2010,** 21 **March** **2011.** This is a strong authority for labour-market-test refusals and for over-tailored role criteria. The court records the authority's view that serious search efforts were lacking, that the existing recruitment was insufficient in time and geographic scope, and that the language profile risked excluding the priority labour pool. The court ultimately upheld refusal on the labour-market-precedence issue and did not need to decide qualificationconclusively. It is therefore most useful as authority on art. 21 priority analysis rather than on current healthcare qualification policy. 35

**BVGer** **C-2216/2010,** 12 **August 2010.** This case is useful for two propositions. First, even after a cantonal authority re-opened the matter and indicated willingness to grant, SEM could still refuse consent. Secondly, the decision shows how thin search evidence and doubtful specialist-fit arguments are treated as substantive refusal reasons. It is particularly relevant where advisers are tempted to over-read the value of a later cantonal change of approach. 36

<!-- 9 -->

**BVGer** **C-5496/2010** **etc., 2011.** This is the clearest authority reviewed for the distinction between procedural fault and evidential weakness. The court held that a non-entry decision was not the right response where the case could in fact be decided on the available record. At the same time, it accepted that prior non-compliance with approved salary and working conditions, incomplete production of records, and unclear payroll practice could justify adverse inferences and support a negative merits decision. It also held that separate statement of statutory social-insurance deductions in the contract was not itself a legal requirement. 25

**BVGer** **F-4755/2018.** Although this case concerned self-employment, it is still useful for general architecture: SEM refusals of cantonal labour-market pre-decisions are appealable to the Federal Administrative Court; the court applies current facts and law at decision time; and SEM exercises original federal decision-making power rather than merely reviewing the canton. Those propositions are directly transferable to local-hire consent refusals. 37

## Practical legal analysis

## H.Practical legal analysis

The strongest internal working distinction is this**: not every refusal reason belongs in an appeal pack.** In Swiss non-EU local-hire cases, many refusal themes are really failures of proof against a cumulative discretionary test. If the file does not convincingly prove genuine labour-market search, qualification, local salary compliance, economic interest and a coherent business need, the safer route is often to rebuild the dossier rather than to argue that the authority committed a legal error merely by refusing it. SEM's completeness rules, labour-market guidance and reconsideration doctrine all point in this direction. 38

On **labour-market-test deficiencies,** the filemust show more than adverts. Official materials expect a chronological record: vacancy notification where applicable, dated advertisements, use of appropriate channels, proof of recruiter involvement where used, an applicant list, reasons for rejection, and a credible explanation of why training or re-training someone already available was not realistic within a reasonable period. Zurich and Geneva both push the employer toward a concrete evidence set, and SEM's application-documents page does the same nationally. A refusal for failing this standard is usually a weak-file problem unless the authority ignored or misstated evidence already filed. 39

**On** **over-tailored** **or** **artificial job** **descriptions,** SEM's directives are unusually explicit. Criteria cannot be crafted so that priority workers are practically excluded through non-essential language demands, foreign stays, orloosely relevant specialisms. This is where file analysis should be particularly conservative: a requirement may be commercially attractive and stilI not be legally safe. The key rebuild question is whether each criterion can be tied to the actual day-to-day tasks and not merely to the preferred foreign candidate. C-2638/2010 is a useful cautionary example. 12

On **candidate** **suitability,** the federal starting point remains restrictive. SEM's public page says non-EU work is generally for highly qualified persons. The directives broaden that slightly for occupations with pronounced shortages, allowing some non-academic but still qualified profiles with special knowledge or indispensable skills. That means the rebuild file should never stop at a CV. It should show ddiploma equivalence where relevant, references, years of experience mapped to the advertised tasks,any shortage-occupation angle, and certified translations for documents not in an official Swiss language or English. Where the role sits in a regulated profession, recognition or a practice authorisation may be independently required. 40

<!-- 10 -->

**On** **salary** **and** **local conditions,** internal analysis should distinguish between the legal rule and the evidential method. The rule is compliance with local, occupational and sectoral norms. The evidential method, according to SEM's directives, can include collective agreements, the conditions paid for comparable work in the same undertaking and sector, and current statistical wages. Geneva explicitly points employers to a salary calculator; SEM points to Salarium and SECO's national wage calculator.A rebuild file should therefore contain the signed contract, working-hours detail, salary benchmark printouts, any CBA/NAV extract, and an explanation of variable pay or allowances. If the earlier refusal involved inconsistent real-world payroll practice, that history must be squarely addressed. 41

**On** **weak** **employer justification** **or** role **necessity,** the correct legal lens is overall economic interest. SEM's basis page says the applicant must explain and document why admission is in Switzerland's overall economic interest; the directives warn against preserving economic structures through low-skilled labour or supporting private convenience interests. Zurich's practice note points to specific justifications, business plans, orders, and financial material. For internal file rebuilding, that means the employer letter should not be generic. It should explain what the Swiss role is, why it must be filled now, why the function belongs in Switzerland, how the worker's skills map onto the need, and what commercial or operational consequences follow if the role is not filled. 42

**On** **quotas, timing and** **procedural sequence,** quota exhaustion is analytically different from an evidentially weak file. The Federal Council fixes annual numbers, and those limits remain independent admission barriers. Equally, sequence matters: the employer files first; the canton screens; positive cases go to SEM where required; and the worker does not gain a right to start simply because a job exists. In Geneva, the employer is told to file around six to eight weeks before entry. Where a refusal is really about quota timing or late filing against the procedural sequence, an appeal on the mnerits is often much weaker than a later, properly timed re-filing if and when the barrier changes. 43

**On** **incomplete** **or** **inconsistent documents,** there are two separate legal questions. First, is the file complete enough for the authorities to process? SEM says it will process only complete applications and lists minimum materials. Secondly, if additional material is missing, does that justify outright non-entry, or does it go to the merits? The 2011 BVGer line is useful here: where the case can be assessed on the record, non-entry is ordinarily not the preferred response; incomplete cooperation may instead support adverse inferences and a substantive refusal. This distinction matters for appeals. If the authority unlawfully chose non-entry, that is a procedural challenge point. If the file simply did not prove its case, the real remedy is reconstruction. 44

**On** **appeal, reconsideration** **and** **re-filing,** the routes should be kept sharply separate. An **appeal** challenges a decision as unlawful or procedurally defective. A **reconsideration** **request** goes back to the authority that issued the original decision and is exceptional; SEM says it is not an ordinary remedy, has no fixed form or deadline, and is not there to heal missed cooperation or to evade time limits. A **re-filing** is a fresh application, but the official sources reviewed do not set out a dedicated, route-specific re-filing code for ordinary nnon-EU work-permit refusals. The sound internal approach is therefore to re-file only where the file is materially improved or where circumstances have genuinely changed, such as a new quota period, a revised role, better recruitment proof, or newly obtained recognition/licensing. That conclusion is an inference, but it is a strong one. 45

**For timing** **after** **refusal,** three conservative propositions emerge. First, SEM appeals are usually subject to a 30-day deadline. Secondly, canton-level refusals go through canton-specific remedy routes stated in the decision itself, not through a single federal template. Thirdly,no official federal source

reviewed sets a universal waiting period before an ordinary local-hire re-filing. Internal drafting should therefore avoid stating any blanket period. The better formula is that timing depends on the appeal deadline, the remedy notice, quota availability, and whether the new filing genuinely differs from the refused one. 46

**A** **rebuild-ready** **evidence** **pack** for a refused local-hire case should normally contain, at minimum, the following: a recruitment chronology with RAV/EURES evidence where relevant; dated adverts and channel screenshots; an applicant assessment grid with legally relevant rejection reasons; recruiter confirmations if used; a role-necessity statement from the employer; the signed contract and salary benchmark material; CVs, qualifications, references and translations; any profession-recognition or practice authorisation evidence; and a consistency check across forms, job description, contract and business explanation. That checklist is not found in a single official page, but it is directly derived from SEM's national minimum-document list, Zurich and Geneva practice materials, and the case law on wage, recruitment and documentary consistency. 47

## Drafting cautions and source list

### I. Drafting cautions

·Do not say or imply that a non-EU local-hire refusal is usually easy to overturn. Swiss official materials present this as a discretionary, cumulative admission regime, and Supreme Court review is generally unavailable where there is no legal entitlement. 48

·Do not collapse **appeal, reconsideration** **and** **re-filing** into one generic "challenge" concept. The first attacks legality, the second is an extraordinary same-authority remedy, and the third depends on materially improved evidence or changed circumstances. 49

·Do not present weak recruitment evidence, generic employer justification, missing qualifications, or thin salary proof as if they were inherently appellate points. In many cases they are weak-file problems that require a rebuilt dossier. 50

·Do not generalise cantonal practice as though it were a universal federal rule. Geneva's filing timing, OCE-notification approach and full-time wording, for example, should be marked as practice notes, not unqualified federal law statements. 34

·Do not suggest that reconsideration is a safe way to "add the missing documents later". SEM's own guidance says reconsideration is not for curing missed cooperation or bypassing appeal deadlines. 21

·Do not use optimistic phrases such as "a job offer is enough", "the refusal can simply be fixed",or "the permit should follow once the canton agrees". SEM's public FAQ and directives contradict all three propositions. 51
