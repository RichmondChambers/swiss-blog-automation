# Swiss quota, timing and filing strategy pack for non-EU local hire work permits

**Internal destination:** knowledge/legalauthorities/workpermits/routespecific/ noneulocalhire/

**Suggested filename:**

## Executive legal summary

In Swiss non-EU local hire cases, quota availability is only one part of the admission analysis. A third-country national who wishes to work in Switzerland requires a permit regardless of the length of stay, and for salaried work the employer must file the application. Admission for salaried employment requires an employer application, overall economic interest, and satisfaction of the labour-market and personal criteria in the FNIA, including the quota rule in Article 20 and the precedence rule in Article 21. The SEM's own guidance summarises the same structure: admission criteria are set by the FNIA and the implementing ordinance, quotas are annual, and employers must still prove a failed Swiss/EU-EFTA recruitment search and satisfy the other admission criteria. ①

For ordinary third-country workers in 2026, the Federal Council left the quota ceiling unchanged at 8,500 units, consisting of 4,500 B residence permits and 4,000 L short-stay permits. SEM's 2026 circular states that 1,250 B units and 2,000 L units are allocated to the cantons, while 3,250 B units and 2,000 L units remain in the federal reserve; unused 2025 units are added to the 2026 federal reserve. The same materials show an annual quota period running from 1 January to 31 December and confirm that cantons may request additional units from the federal reserve where they have justified need. 2

The operative filing sequence is legally and practically important. In quota-sensitive local hire cases, the employer files with the competent cantonal labour-market or migration authority; the canton screens the application and issues the labour-market preliminary decision; approved cases are then submitted to SEM for final approval; and, if the individual is abroad and requires a visa, the cantonal migration authority sends the electronic visa authorisation to the Swiss representation abroad only after SEM approval. The official SEM procedure page states expressly that SEM's official decision does **not** yet permit entry, and that the employee may begin work only after registering in Switzerland following entry. Zurich's official guidance mirrors the same sequence and expressly notes that a positive labour-market decision can still be followed by a refusal on immigration-law grounds. 3

The official materialIs reviewed do **not** support any general proposition that urgent commercial need can bypass the normal seqquence. What they do support is narrower. First, there is a non-contingent route for work of up to four months within a twelve-month period, but it still requires prior authorisation, and visa nationals still need a visa; if the stay is a single stay of three to four months, SEM guidance says the person receives a visa D. Secondly, where cantonal quota units are insufficient, SEM's 2026 circular asks cantons to notify additional demand early and to explain, for example, impending company establishments, transfers of business units to Switzerland, or planned projects requiring qualified foreign personnel. None of those sources says that urgency dispenses with substantive

eligibility, SEM approval, visa issuance where applicable, or the prohibition on starting work before the final administrative sequence is completed. 4

## Structural overview of quotas, timing and filing sequence

At the statutory level, the FNIA permits the Federal Council to limit the number of **first-time** short-stay and residence permits for work purposes. Those are the L and B categories: the short-stay permit is for limited stays of up to one year, and the residence permit is for stays of more than one year. The same Act leaves quota competence and the approval procedure to the Confederation even though the cantons issue the permits themselves. 5

At the substantive eligibility level, quota units do not replace the ordinary admission tests. The employer must still show overall economic interest, an unsuccessful search in the Swiss and EU/EFTA labour markets, compliance with local salary and employment conditions, and that the candidate fits the personnal qualification rules for third-country admission. Geneva's official page is particularly useful here because it lists quota availability as only one condition among several, rather than the decisive one. Zurich likewise states that a positive labour-market decision may still be followed by an immigration-law refusal. 6

At the allocation level, the reviewed official materials describe an annual system rather than a rolling one. SEM's 2026 circular allocates part of the ordinary third-country quotapool to cantons and leaves the balance in the federal reserve. SEM's 2026directives explain that quota management is tied to the quota period, that cantons may apply for federal units when their own units are insufficient,and that the Confederation prioritises special needs such as important company establishments, major national projects, research, intra-group transfers with major know-how transfer, reciprocity considerations,and international institutions. 7

By contrast, official 2026 materials expressly describe **quarterly** **release** for two other quota pools: the separate temporary UK quota and the EU/EFTA service-provider quotas for stays above the relevant day thresholds. SEM's January 2026 migration statistics also publish remaining balances differently for those categories. For the ordinary third-country local-hire quota pool, the reviewed 2026 materials instead describe annual cantonal allocations plus a federal reserve and monthly remaining balances; I did not find an official statement that those ordinary third-country L/B units are released quarterly in 2026. That point should therefore be drafted as an inference from the official allocation materials, not as an express rule. 8

The filing sequence for a standard overseas local hire should therefore be understood as follows: recruitment evidence and conditional contract drafting; cantonal filing by the employer; cantonal labour-market screening; SEM approval in principle for positive third-country cases; cantonal migration follow-through; visa issuance for visa nationals; entry; communal registration; then work commencement. SEM's official process page, the general SEM "Working in Switzerland" page, and Zurich's canton page all support the final step that work begins only after the entry and registration stage, not merely after filing or cantonal support. 9

A narrower, time-sensitive exception exists for stays of up to four months within a twelve-month period. SEM's directives describe this as a non-contingent route; Zurich's guidance says those stays are not quota-limited and use an entry authorisation rather than a residence card; and SEM's procedure page still requires cantonal screening, SEM final approval, visa application abroad for visa nationals, and registration-free work start only within the authorised short-stay framework. This is therefore a genuine planning tool for short urgent assignments, but not a general shortcut around the Swiss work-permit system. 10

### Federal legal texts and official administrative materials

**Primary** **legal texts.** The core federal statutory provisions are in the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act. Article 11 establishes the permit requirement for any gainful employment and places the filing

<!-- 6 -->

obligation on the employer in salaried cases. Articles 18 and 21 set the basic local-hire admission logic: overall economic interest, employer application, and precedence for Swiss and free-movement labour. Article 20 creates the quota power for first-time L and B work permits. Articles 32 and 33 define the relevant permit categories, Article 40 allocates cantonal and federal roles, Article 17 deals wvith waiting abroad in change-of-purpose cases, and Article 99 establishes SEM's approval procedure. 29

For the implementing framework, SEM's 2026 chapter-4 directives are the most accessible official administrative authority in the reviewed materials. They treat quota questions as arising under Article 20 FNIA together with Articles 19-21 OASA, explain quota-period operation and federal-reserve requests, set out the non-contingent four-month work route, distinguish repeat short-stay "renewal" from mere extension, and explain the approval system by reference to Article 85 OASA and Article 1 of the DFJP approval ordinance. 30

**Official guidance and administrative materials.** SEM's "Basis for admission" page is the clearest federal summary of the relationship between quotas and the other admission criteria, including precedence, job-registration obligations, salary conditions, and the recommendation to use a conditional employment contract. SEM's official "Procedure" page sets out the operative order for sub-four-month cases: cantonal filing, cantonal screening, SEM final approval, visa authorisation, entry, registration, and only then work commencement. SEM's general "Working in Switzerland" page confirms that third-country nationals may begin work only after registration in Switzerland after entry. 31

For current quota numbers, the controlling current-year materials are the Federal Council's 19November 2025 announcement and SEM's circular of the same date on the 2026 quota period. Those materials provide the annual ceilings, the current cantonal/federal split, the continued separate UK quota, the quarterly release rule for UK and EU/EFTA service-provider quotas, and SEM's request for early notification where a canton will need additional reserve units for justified projects or relocations. SEM's January 2026 immigration statistics then show how the authorities monitor balances in practice during the year. FDFA consular guidance published by Swiss representations confirms that the entry visa is granted only after the required authorisations have been delivered and that the work-visa application is built around the cantonal authorisation / visa-D sequence. 32

## Cantonal practice notes

**Zurich.** Zurich's official pages are especially useful for sequence and categorisation. They state that third-country nationals need a poermit from the first day, that the canton issues the labour-market preliminary decision and forwards positive cases to SEM for approval, and that, where the person is abroad, the cantonal migration office then authorises visa issuance or gives the employer an assurance of permit where no visa is needed. Zurich also states expressly that a permnit can still be refused on immigration-law grounds even after a positive labour-market decision. It further confirms that stays of less than four months are not contingent, proceed by entry authorisation, and still require a visa for visa nationals. 33

**Geneva.** Geneva's official guidance states that a Swiss employer wishing to hire a third-country national must in principle apply to the OCPM, that the employer should file about six to eight weeks before the person's entry into Switzerland, and that the OCIRT labour-market directorate screens the application first. Geneva is also useful because it lists quota availability for B and L permits as only one condition among several, alongside economic interest, precedence, salary compliance, and qualifications. 24

**Ticino.** Ticino's official third-country L-permit page for persons abroad states that the application must be made by the employer and that the foreign national waits abroad for entry authorisation. Its L-

<!-- 7 -->

renewal/modification page states that the foreign national must request renewal 14 days before expiry, notify relevant changes within 14 days, and notify the start of remunerated activity no later than the day before it begins. These are cantonal compliance rules rather than federal quota rules, but they are valuable operational timing authorities. 34

**Zug.** Zug's official page states that anyone from a third state who wishes to be admitted for work needs a permit from the first day and that there is no legal entitlement to a particular permit or to its extension. That is useful as a succinct cantonal statement against overly confident promise language in public-facing content. 35

## Practical legal analysis

The first drafting discipline is to keep **quota** **availability** separate from **legal eligibility.** The official authorities consistently treat quotas as a limiting mechanism layered on top of substantive requirements, not as a stand-alone gateway. A blog or internal content item should therefore never suggest that "if quota units are available, the permit should issue". The legally safer formulation is that a quota-sensitive first-time L/B case requires both a free quota unit **and** full satisfaction of the ordinary admission criteria, followed by approval in the correct federal-cantonal sequence. 6

The second discipline is to respect the **actual** filing **sequence.** In official SEM guidance, the employer files first at cantonal level, the canton screens and makes the labour-market preliminary decision, SEM handles the approval layer, and only then can the entry/visa layer be completed. This matters because the same case may fail at more than one stage: a cantonal labour-market authority may refuse, SEM may refuse approval, or the cantonal migration authority may still refuse at the imnmigration-law stage even after a positive labour-market assessment. Content should therefore distinguish clearly between filing, cantonal support, SEM approval, visa issuance, entry, residence registration, and work commencement. 36

The third discipline is to treat **recruitment** **timing** as a legal, not merely administrative, matter. SEM's guidance says employers must prove genuine efforts to recruit in Switzerland and the EU/EFTA area and even states that contact with third-country nationals should only begin after that search has proved unsuccessful. Combined with the separate job-registration obligations in higher-unemploymnent occupations, this means that local-hire planning should begin earlier than the permit filing date itself. In practice terms,the legally safest sequence is: compliant vacancy strategy and evidence collection first; only then conditional contracting and permit filing. 23

The fourth discipline is to treat **start** **dates** conservatively. Swissofficial sources are aligned on the point that the employee may not begin work merely because a filing has been made, nor merely because a favourable labour-market view exists. SEM's procedure page says the official SEM decision does not by itself allow entry and that the employee can only begin work after registration; the general SEM overview says that only after registration may the third-country national take up employment; and FDFA guidance says the entry visa is granted only after the required authorisations are delivered. The contract date, mobilisation plan, relocation steps, and payroll onboarding should therefore be built around approval, visa and registration milestones, not around the initial filing date. 37

The fifth discipline is to understand what **urgent starts** can and cannot do. The reviewed official materials do not reveal a general federal fast-track regime for urgent commercial cases. The lawful planning levers visible in the sources are narrower: the non-contingent up-to-four-month route; early and well-evidenced reserve requests where cantonal units are insufficient; and careful sequencing of entry / visa steps. Even the short-stay non-contingent route is not a bypass: the SEM procedure page still requires cantonal screening and SEM approval, the directives still require prior authorisation, and visa nationals still need a visa. The correct presentation is therefore that urgency may affect filing strategy and reserve requests, but not the underlying eligibility or sequencing rules. 38

<!-- 8 -->

The sixth discipline is to state **quota** **exhaustion** carefully. Official 2026 materials clearly state what happens first: a canton facing insufficiency may request additional federal reserve units, and SEM wants early notice and project-level justification. The sources do not contain a simple published sentence saying "if all quotas are exhausted, the case must wait until next year"; however, that is the conservative legal inference for a quota-sensitive first-time L/B case once neither cantonal units nor federal reserve units are available and no non-quota exception applies. That inference should be labelled as such in internal content. It should also be kept separate from the other quota pools: UK and EU/EFTA service-provider quotas are expressly quarterly released in 2026, whereas the reviewed materials do not expressly characterise the ordinary third-country worker pooI that way. 39

The seventh discipline is to distinguish **extension** **from** **renewal** where renewals are discussed. Article 20 FNIA is limited to first-time permits. SEM's directives then add an important nuance for short-stay permits: a "renewal" after interruption is treated as a new stay and is quota-charged each time, whereas a mere consecutive extension is conceptually different. Internal content should therefore avoid broad statements such as "renewals are not quota-sensitive" or "all renewaIs use new quota units". The legally safer wording is narrower and source-based: straightforward consecutive extensions are not the same thing as repeat short-stay renewals after a break, and the latter are expressly treated as chargeable to quota each time in SEM's directives. 26

## Drafting cautions

Do **not** write that quota availability equals sponsorship success. The official sources support only the weaker proposition that quota availability is one condition within a broader multi-stage admission and approval system. 18

Do **not** collapse the stages into a single event called "work permit approval". For legally accurate public content, keep separate: filing; cantonal labour-market screening; SEM approval; cantonal migration follow-through; visa issuance where required; entry; registration; and only then work commencement. 20

Do **not** say that a visa is a mere formality after permit approval. FDFA guidance states that the entry visa is granted only once the required authorisations have been delivered, and SEM states that its decision does not itself yetauthorise entry. 40

Do **not** say that ordinary third-country local-hire quotas are "released quarterly" unless the content is expressly about the separate UK or EU/EFTA service-provider pools. For the ordinary third-country pool, the reviewed official 2026 materials describe annual numbers, cantonal allocations, a federal reserve, and monthly remaining balances, but not an express quarterly release rule. 27

Do **not** convert cantonal lead-time guidance into Swiss-wide promises. Geneva's "about 6-8 weeks" is a canton-specific planning indication, not a federal guarantee and not a prediction of end-to-end timing in every case. 24

Do **not** ignore the short-assignment route, but describe it precisely. The lawful point is not "stays under four mnonths are easy"; it is that work of up to four months within a twelve-month period may be non-

<!-- 9 -->

contingent while still requiring prior authorisation, SEM approval, and a visa where the worker is visa-required. 41

Do **not** present the employment contract as unconditional where content is explaining filing practice. SEM guidance expressly recommends a contract signed by both parties but made conditional on permit grant. 23
